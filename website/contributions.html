    Wrestling With Eels

    Scientific self-experimentation (running your life like a lab, isolating variables, tracking datapoints) fails structurally in a transitory age where the baseline never holds still and the observer keeps rewiring himself. Reading his own failed “experimental phase” through the Divergence Machine frame, Florian recovers Alexander von Humboldt’s idiosyncratic field studies (embodied, embedded, comfortable with gaps) as the seed of an “epistemology of divergence.” The closer turns on a distinction: failing backwards casts a good idea into a dead diorama; failing forwards keeps eyes open and marks the gaps.
