# Stigmergic-Verbose Cycle

Civilization-scale culture seems to cycle between two modes:

**Verbose mode:** Highly conversational, fragmentary, aimed at creating highly integrated gestalt self-understandings at group and higher levels. People engage with each other's ideas in real time, building shared frameworks through dialogue.

**Stigmergic mode:** Individuals develop and put out individualist worldviews in relatively polished, finished forms — books, essays — and try to create integrated understandings in relative isolation by absorbing other people's similar outputs rather than engaging with them in conversation.

These two modes are adapted to different modes of governance of world machines. Stigmergic modes are relatively divergent; verbose modes are relatively convergent.

## Examples

**Verbose:** The Romantic era of the Jena Circle in late 18th/early 19th century Germany. The blog/Twitter era of the 2010s.

**Stigmergic:** The Substack era of online writing.

## Notes

The cycle is not simply about technology, since the Jena Circle predates mass media. The determining factor seems to be the phase of the dominant world machine and the governance needs of the moment — convergence is called for when a new synthesis needs to be built collectively; divergence when individuals need to develop positions before they can productively converge.

The current Substack era may be a stigmergic phase preceding a verbose phase — the positions are accumulating, and the next mode may be one of intensive cross-pollination and synthesis.
