# World Machines become more visible as they decay

## Claim

> World machines become visible precisely as they decay — you only see the plumbing when it breaks, and visibility itself may be a sign of decline.

## Body

Evidence: 
1) the US “Underground Empire” (Farrell/Newman) was powerful because hidden — exposure and overreach triggered decline
2) the Soviet system (Yurchak) was simultaneously eternal and stagnating, its collapse “completely unexpected and completely unsurprising”
3) the modernity machine’s six relationships are most legible now, in their end state. 

Connects to [[Canonic infrastructure decays in hilarious drainage]] — implies a stage where structure becomes *more* visible as it loses function, like scaffolding noticed only when the building starts to crumble. This visibility can also lead to [[The afterlife of canonical structures is culturally richer than their main act|a richer cultural afterlife of decaying structures]].

## What would weaken this / change my mind
- some structures may be more visible during its peak performance, not when they break apart. But this would require that people don’t take them for granted while they work
