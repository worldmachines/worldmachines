# Divergence grows in peripheral fields, not in the fortified center

## Claim

> Divergence happens in the landscape outside the walls: modernity = fortress (canonical space, inside the walls), postmodernity = siege (contesting the fortress, still defined by it), divergence = the always-present landscape beyond, invisible while attention stays on the fortifications.

## Body

Supplies the spatial imagery the framework lacks (Gap 3): Rao describes divergence conceptually but offers no spatial picture. 

The Kent Leap — Walpole on William Kent, “He leaped the fence, and saw that all nature was a garden” — is the candidate image: Kent jumps the ha-ha (the last invisible moat) and finds the landscape identical on both sides; the controlled/uncontrolled distinction stops mattering. 

Connected to Clément’s “Third Landscape” (52 species on unmanaged land vs. 5 in managed fields). 

Builds on the ha-ha = invisible-moat stage of [[Canonic infrastructure decays in hilarious drainage]].

Closely related: [[The margin provides sharper insight on historic infrastructure than the canonical center]]

Other examples:
- language proliferation happens at the linguistic borders, where non-native speakers push language forward, while native speakers in the linguistic centers try to maintain the status quo
- Economic innovation often happens in the periphery, the border zones between economic centers
- Scientific paradigms are usually challenged from the fringes of established fields, while the apparatus at the academic center fight back

## What would weaken this / change my mind

- significant proofs of divergence to be found in the center, more than in the periphery
