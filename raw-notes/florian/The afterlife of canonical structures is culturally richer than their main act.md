# The afterlife of canonical structures is culturally richer than their main act
## Claim

> Defunct canonical structures produce more culturally generative material than functional ones — the ridiculed and repurposed stages of the decay sequence are more generative than the functional stage.

## Body

Split out from [[Canonic infrastructure decays in hilarious drainage]] capture because it is separately falsifiable: the sequence could run exactly as described while cultural generativity stayed flat or front-loaded. 

Evidence: 
1) Pangloss is more famous than monad theory
2) the ha-ha reshaped garden design
3) Bodiam became “Castle Swamp” in Monty Python
4) Tower of London moat → parade ground → allotments → poppy memorial


## What would weaken this / change my mind

- canonic ruins that do NOT have an interesting cultural afterlife
- canonically alive structures having a more vivid cultural relevance than canonic ruins
