# The margins provides sharper insight on historic infrastructure than the canonical center
## Claim

> The historical record that matters most is often produced by observers considered irrelevant in their own time — the future is already around, in “dull notes in the margins” that later become primary sources.

## Body

Supports the horizontal-history method (reading geographically wide within a narrow time band) and the machinic perspective (centering overlooked components). 

Evidence: 
1) Majapahit’s primary historical source is a court official whose observations were dismissed when published but are now the only detailed record of the empire. 
2) Implicit second move: WMP contributors are themselves writing “notes in the margins” of the current transition.

Connects to method in general, not to the [[Frameworks can turn performative as they become more sophisticated]] group.

## What would weaken this / change my mind
- Survivorship Bias: we only see observers from the margins whose records survived - how good is the success rate vs. observers from the center?
- In general systematically more generative observations from canonical centers than from the margins
