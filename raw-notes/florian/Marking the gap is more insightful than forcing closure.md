# Marking the gap is more insightful than forcing closure

## Claim
> When a theory can't reach closure, marking the gap honestly — registering what is not known, including by shifting registers — is a more productive epistemic move than forcing artificial closure. It points at what might come later instead of faking completeness. (Humboldt inserting the Lebenskraft poem and letting it "stand for the gap.")
