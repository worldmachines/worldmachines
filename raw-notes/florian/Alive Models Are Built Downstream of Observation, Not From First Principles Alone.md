# Alive Models Are Built Downstream of Observation, Not From First Principles Alone

## Claim
> Mental models are put together quickly, but they often turn out to be dead infrastructure the moment they are shared. To build models which can really interact with reality, you need to tether them to living data drawn from observation, not context-free first principles.


## Body

- Model is downstream of the anomaly, not upstream of it. VGR’s Gervais Principle as exemplar: the 3-tier model emerged as the minimal machinery explaining a pattern that felt structurally true — not deduced from "organizations are meritocracies."


Relations:
- Humboldt: [[Divergent self knowledge requires embedded, embodied, non-reproducible inquiry]]
- Complements [[Every Model Has an Activation Regime]] — a model built from one anomaly can be misapplied outside its regime.
- Live tension with [[Frameworks can turn performative as they become more sophisticated]]: "justification is solely that it works" is one step from the deferral structure you distrust. Branch, don't reconcile.

## What would weaken this / change my mind
- purely first principles derived models that are not Muskian weed chatter, but actually useful filters for reality - seems less likely in divergent conditions, where axiomatic truth is limited to smaller domains than in modernity conditions
