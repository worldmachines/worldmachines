# The lab-science toolkit of self-knowledge fails once its preconditions withdraw

## Claim
> Lab science of the self requires three conditions — a baseline that holds still, variables that isolate, and an observer who is not also the system observed. Under divergence these withdraw: the baseline drifts faster than the experiment runs, the variables loop, the observer rewires while running. The toolkit doesn't fit the divergent self — not because it was never valid, but because its configuration is passing.

## Body
Self-level instance of [[Canonic infrastructure decays in hilarious drainage]]. Precondition: [[A transitory state is the honest default of any historical moment]].
Adjacent: [[Frameworks can turn performative as they become more sophisticated]].

## What would weaken this / change my mind
- The Seth Roberts case: disciplined N=1 worked for over a decade. The claim survives only as "the configuration it required is passing," NOT "it never worked." If someone in my position today runs it well, the claim breaks.
