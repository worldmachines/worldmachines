# It takes connoisseurship for ornamental baggage to identify dead frameworks
## Claim

> You can assess the aliveness or deadness of a framework by examining it for functional flaws — the more decorative, non-functional features you find, the more likely you face a dead facade than a working fortification.

## Body

A practical instrument for applying Rao’s four-stage filter: to tell a late-modern zombie or postmodern ghost from genuine divergence, check the moats — the more functional flaws, the more likely zombie or ghost. 

Historically grounded in the “licence to crenellate” (550 granted in England 1194–1589, mostly token battlements on manor houses; Coulson’s “passports to respectability”).

Operationalizes [[Canonic infrastructure decays in hilarious drainage]].

## What would weaken this / change my mind
- structures that are both *alive* (not zombified) AND purely decorative. I did not see this so far.
