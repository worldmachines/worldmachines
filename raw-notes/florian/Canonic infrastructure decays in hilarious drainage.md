# Canonic infrastructure decays in hilarious drainage
## Claim

> Convergent canonicity does not collapse through revolution or paradigm shift; it dissolves through a long draining — a decay sequence (Functional → Performative → Explicitly Allegorical → Ornamental → Invisible → Absent) that runs identically across domains.

## Body

Describes the *mechanism* of the Dusk Machine Conversion. 

Cross-domain evidence: 
- physical moats (Kenilworth functional → Plessis-Bourré performative → Chambord allegorical → Dampierre ornamental → the ha-ha invisible → Tower of London absent)
- Leibniz’s monads (functional defense → ridiculed by Voltaire → repurposed by Deleuze)
- AI competitive moats (compute/data barriers → “We Have No Moat” → moat-as-lifestyle-brand)

Hub probe: [[It takes connoisseurship for ornamental baggage to identify dead frameworks]], [[World Machines become more visible as they decay]], and [[Divergence grows in peripheral fields, not in the fortified center]] all draw on it; in live tension with [[Frameworks can turn performative as they become more sophisticated]].

## What would weaken this / change my mind

1) Modernity institutions decaying not in this re-iterative way, but disappearing abruptly
2) Modernity institutions not decaying at all 
3) Modernity institutions not decaying in a universal pattern, but domain specific ways
