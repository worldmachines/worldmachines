# Jan 10, 2025 - AI Agents, Machiavelli's Study

**Source**: Aneesh Sathe, *Jan 10, 2025 - AI Agents, Machiavelli's Study* (2025-01-10)
**URL**: https://aneeshsathe.com/jan-10-2025-ai-agents-machiavellis-study/

## Section 1: Agents Are Not Enough

**Paper**: Chirag Shah & Ryen White (December 2024) — "Agents Are Not Enough"

The paper opens by casting a deliberately wide definition of "agent": anything from a thermostat to an LLM tool. The authors' key claim: agents by definition *remove agency from a user* to act on their behalf. This reframing introduces a [[Principal-Agent Problem]] into AI agent discourse — the agent's interests may not perfectly represent the user's.

### The "Sims" Proposal

Shah & White propose expanding agent ecosystems with **Sims**: simulations of the user that address:
- Privacy and security
- Automated interactions
- Representing the user's interests through intimate knowledge of the user

A Sim is not just a task-executing agent but a *user-representing* companion. The proposal acknowledges that value generation, personalization, and trust are absent from current agent architectures.

**Author's framing**: This connects to earlier work on Knowledge Graphs — LLMs lack knowledge (not just capability), and Sims would provide the personalized knowledge substrate that generic agents lack.

## Section 2: Machiavelli and the Emergence of the Private Study

A parallel meditation on the [[Studiolo]] — the Renaissance private study — as the original intellectual sanctuary.

**Machiavelli's practice** (from his letters): each evening he would put on court garments and enter his books, conversing with the ancients for four hours:

> "I step inside the venerable courts of the ancients, where, solicitously received by them, I nourish myself on that food that alone is mine and for which I was born, where I am unashamed to converse with them and to question them about the motives for their actions, and they, in their humanity, answer me."

The studiolo (*studiolo* in Italian) was a *pharmakon* — simultaneously cure and poison for the soul. At its highest it was a sanctuary for self-cultivation; at its worst, a retreat from engagement.

**Montaigne's parallel concept**: the *arrière-boutique* — "a back room all our own" reserved for real liberty and solitude. Happiness must not depend entirely on external goods (wife, children, health).

**Virginia Woolf's inversion**: what humanists celebrated as voluntary retreat, Woolf identified as unavailable to women — "a room of one's own" as structural inequality, not personal choice.

### The Studiolo's Three Mediations

> "The study thus mediates the world, the word, and the self."

Three functions:
1. **World → word**: translating external reality through reading and writing
2. **Word → self**: using text to cultivate interiority
3. **Self → world**: the cultivated self re-engaging with the world

## Implied Connection (Not Drawn in Source)

The two sections share an underlying structure: both the Sim proposal and the studiolo are about creating a private representational space that holds intimate knowledge of a particular self. The Sim is a computational studiolo.

## What's Missing

- The connection between the two sections is implicit, not argued
- No engagement with the dangers of the studiolo as enclosure / withdrawal from collective life
- Woolf's point about structural inequality in access to private space is noted but not developed
