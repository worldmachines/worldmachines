# The World Machines Project

**Source:** "The World Machines Project" by Venkatesh Rao, Contraptions (Substack), Mar 28, 2026.

## Central Argument

The World Machines (WM) framework — originally developed to guide reading selection for the Contraptions Book Club — has become generative enough that a subset of contributors are now building a collaborative project around it. The goal is a collaborative book (or "suitably unholy LLMified monstrosity") written collectively, with minimum viable scaffolding and rough consensus.

The framework is described not as a theory of history but as "an opinionated cognitive coworking space" — sensemaking triage and shared conceptual language rather than a Grand Unified Theory (GUT).

## What Is a World Machine?

A World Machine is a long-lived civilizational configuration that:
- Takes ~400 years to **build**
- Operates stably for ~400 years
- Declines/collapses **relatively rapidly**

At any given time, three world machines operate in parallel: a growing one (**Dawn Machine**), a mature one (**Day Machine**), and a dying/dead one (**Dusk Machine**). Rao borrows the Dawn/Day/Dusk naming from the Cleons genetic dynasty in the *Foundation* TV show.

### The Canonical Table

| Era       | Dawn Machine | Day Machine | Dusk Machine |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| 1200–1600 | Modernity   | Medieval    | Axial        |
| 1600–2000 | Divergence  | Modernity   | Medieval     |
| 2000–2400 | Liveness    | Divergence  | Divergence   |

("Configurancy" is offered as a more accurate technical term than "machine" but Rao prefers "machine" for its evocativeness.)

## Key In Media Res Notes

1. **Dawn/Day Machine co-existence**: In the 1740s–90s, Hume and Smith were heretics within the Modernity Machine while also founding figures of the Divergence Machine — illustrating how two WMs overlap without either dominating. The "tell": both excluded religion from their intellectual work entirely.

2. **Not cyclic history**: The WM framework resembles Strauss-Howe but at civilization scale (~1000-year cycles). Rao explicitly distances it from Kondratriev, Perez, Turchin, Sorokin, Toynbee, Gibbon, Ibn Khaldun. Raw historical phenomenology first; don't force-fit.

3. **Reading protocol**: Contributors should maintain at least 30% overlap with the shared book club list to stay in consensus headspace.

4. **Liveness Machine**: The current Dawn Machine, associated with generative AI — "poised between a divergent non-living process and a self-organized critical living process. Shoggoth-like basically."

5. **Pull vs. Push**: The Modernity Machine was a *pull* machine (telos of Progress™, convergent). The Divergence Machine is a *push* machine (individual/small-scale forces, divergent — like rear-wheel drive with no steering).

6. **Cybernetics caveat**: Rao as BDFL is "mildly hostile" to cybernetics/system dynamics approaches (Maturana/Varela autopoiesis), adopting "disagree but commit" toward contributors who use those frames.

7. **Psychohistory framing**: WMP resembles Asimov's psychohistory but corrected for chaos theory — closer to long-range climate forecasting than deterministic prediction.

## Starter Questions

- Full inventory of WMs since the Neolithic Revolution
- Necessary/sufficient conditions for a WM (test case: Bronze Age tin trade)
- Was the 400-year time constant slower before? (Axial Age ~1600 years)
- Micro-to-macro fractal structure of WMs
- How are WMs understood from within their own time?
- Geographic and social loci diversity

## What's Missing

- The framework currently has no formal mechanism for falsification
- The "Liveness Machine" is entirely speculative — no historical anchor yet
- The pull/push distinction is evocative but underspecified mechanically
