# A Good Dictionary

**Source:** https://aneeshsathe.com/a-good-dictionary/  
**Author:** Aneesh Sathe  
**Date:** 2025-07-17

## Central Argument

Modern dictionaries have stripped language of its richness: "modern dictionaries have taken all the fun out and left all the crud in." The proper tool for a serious writer is the 1913 Webster's Unabridged — a dictionary as literary object, not just a lookup table. This continues the [[Divine Documentation]] thread: the dictionary is itself a form of documentation, and the choice of dictionary shapes what you can think.

## The Problem with Modern Dictionaries

- Modern dictionaries optimize for disambiguation, not discovery
- They are in-context tools (on phones, computers) but stripped of the richness that makes them useful for writing
- The old way is "the proper way to play with words"

## The 1913 Webster's as Ideal Tool

The 1913 Webster's Unabridged is the recommended dictionary. It is:
- Generative (opens options you didn't know you had)
- Literary (entries written with prose care)
- Available via GitHub ([WebsterParser](https://github.com/cmod/websters-1913)) and installable on macOS

The essay is also a rare how-to: it includes updated installation instructions for macOS, positioning it as a piece of practical documentation — meta-consistent with the previous essay.
