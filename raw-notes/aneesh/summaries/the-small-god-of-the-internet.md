# The Small God of the Internet

**Source**: Aneesh Sathe, *The Small God of the Internet* (2025-08-02) — https://aneeshsathe.com/the-small-god-of-the-internet/
**Author**: [[Aneesh Sathe]]

## Central Argument

RSS is personified as a "small god" — ArrEsEs — who presides over the River of Posts. The essay traces his lineage from the printing press, his rise as a first-class citizen of every major browser, his eclipse by platform-controlled feeds, and his quiet persistence among a small faithful. The argument is simultaneously nostalgic and normative: the open, pull-based reading of RSS is architecturally superior to the engagement-optimized, push-based scroll. The "Gloomwheel" (the infinite scroll) is explicitly contrasted with the measured abundance of a feed.

## Key Claims

1. **RSS has a genealogy** — descends from the printing press (Old Mother Press) via the telegram and broadside. The inheritance is text in tidy rows, dated entries, headlines that stand up straight.
2. **Browsers once embraced RSS** — Firefox Live Bookmarks, Internet Explorer's orange beacon, Opera's feed pew: RSS was a house number on the web's front door. Then platform accountants discovered "candles are unmonetizable."
3. **The Gloomwheel is a structural critique** — platforms build ornamental channels that "reflect only the palace" and power a thumb-crank that "insists there is more, and worse, if you'll just keep scrolling." No flour is made.
4. **RSS's virtues are architectural** — it respects silence, delivers in chronological order, remembers what you finished, inserts no "You Might Also Like" leaflet.
5. **Ritual fidelity matters** — exposing a feed link, maintaining blogrolls, sharing OPML files are small liturgical acts that sustain the god and the community.
6. **The congregation is small but alive** — "home pages with kettles on, personal sites that remember how to wave, gardeners who publish their lists of other gardeners."

## Key Figures / Images

- **ArrEsEs** — the RSS god, wears an orange halo with three ripples; the icon rendered as divine attribute
- **Old Mother Press** — the printing press goddess; RSS's great-grandmother
- **The Gloomwheel** — the infinite scroll; turns without making flour
- **The River of Posts** — the RSS feed as universal flowing infrastructure
- **OGRP** — unnamed successor readers after Google Reader's death (likely Feedly-era alternatives)
- **The temple shut its doors with a soft click** — Google Reader's 2013 shutdown

## What's Missing

- No engagement with ActivityPub/Fediverse as an alternative open-protocol lineage
- The essay treats RSS as purely a reading technology; doesn't address its role in podcasting (where it remains dominant)
- The "gardeners" metaphor points toward a whole philosophy of the personal web that is not fully developed here (cf. [[The Cloister Web]])
