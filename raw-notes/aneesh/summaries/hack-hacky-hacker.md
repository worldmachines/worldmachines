# Hack, Hacky, Hacker

**Source:** https://aneeshsathe.com/hack-hacky-hacker/  
**Author:** Aneesh Sathe  
**Date:** 2025-07-18

## Central Argument

The three words *hack*, *hacky*, and *hacker* describe a horseshoe: at both ends are difficult, narrowing postures (fraudulence and mastery), while the center — being *hacky* — is the optimal, playful, fox-like approach to problems. "Breadth, speed, and joy beat fakery and fixation every time."

## The Horseshoe Topology

| Position | Description | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Hack (fraud) | Pretending to mastery; constant desperation; no play | Narrowing, exhausting |
| Hacky | Imperfect shortcuts; playful problem-solving; lateral | Broad, joyful, effective |
| Hacker (master) | Actually achieving difficult things through mastery | Narrowing, deep |

- **The hack** (fraud): Takes tremendous effort *to pretend* to be great. Humans are good at spotting and discarding hacks; long-term survival requires fooling a large population. No play, constant desperation.
- **The hacker** (master): [[Richard Stallman]]'s definition — "Playfully doing something difficult, whether useful or not." The author endorses playfulness and difficulty but rejects the "or not" — hacking should serve at least a prank. Pure intrinsic hacking is "felling a tree in a forest when no one is around."
- **The hacky**: The [[Nakatomi Tunnel]] approach — try enough approaches, push buttons until alignment slips through. Effectiveness = solving real-world problems quickly while preserving playful momentum.

## The Fox and the Hedgehog

[[Isaiah Berlin]]'s essay [[The Hedgehog and the Fox]] maps directly:
- **Hedgehog**: great at one big thing (analogous to the Hacker/master)
- **Fox**: mediocre at many things, thrives on lateral moves and opportunistic shortcuts (analogous to hacky)

The author explicitly aligns with the Fox: breadth, lateral moves, opportunistic shortcuts.

## Jugaad Distinction

The essay explicitly distances *hacky* from [[Jugaad]]:
- Jugaad was framed as creative improvisation but is actually substandard work presented as acceptable
- Hacky is about routing *through* small issues with playful momentum, not degrading the output
- "Build good stuff, be hacky route through the small issues"
