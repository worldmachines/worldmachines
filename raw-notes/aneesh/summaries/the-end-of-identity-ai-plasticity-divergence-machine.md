# The End of Identity: AI, Plasticity, and the Divergence Machine

**Source**: [aneeshsathe.com, 2026-03-08](https://aneeshsathe.com/the-end-of-identity-ai-plasticity-and-the-divergence-machine/)
**Author**: [[Aneesh Sathe]]
**Context**: Written for the Contraptions club, which has been reading from Bruno and Montaigne to Spinoza, Smith, and Hume

## Central Argument

Philosophy since Spinoza has been systematically peeling back "fiat" layers — theological, narrative, ideological — imposed over raw reality. AI represents the final peel: it processes the raw log files of our know-how without caring about the grand narrative of who we are. The response cannot be flexibility (adapting within a fixed system) but **plasticity** — actively giving and taking form without relying on any centralized system to define what those forms mean. Drawing on [[Catherine Malabou]]'s neurophilosophy and [[venkatesh-rao]]'s [[divergence-machine]] framework.

## The Divergence Machine Framework (Rao)

- **Modernity Machine**: constructed ~1200, plateau of capability 1600–2000, now in disassembly
- **Divergence Machine**: constructed ~1600, fully deployed for ~25 years
- Human history operates through massive "world machines"; we are in transition between them

## The Historical Arc of Identity-Stripping

| Era | Mechanism | What Gets Peeled |
|-----|-----------|-----------------|
| Pre-modern | Fiat Progress — religious/state authority | Reality subordinated to theological narrative |
| Spinoza, Hume | Philosophical naturalism | Theological conceits stripped; focus on reality |
| Industrial Revolution | Mass production, modernity machine | Humans as legible functional parts of centralized system |
| Consumer capitalism | Baudrillard's consumer identity | Objects chosen to signal identity, not personalized for us |
| AI | Cognitive labor automation | The last layer: professional know-how as identity |

## Key Claims

1. **Hume as "the first philosopher of the AI age"**: Hume abandoned causation theories for near-pure phenomenology — he "only treated the log files of reality as real." AI operates exactly the same way: it processes prompts (log files of know-how) without regard for grand narratives.
2. **The Baudrillard shift**: In pre-industrial times, technology was personalized for us. Now we choose objects to signal who we are — constructing consumer identity to remain legible to a dying system exhibiting "zombie persistence."
3. **The final strip**: AI automates cognitive labor — the very last layer of the fiction of identity. The result: "ontological dread."
4. **Flexibility vs. Plasticity** (Malabou):
   - *Flexibility*: taking forms due to external forces — plasticity without its genius
   - *Plasticity*: the active giving *and* taking of forms
5. **The divergence imperative**: In the new era, we must diverge not just from each other but from our immediate past selves — very quickly.

## Key Quotations

> "AI operates exactly like Hume's philosophy: it doesn't care about the grand narrative of who you are; it simply processes our prompts, the raw log files of our know-how."

> "With AI, our raw genius is left bare. There isn't much of external anything to situate our identities."

> "We are no longer required to be a flexible pliant cog in a dying machine."

## What's Missing

- The piece is optimistic about plasticity but doesn't address who benefits — those already living "plastically" (precarious workers, nomads) vs. those entering it suddenly
- No engagement with what new forms of collective identity or legibility might emerge post-divergence
- Hume as "proto-AI philosopher" is suggestive but somewhat loose; deserves a standalone concept page
