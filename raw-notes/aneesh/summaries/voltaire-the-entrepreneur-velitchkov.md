# Voltaire, the Entrepreneur

**Source:** "Voltaire, the Entrepreneur" by Ivo Velitchkov, Link & Think (Substack), Feb 6, 2026.

## Central Argument

Voltaire is remembered as an Enlightenment philosopher and playwright, but he was also a successful entrepreneur. At 76, he founded a watchmaking enterprise at Ferney that grew into an international business. His background in financial speculation and manufacturing positioned him well; the enterprise arose from political circumstances and operated simultaneously as a social enterprise and a business incubator.

## Prior Financial History

- **1729 lottery arbitrage**: With mathematician Charles Marie de la Condamine, exploited a miscalculation in state lottery where prize > total ticket price; earned ~500,000 livres
- **Share speculation**: Invested in duc de Lorraine shares (restricted to Lorraine citizens — Voltaire constructed dubious lineage proof), tripled money
- **Steady income**: Lending at interest, business investments including a military supply company
- **Silk startup**: Small silk business combining sericulture with stocking manufacture — shows affinity for vertical integration

## The Ferney Watchmaking Enterprise

**Circumstances**: Political conflict in Geneva between patrician families and natifs (ordinary citizens/artisans with limited rights) escalated in the 1760s, culminating in French blockade (1767) and renewed violence (1770). Voltaire tried to create a new trading port (Versoix) on French-Swiss border to reduce Geneva dependence — French government backed out financially. Meanwhile, skilled natif craftsmen were already arriving in Ferney.

**The startup**: Social enterprise + business incubator for natif refugees who were skilled craftsmen.

**Scale**:
- Craftsmen: 40 (1770) → 600 (1773)
- Revenue: 450K (1775) → 600K (1776) livres (~€10M today at peak)
- Markets: Spain, France, Russia, Turkey, Holland, Italy, Algeria, Tunisia

**Voltaire's roles** (per Ian Davidson): "not just the overall manager, co-ordinator and organiser but also the financier, the virtual bank manager, the sponsor, the builder of homes and factory space, the buyer of precious metals and other raw materials and the international sales manager."

**Favourable conditions created**: Housing for watchmakers, tax exemptions, interest-free financing (self-supplied).

## Illustrative Details

- Converted his theatre into workshops: "There, where we once recited verse, we are now melting gold and polishing cogs."
- Religious tolerance achievement: "sixty Huguenots live so well with my parishioners, that it would not be possible to guess that there are two religions here."
- Russia sales: driven by Catherine the Great's patronage (single rich fan model)
- Turkey: commerce reversed his pro-Catherine military hawkishness — commercial interests prevailed over ideology
- Tax politics: after achieving exemptions, he was appointed tax advisor and proposed a *progressive* tax — rich watchmakers pay more than poor peasants. Prior position completely reversed.
- Considered the estate an investment: "I am convinced that our property will double in price within a year." (Dec 1775)

## Decline and Legacy

Peak: summer 1776. New finance minister restored land tax and road-maintenance labor requirements; some watchmakers left. Business continued for decades after Voltaire's death (1778).

## Voltaire as Entrepreneur Among Pre-Industrial Peers

Velitchkov nominates the trio of Fugger, Wedgwood, and Voltaire as comparable pre-industrial entrepreneurs who contributed to the operation of the Modernity Machine — to be elaborated in the follow-up essay "Three Engineers of Modernity."

## Key Contradictions

- Voltaire's position-changing (Turkey, taxation, religion, France/Prussia) is a running theme — consistently subordinates principle to interest but frames reversals publicly
- The social enterprise began as a vehicle for natif refugees but became a profitable business whose tax privileges he then renegotiated
- Writing royalties were NOT a major income source (no copyright law, piracy, censorship, anonymous publication)
