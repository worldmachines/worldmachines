# Nakatomi Space

**Source**: [BLDGBLOG, January 11, 2010](https://www.bldgblog.com/2010/01/nakatomi-space/)
**Author**: [[Geoff Manaugh]]

## Central Argument

Buildings contain near-infinite interiors accessible through means that bypass their designed grammar (doors, hallways, stairwells). [[Nakatomi Space]] names this condition — after the Nakatomi Plaza of *Die Hard* — and connects it to a documented military practice: the IDF's "walking through walls" during the 2002 invasion of Nablus. The concept bridges cinema, architecture, urbanism, and military theory.

## The Two Primary Cases

### Die Hard / Nakatomi Plaza
John McClane moves through a Los Angeles high-rise via elevator shafts, air ducts, windows, and rooftop hatches — never once using the building's designed circulation system. The film asks: if you have to get from A to B, why use the grammar the architect intended?

### IDF in Nablus (2002) — Eyal Weizman, "Lethal Theory"
Soldiers moved through the city using "hundred-meter-long overground-tunnels carved through a dense and contiguous urban fabric." They moved:
- Horizontally through party walls
- Vertically through holes blasted in ceilings and floors
- Using *none* of the streets, alleys, doors, stairwells, or windows

> "More than half of the buildings in the old city center of Nablus had routes forced through them, resulting in anywhere from one to eight openings in their walls, floors, or ceilings."

## The Concept: Nakatomi Space

Buildings reveal **near-infinite interiors** when traversed through non-architectural means. The topology of a building is not fixed by its design but by the traversal methods applied to it. "Walking through walls" becomes a kind of **militarized parkour**.

Weizman notes this is not new — the defenders of the Paris Commune, Kasbah of Algiers, Hue, Beirut, Jenin, and Nablus all used the same technique.

## The Bourne/Bond Contrast (Matt Jones)
- **Bond**: exotic destinations, international late-capitalist nonplace; "establishing shots" that consume architecture
- **Bourne**: Schengen-space, borderless Mitteleurope — public infrastructure as superpower. "A battered watch and an accurate U-Bahn timetable are all he needs."

This is the urban-scale version of Nakatomi space: Bourne *hacks* the city rather than being positioned within it.

## Key Claims

1. **Architecture has grammar**: streets, corridors, doors, stairwells constitute the "syntax of the city" — and syntax can be bypassed
2. **Non-architectural traversal reveals hidden topology**: every building contains routes its architect never intended
3. **This is ancient**: not a modern military innovation — it is the default tactic of the outnumbered defending dense urban terrain
4. **Militarized parkour**: the transgression of designed space as combat technique

## Key Quotations
