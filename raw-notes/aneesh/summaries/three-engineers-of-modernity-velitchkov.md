# Three Engineers of Modernity

**Source:** "Three Engineers of Modernity" by Ivo Velitchkov, Link & Think (Substack), Feb 27, 2026.

## Central Argument

Three pre-industrial entrepreneurs — [[Jakob Fugger]], [[Josiah Wedgwood]], and [[Voltaire]] — were not just successful businessmen but *engineers of the Modernity Machine*. Each transformed one or more of the four class relationships that define the MM: monarch ↔ oligarchy, oligarchy ↔ technocracy, technocracy ↔ workers. Two (Wedgwood and Voltaire) also made early "commits" to the [[divergence-machine]].

## The Modernity Machine's Structure

The MM (constructed 1200–1600) restructures four class relations:
1. **Monarch ↔ Oligarchy**
2. **Oligarchy ↔ Technocracy**
3. **Technocracy ↔ Workers**
4. **Monarch ↔ Technocracy**

The machine's core optimization: **legibility**. "To control space, time, people, resources, goods and money, they had to be made readable."

Theoretical grounding: Velitchkov draws on [[venkatesh-rao]]'s Modernity Machine series, [[Bruno Latour]]'s *We Have Never Been Modern* (science and politics pretend not to mix so they can mix more effectively), [[Max Weber]]'s disenchantment, and [[Niklas Luhmann]]'s systems theory. Notably uses Varela/autopoiesis to defend the word "machine" itself.

## The Three Engineers

### Jakob Fugger (1459–1525) — Financial Legibility
- Merchant → miner → banker; wealth reached ~2% of European GDP
- **Class transformation**: Converted the monarch-oligarch relation from divine right to *contractual creditor-debtor* relationship
- **Innovations**: Double-entry bookkeeping (learned in Venice), pioneering use of auditors, private information network (intelligence before rivals and monarchs), consolidated financial statements across branches (precursor to ERP), virtual money transfer
- **Legibility contribution**: Made financial information reliable, timely, interoperable, consolidated
- **Moral complexity**: Financed the bloody suppression of the German Peasants' War (100,000 deaths); the Fuggerei social housing was "Feudalism 2.0"

### Josiah Wedgwood (1730–1795) — Industrial Legibility
- Turned pottery into an industry; pioneered modern marketing and branding
- **Class transformation**: Used royal patronage ("Potter to Her Majesty") to cascade desire through social hierarchy — monarch signals prestige → nobles imitate → merchants follow → mass consumption
- **Innovations**: Pyrometer (one of the first temperature measurement devices), illustrated catalogues, time-tables and clock-in systems, primitive cost accounting revealing fixed-cost leverage, division of labor
- **Legibility contribution**: Made kiln temperatures measurable, workers' time legible, brand as market signal
- **Contradiction**: Anti-slavery abolitionist (famous medallion) whose business depended on the sugar economy driven by slave trade

### Voltaire (1694–1778) — Epistemic and Political Legibility
- Transformed the intellectual from "subservient court pet" to "negotiated celebrity"
- **Class transformation (monarch-technocrat)**: Bought financial independence through lottery exploitation and lending; remained embedded in elite networks without being captured by them
- **Class transformation (technocracy-oligarchy)**: Like Fugger, became *creditor to the ruling class*
- **Class transformation (oligarchy-workers)**: Supported Genevian natifs and provided platform for independent watchmakers; penal reform campaigns made state accountable to public opinion
- **Legibility contributions**: Made state legible to citizens (exposing secret French legal proceedings, Calas affair), made science legible to the public (Newton book), made his own influence portable (first European celebrity if celebrity = private life consumed as commodity)
- **Moral complexity**: Changed positions on Turkey and taxation for personal gain; fabricated letters; performed Easter communion for social access while privately mocking it

## Legibility as Machine Core

Both Fugger and Wedgwood exemplify the MM's drive toward legibility — financial and industrial respectively. The state itself (post-Westphalia) created legibility through surnames, standardized weights/measures, cadastres. Voltaire inverted this: making the state legible to people.

## Commits to the Divergence Machine

Wedgwood and Voltaire (but not Fugger) also contributed to the [[divergence-machine]]:

- **Wedgwood**: Relentless experimentation as an end in itself (not just commercial), participation in the pluralist [[Lunar Society]], treating progress as growth of instrumental knowledge rather than material plenty
- **Voltaire**: *Candide* as attack on Leibniz's prefigured optimism; campaigns for tolerance as civilizational space for plurality; attacked dogma with absurdity rather than counter-dogma

The Divergence Machine "brackets" the Modernity Machine rather than subverting it — creating civilizational space beyond centralized control. It "treats bugs as features" in normal operating mode. Operators must live in "ontological doubt."

Rao's first commits to DM: credited to Spinoza.

## What's Missing / Contradictions

- The essay explicitly de-heroizes its subjects ("people are complicated, the world is messy") — the messiness that was a *bug* for the MM is a *feature* for the DM
- The selection of three engineers is acknowledged as partly due to preferential attachment in networks (notability loops notability) — there may have been bigger contributors who were quieter
- The cybernetics/autopoiesis framing (Varela) is invoked in defense of the word "machine" — interesting given Rao's explicit skepticism of these frameworks
