# Cosmic Evolution: State of the Science — Eric J. Chaisson (2009)

**Source**: Chapter 1 in *Cosmos and Culture*, NASA SP-2009-4802, eds. S.J. Dick and M.L. Lupisella  
**Author**: Eric J. Chaisson (Harvard)

## Central Argument

Cosmic evolution is the unifying narrative of all change in the universe — from the Big Bang to humankind — and its driver is **energy**. Specifically, the metric of **energy rate density** (free energy flow per unit mass, measured in erg/s/g) serves as an objective, cross-domain index of complexity. Higher energy rate density = greater complexity, regardless of whether the system is a star, a plant, an animal brain, or a civilization. This framework dissolves the traditional boundary between physical, biological, and cultural evolution.

## Framework: Seven Evolutionary Phases

The "arrow of time" passes through:
1. **Particulate** — elementary particles
2. **Galactic** — galaxy formation
3. **Stellar** — stars and heavy element production
4. **Planetary** — rocky planets
5. **Chemical** — precursor molecules to life
6. **Biological** — cells to organisms
7. **Cultural** — language, technology, civilization

All phases are simultaneously active — stars still form; galaxies still evolve. The phases are overlapping durations, not stages that end.

## Three Eras of Natural History

1. **Radiation Era**: First ~500,000 years post-Big Bang. Uniform, symmetrical, informationless. Radiation dominated. No structure possible.
2. **Matter Era**: Radiation gives way to matter; galaxies, stars, planets, and life emerge. "Natural history became more interesting."
3. **Life Era**: Now beginning — technologically intelligent life can manipulate matter as matter once overwhelmed radiation. Life is becoming a *cosmological actor*, not just a product.

## Nonequilibrium Thermodynamics as Engine

- Complexity violates neither the first nor second law of thermodynamics
- Open systems that export entropy to their environments can build internal order — "order through fluctuations" (Prigogine 1972)
- Energy flow is necessary and probably sufficient to build structures: local order increases at the expense of larger environmental disorder
- The second law of thermodynamics is satisfied at the system + environment level even as local complexity rises
- Key thinkers: Bertalanffy (1932) — open systems; Schrödinger (1944) — *What is Life?*; Prigogine (1972) — dissipative structures; Salk (1982)

## Energy Rate Density as Complexity Metric

Erg/second/gram rankings (approximate):
- Stars: ~1 erg/s/g
- Planets: ~75 erg/s/g  
- Plants: ~900 erg/s/g
- Animals: ~20,000 erg/s/g
- Brains: ~150,000 erg/s/g
- Modern society: ~500,000+ erg/s/g

This is *not* absolute energy (a star has more energy than a brain) but energy *per unit mass* — a normalization that reveals the brain as extraordinarily complex relative to its physical substrate.

## Selection, Broadly Construed

- "Natural selection" is a misnomer — "nonrandom elimination" (Ernst Mayr) is more precise
- Selection = *preferential interaction of any object with its environment*
- Applies to stars (which "evolve" through nuclear burning cycles), plants, animals, and machines
- Machine evolution is more Lamarckian than Darwinian (acquired traits accumulate, e.g. aircraft engines from Wright Flyer to F-117)

## The Life Era Thesis

> "Life is now beginning to lose its [matter's] dominance, at least at those isolated locales of hi-tech civilization, such as on planet Earth."

- Technology enables life to manipulate matter and energy, much as matter overwhelmed radiation
- Civilization + machines will likely emerge as a "symbiotically functioning technoculture" — highest known complexity in nature
- This is either the onset of a new cosmological era or "a passing event in spacetime whereby human life on Earth and its great cultural experiment end"

## Key Quotations

> "Evolution, broadly considered, has become a powerful unifying concept in all of science."

> "The flow of energy, as dictated by nonequilibrium thermodynamics in an expanding universe, does seem to provide a powerful means to explain the growth of order, form, and complexity on all scales, from quarks to quasars, from microbes to minds."

> "For we, too, partake in the cosmic-evolutionary narrative, an epic-class story of rich natural history for the new millennium."

## What's Missing
- No engagement with information-theoretic approaches to complexity (Shannon, Kolmogorov)
- The "Life Era" thesis is speculative and anthropocentric despite disclaimers
- Scant treatment of what triggers phase transitions between evolutionary eras
- The unification thesis risks being too smooth — it may paper over genuine discontinuities between physical and biological evolution
