# Theory — World Machines (Canonical)

**Source**: [worldmachines.org/theory](https://worldmachines.org/theory) (public-facing canonical theory page of the [[World Machines Project]])
**Origin**: Grew out of the [[Contraptions Book Club]]; framework developed by [[venkatesh-rao]] and contributors
**Status**: The reference page. All World Machine essays in this wiki should anchor terminology and definitions here.

> "A World Machine is a large-scale civilizational system — an interlocking set of institutions, technologies, practices, and assumptions — that organizes human life across centuries. Not a nation, not an empire, not an ideology, but something longer-lived and more fundamental than any of those: the deep infrastructure of a historical era."

## Why This Page Matters

This is the project's **public-facing canonical** statement — refined and consolidated relative to Rao's earlier internal/announcement essays (see [[the-world-machines-project-rao]] for the announcement). It distills:

- The minimal definition of a World Machine
- The three-machine simultaneity hypothesis
- The lifecycle structure (≈400 + ≈400 + rapid decline)
- The five canonical open questions
- The project's epistemological stance

For internal mechanics and framework apparatus, see [[world-machines-framework]]. For the concept-object analysis, see [[world-machines]]. **This page is the source those pages defer to for canonical phrasing.**

## The Definition (Verbatim)

A World Machine is:
- An **interlocking set** of institutions, technologies, practices, and assumptions
- That organizes human life **across centuries**
- "**The deep infrastructure** of a historical era"

What it is *not*: a nation, an empire, or an ideology. Each of those operates inside the World Machine and inherits its grammar.

## Lifecycle: ≈1000 Years, Three Phases

| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Dawn** | ~400 years | Emerging, not yet dominant; logic still being worked out |
| **Day** | ~400 years | Mature, dominant; the organizing framework most people take for granted |
| **Dusk** | rapid decline | Increasingly unable to solve the problems it once handled, but still structurally present |

Three machines **always coexist** — a Dawn, a Day, and a Dusk — overlapping, interacting, competing. See [[Dawn Day Dusk Machine Typology]].

## Conflict as Machine Friction

The page's most consequential reframing:

> "Much of what we call political, cultural, and economic conflict is better understood as friction between them."

This is a strong claim: that the texture of contemporary discourse — confusion, contradiction, polarization — is *not* a failure of reasoning or institutions but a structural consequence of three civilizational logics operating at once.

## The Three Machines Currently Studied

| Machine | Construction | Day Phase | Status (2026) | Animating Drive |
|---------|-------------|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| [[Modernity Machine]] | c. 1200–1600 | 1600–2000 | **Dusk** | **Progress** — *pull* system: convergence on universal development, scientific mastery, liberal order |
| [[divergence-machine]] | c. 1600–2000 | 2000– | **Day** (early) | *Push* system: radically different outcomes from local conditions, values, trajectories. No single destination. |
| [[Liveness Machine]] | now | future | **Dawn** (early) | Outlines only beginning to be visible |

The pull/push distinction is the canonical framing for what differentiates Modernity from Divergence. See [[pull-vs-push-machines]].

## Epistemological Stance

The page is explicit about what the framework is and isn't:

**It is**:
- "Sensemaking triage and a shared conceptual language"
- A **nonfiction extended universe** — shared scaffolding allowing many writers working independently to produce mutually-legible, cumulatively-stronger analyses
- A way to **organize observations across multiple disciplines in novel ways**

**It is not**:
- A deterministic theory of history
- A claim that history is mechanically cyclical
- A claim that the future is predictable

The "extended universe" framing is the operational metaphor: writers contributing essays into a shared canon. See [[world-machines-as-interim-theory]] for a Weickian defense of this stance.

## The Five Canonical Open Questions

The project is explicitly organized around five foundational unanswered questions:

1. **Inventory** — What is the complete inventory of World Machines since the emergence of complex civilization?
2. **Demarcation** — What are the necessary and sufficient conditions for identifying something as a World Machine, rather than a mere institution or technology?
3. **Fractal structure** — Do World Machines have fractal structure — sub-machines operating on shorter cycles within longer ones?
4. **Internal phenomenology** — How did the people living inside a prevailing machine understand it — and what were the limits of that self-understanding?
5. **Phase pathologies** — What are the characteristic pathologies of Dawn, Day, and Dusk phases?

Questions 4 and 5 are the most generative for essay seeds: they invite both historical excavation (what did medievals see when they looked at modernity dawning?) and contemporary diagnosis (what are the pathologies of late-Dusk Modernity, early-Day Divergence, faint-Dawn Liveness?).

## The Long-Term Goal

> "Oracular rather than predictive, in the spirit of Asimov's psychohistory."

The aim is to combine qualitative historiographic analysis with quantitative methods and AI modeling to generate long-term forecasts and speculations. The "oracular" framing is load-bearing: see [[psychohistory-and-world-machines]] for how the project distances itself from Asimov's chaos-blind determinism while keeping the spirit of civilizational-scale pattern recognition.

## What This Page Reframes vs. the Internal Documents

The internal Rao essays (see [[the-world-machines-project-rao]], [[world-machines-framework]]) gave the apparatus. This canonical page **simplifies and re-anchors** in three ways:

1. **Conflict-as-friction** is foregrounded — not buried in sub-points but stated as the main interpretive payoff
2. **Pull/push** is presented as the primary contrast between Modernity and Divergence, accessible without prior context
3. **Five clean open questions** replace the longer scattered lists in earlier documents — suitable as the standing research agenda

The "characteristic pathologies of Dawn/Day/Dusk phases" is the most underdeveloped open question in the wiki and likely the richest essay-seed cluster.

## Essay Seeds (from the Five Open Questions)

- **The Pathologies of Dusk** — what does a Dusk Machine look like from inside? Symptoms catalog: zombie persistence, ghost intelligence, necromantic resurrection (cf. [[late-modernity-as-energetic-zombie]], [[postmodernity-as-intelligent-ghost]]).
- **The Phenomenology of Living Inside** — Open question 4. What did medievals see in dawning Modernity? What did 17th-century moderns see in dying Medievalism? What do *we* see in the Liveness Machine? The "limits of self-understanding" framing is rich.
- **What Counts as a World Machine** — Open question 2 as essay: the demarcation problem. Bronze Age tin trade as test case. Why is the printing press not a WM but Modernity is?
- **Fractal Machines** — Open question 3 as essay: are there sub-machines (corporate machines, regional machines) that share lifecycle structure with civilizational ones?
