# Bayo Akomolafe — The Untimely

Source: `essays/raw_sources/2026-05-20_bayo-akomolafe-the-untimely-youtube-transcript.md`; YouTube talk for The Long Now Foundation, 2026-05-20. Transcript is auto-generated and should be checked against the video before exact quotation.

## Central Argument

[[bayo-akomolafe]] argues that modern time is neither natural nor neutral: it is an accommodation that makes bodies legible, punctual, productive, and governable. But his response is not to create another temporality — indigenous time, deep time, long time, spiritual time — as an alternative clock. Alternatives too often reproduce the logic of what they oppose. The more radical possibility is [[untimely]]: the excess, gap, or surplus generated inside the clock itself, the interval between tick and tock where bodies can syncopate, fail, misbehave, grieve, and be enlisted by futures they cannot plan.

## Key Moves

1. **Critique of long/deep time** — Akomolafe begins at Long Now by criticizing Long Now's own idiom. Ten-thousand-year clocks and geological deep time may extend the horizon, but they can leave human mastery intact: "look at us with the capacity to calculate millions of years into the future." See [[deep-time-without-decentering]].
2. **Yoruba twin cosmology** — The story of Taiwo, Kehinde, and Idowu becomes the conceptual engine. Idowu, the child after twins, is older than the twins and holds their charge without resolving them. The [[idowu-function]] names this third term: not synthesis, not alternative, but excess that hosts a binary.
3. **The clock's incompleteness** — Plantation bells, dancing the slave, and the Middle Passage show time as discipline, but also as incomplete discipline. The downbeat creates an upbeat; the ledger-mark creates a not-mark. See [[clock-as-incomplete-discipline]].
4. **Accommodation of modernity** — Modernity hates cracks because cracks obstruct legibility. It pathologizes monsters, lateness, African time, disability, and failure as defects, while these may be sites where the accommodation's incompleteness appears. See [[accommodation-of-modernity]].
5. **Parapolitics, not politics** — Ordinary politics seeks visibility and recognition inside the accommodation. [[parapolitics-of-the-untimely]] works in excess of visibility: sanctuary, gathering, cooking, reading, grieving, and staying with cracks without converting them into a program.
6. **Awkward grace** — In the Q&A, grace is not smooth arrival but monstrous interruption: the returning Christ whom the church imprisons because he disrupts its business. See [[awkward-grace]].
