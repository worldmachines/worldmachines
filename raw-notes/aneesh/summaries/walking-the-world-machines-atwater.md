# Walking the World Machines

**Source:** "Walking the World Machines" by Patrick Atwater (Pioneering Spirit, Substack/Paragraph), March 30, 2026.

## Central Argument

Atwater weaves together two slow projects — Paul Salopek's decade-long Out of Eden Walk retracing human migration out of Africa, and Venkatesh Rao's World Machines framework — as complementary modes of making sense of the present. Salopek's ground-level, worm's-eye journalism is offered as a methodological complement to the WM framework's civilizational-scale lens.

## On Paul Salopek's Out of Eden Walk

- ~12 years into a walk retracing *Homo sapiens*' migration route out of Africa across major landmasses
- Starting in Ethiopia, now reaching far North of the Americas
- A "NatGeo mashup of anthropology and slow journalism"
- Emblematic post: container ships — "rewilds something mundane about our world"
- Methodology: worm's-eye view, no geopolitical headlines or tech trends, just everyday life across the world

## On the World Machines Framework

Atwater's initial skepticism (deep liberal arts training, trained to poke holes in Big History grand unified theories) gave way to appreciation that VGR's project is "foxlike" — signposts and shared vocabulary rather than GUT. "Intellectually enjoyable and also effective play in a world undergoing lots of seismic shifts."

## Pre-Axial Speculative Extensions

Atwater plays with what constitutes the substrate of world machines *before* the Axial Age:

### Numbers Before Words
- The stable finding that numerals predated words in codified language
- Earliest numerals: Egypt ~3400 BCE, Mesopotamia ~3000 BCE — long before China, Crete, India
- Base choice (10 vs 20) depended on whether counters used fingers only (decimal) or fingers+toes (vigesimal)
- Maya: three symbols (line, dot, shell for zero), fully vigesimal
- Babylonians: sexagesimal (base-60) — legacy in hours/minutes
- Celtic traces in modern French: "quatre-vingt" (80 = four-twenties)
- Implication: numeral systems were "competing solutions to making quantities legible across populations and time" — each rooted in somatic fact of counting appendages
- Connection to WM: **counting/numeracy as a key substrate of legibility**

### Domestication and Self-Domestication
- Plants and animals domesticated, "arguably ourselves" too
- Agriculture enabled population growth but often *lowered* modal standard of living
- Many instances of humans leaving settled agricultural cities to return to hunting and gathering
- Reference: *Ishmael* (Daniel Quinn) — humanity creating conditions of captivity for itself as nomadic lifeways lost to sedentary cities

### Nomadic vs. Sedentary
- By 1200 CE, sedentary civilizations' triumphal march over nomadic lifeways was near-final
- Vikings: last gasps in Europe
- Mongols: upended Eurasian world but since 1600 no nomadic power has had comparable geopolitical impact
- Open question: will this change as humanity expands (space) or climate disruption reshapes habitability?

## On Consciousness
- "Stone ape" theory: psilocybin mushrooms helped induce self-awareness in simian ancestors
- Julian Jaynes: consciousness was not always conceptualized as a monolithic single experience — "bicameral mind"
- Reference to Sachin's piece on "revenge of the bicameral mind" and implications for living with AI

## On the Liveness Machine
- "I don't know what constitutes liveness" — appropriate epistemic humility
- The "cyberpaleo" approach (taking a broader view of work and play, looking at slow takes like Salopek's journey) seems "apropos" as a method for understanding liveness
- The cyberpaleo concept: seeing the blurred world of bits through Paleolithic-ancestral eyes to notice what's been lost/gained in embodied, place-based living

## What's Missing

- No formal argument about *how* Salopek's walk methodology should interact with WM theorizing
- The pre-Axial extensions are speculative footnotes, not developed arguments
- The cyberpaleo-to-Liveness Machine connection is gestured at but not argued
