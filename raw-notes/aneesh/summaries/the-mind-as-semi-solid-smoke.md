# The Mind as Semi-Solid Smoke

**Source**: https://aneeshsathe.com/the-mind-as-semi-solid-smoke/
**Author**: [[Aneesh Sathe]]
**Date**: 2025-07-07

## Central Argument

The mind is the inward application of the [[Space and Place in Cognition]] framework. From the outside it appears as a unified place (our identity); probe it and it dissolves fractally into smaller, more loosely anchored places. The navigational landmarks we use internally — "insta-places" conjured from experience — are treated as solid even when they are not. This is not a bug but a feature: **normative self-blindness** keeps us moving. Understanding this makes Socratic inquiry psychologically easier to adopt.

## Key Concepts

### Biological Basis of Place-Making
- Navigation is deep evolutionary heritage: even bacteria performing chemotaxis use signal-fields as proto-places.
- At the cellular scale, *territory is the map*; at human scale, we externalize cues as landmarks, then mirror the process internally.
- References: Chan et al. (2012) on visual landmarks in spatial navigation; Freas & Cheng (2022) on cross-species navigation.

### Identity as First Place
- From outside, the mind is a single place — our identity.
- Closer inspection: identity dissolves like a city dissolving into neighbourhoods into streets.
- We use identity to create the first stable place, then use other places to navigate life.

### Insta-Places
- Cognitive shortcuts: landmarks conjured from experience, treated as solid regardless of epistemic grounding.
- Accept: places loosely formed in the past (errors) or not yet defined in the future (unknowns).
- Cannot accept: that a current insta-place might be false *right now* — that would undermine the very stance from which we operate.

### Normative Self-Blindness (after [[Agnes Callard]])
- The built-in refusal to saw off the branch we stand on.
- A trick to gloss over detail and keep moving — functionally useful, epistemically dangerous.
- Key tenet for Socratic inquiry: *admit* that everyone's guiding landmarks may be semi-solid smoke.

## The Fractal Metaphor
> "Our identity is nebulous and dissolves the way a city dissolves into smaller and smaller places the closer you look."

## What's Missing / Not Addressed
- The neuroscience citations are light — the claim of "biological predisposition" gestures at work on hippocampal place cells and grid cells that is not unpacked.
- No account of how insta-places form: learning, cultural transmission, trauma?
- The positive case for *updating* insta-places (the mechanics of Socratic inquiry resolving a bad landmark) is deferred.
