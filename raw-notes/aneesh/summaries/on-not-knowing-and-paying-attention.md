# On Not Knowing and Paying Attention

**Tim Ingold**, "On not knowing and paying attention: How to walk in a possible world," *Irish Journal of Sociology* 31(1): 20-36 (2023). Written for the volume *Walking Methodologies: Sociological Research on the Move*; it began as a March 2020 keynote to the "Walking Conversations: The Art of Paying Attention" workshop at University College Cork, and is assembled as a collage from three of Ingold's own books -- *The Life of Lines* (2015), *Anthropology and/as Education* (2018), and *Imagining for Real* (2022) ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-back-matter-references]]).

## The argument in one line

Knowledge and wisdom face opposite directions: knowledge turns away from the world to secure certainty and prediction; wisdom turns toward it and pays attention. Because a world of life is irreducibly uncertain, that uncertainty is not a deficit to be cured but the very excess that makes possibility real -- and so "not-knowing" is a form of wisdom, not a form of ignorance ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-abstract-and-keywords]]).

## Structure and throughlines

### 1. Knowledge vs. wisdom; self vs. soul
The essay opens on a frame poem: a boy walking home from school finds a bearded stranger crouched with a telescope, watching the moon. From it Ingold draws his governing dichotomy. Knowledge builds "a citadel of its own making," adding a stone to its walls with every increment and fixing the world within concepts "so they no longer trouble us"; wisdom leaves the soul undefended and open. The *self* is "an invention of modern thought" -- an interior intelligence that theorizes but cannot itself touch the world; the *soul*, borrowed from "peoples ... uncrushed by the modernist onslaught" and from [[michel-serres]], is a vortex "cast upon the current of life" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-man-in-the-moon-knowledge-and-wisdom]]).

### 2. Wisdom beyond the facts; observation as correspondence
Wisdom is not assembled from facts or "data": facts can be manufactured and manipulated, but truth was never reducible to what can be objectively ascertained. To attend to something is "to bring things to presence" by joining with them, not looking at them from a distance. Ingold turns this into an ethics of science, calling the normal-science protocol of extracting data "by force or subterfuge" and then "cutting and running" *deeply unethical*, and asking science to re-embed itself in wisdom as "a labour of love" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-turn-towards-world-uncertainty-possibility]]).

### 3. Uncertainty as excess, not deficit (Bergson)
Against the wish for certainty -- which would only "augur the dead-end" -- uncertainty is reframed as the sign that life "continually overreaches itself," pure excess rather than missing knowledge. Following [[henri-bergson]], genuine possibility outruns the probability to which science restricts itself; the chance/determination binary "drains life of its creative impulse, reducing freedom to random variation within a phase space." The moon -- fully law-bound yet, to the attentive eye, never twice the same -- is the essay's recurring emblem ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-turn-towards-world-uncertainty-possibility]], [[probability-as-epistemology]]).

### 4. Replenishing potential, not fulfilling it; doing and undergoing
Against the Western "fulfilment of potential" -- a funnel narrowing a thousand possible lives to the one lived -- Ingold sets the Pintupi "one-possibility thing," a life walked into and continually renewed. Reading Dewey's *Art as Experience*, he argues that undergoing always overflows doing: whatever you do carries something of what you did before into what you do next, so a life is a longitudinal "becoming rather than being" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-potential-fulfilment-doing-undergoing]]).

### 5. The two sides of attention; falling forwards
Attention has two sides. *Attunement* is Gibson's skilled uptake of the affordances a stable world already offers -- the carpenter reading grain, the smith reading iron -- an "education of attention." *Exposure* (via Masschelein's etymology, ex-positio, "pulled out of position") is submission to a world that is restless and unfinished. The two alternate in one fixed direction, exemplified by walking as a controlled fall -- every step a moment of jeopardy resolved into balance. On this footing anticipation "opens a path" while prediction closes one ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-two-sides-of-attention-falling-forwards]], [[wayfinding-as-practice]]).

### 6. Generations as a braid; astonishment vs. surprise
Ingold rejects "stratigraphic thinking" -- generations as stacked layers that inherit a stock and pass on the discards -- for an intergenerational *braid* of overlapping strands, where transmission can break without extinction. He then distinguishes *astonishment* (the openness of those genuinely attentive to the world) from *surprise* (what befalls prediction when it fails), and *not-knowing* from *ignorance*: scientists "are surprised but not astonished," while indigenous peoples and children "yet to be subdued" are "perpetually astonished but never surprised" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-passage-of-generations-astonishment-surprise]]).

### 7. Correspondence vs. interaction; understanding vs. undercommoning
*Correspondence* -- a longitudinal, side-by-side "middle-voice" joining-with, where "I am my walking, and my walking walks me" -- is set against *interaction*'s transverse, interest-bound back-and-forth between fixed points. Living correspondence well means choosing Harney and Moten's *undercommoning* ("a way of living together in possibility") over the false security of settled *understanding*, and sustaining the uncategorizing astonishment Erin Manning locates in autistic attention and in artists ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-correspondence-interaction-understanding-undercommoning]]).

### 8. Real problems, real freedom; the production of ignorance
Fake problems -- the jigsaw, the crossword, the maze -- come pre-loaded with their own solution; so does "fake freedom," a menu of pre-fabricated objectives dressed up as choice. Real freedom is "the singular possibility of the path." Citing Ranciere, Ingold indicts "education, science and the state" as "all-powerful machines for the production of ignorance," and closes by choosing to "walk in the moon's company" rather than "plant our feet upon the moon itself, only to leave our prints in the dust" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-real-problems-real-freedom-conclusion]]).

## Recurring devices

- **The moon and the frame poem** return in every section as the concrete anchor for an abstract argument about attention.
- **Transverse vs. longitudinal** is the essay's master geometry: intention, interaction, and doing run transverse (point to point); attention, correspondence, and undergoing run longitudinal (along the way).
- **Paired oppositions** carry the argument throughout: knowledge/wisdom, self/soul, fixing/unfixing, prediction/anticipation, surprise/astonishment, ignorance/not-knowing, interaction/correspondence, understanding/undercommoning, fake/real freedom.
