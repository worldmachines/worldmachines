# Nick Cave

Australian musician, novelist, and cultural figure. Most relevant here as the author of a widely-circulated letter arguing that using ChatGPT to write songs is fundamentally wrong — not merely stylistically inferior but spiritually empty.

## The Letter

Cave's argument, as narrated by [[Stephen Fry]] in a video Sathe cites (see [[On Being Good vs. Knowing Good]]): AI creativity shortcuts bypass the formation of the judgment required to recognize greatness. The imperfection of the creative process is not a bug — it is the mechanism through which taste develops. AI can approximate the output of creative work without having undergone the experience that makes the output meaningful.

Sathe's response is notably sympathetic: "Surprisingly, I found myself in agreement." He relocates Cave's intuition from a spiritual/artistic register to an epistemological one: the problem with AI as a creative shortcut is not mystical but structural — taste is experience-dependent, and shortcuts skip the experience.

## Intellectual Position

Cave's letter represents one of the more philosophically interesting critiques of AI creativity — not "AI can't be as good as humans" (empirically contested) but "the process of striving and failing is constitutive of creative capacity." This maps to a virtue-ethics framework: excellence is a habit formed through practice, not a property of outputs.
