# Louis Althusser (1918–1990)

French Marxist philosopher, professor at the École normale supérieure. Major works include *For Marx* (1965), *Reading Capital* (with Étienne Balibar, 1965), and *Ideology and Ideological State Apparatuses* (1970).

## Core Position (for Massey's purposes)

Massey's interest in [[doreen-massey]]'s *For Space* (2005) is Althusser's critique of **Hegel's "essential section"** — the notion that the cross-section of historical time at any moment is a totality in which every part expresses the whole, in immediate relation to every other part. Hegelian time is **homogeneous** and **expressive**: every moment is the moment of the whole.

Althusser objects:

> "All the elements of the whole revealed by this section are in an immediate relationship with one another, a relationship that immediately expresses their internal essence." (Althusser 1970: 94, quoted Massey 2005: 40)

For Althusser, this is "an expressive totality, i.e., a totality all of whose parts are so many 'total parts', each expressing the others." This forecloses the possibility of real difference, real alterity, real multiple temporalities. He wants instead a Marxism in which different "instances" of the social formation (economic, political, ideological) operate with their own distinct temporalities — uneven development at the conceptual level.

## Why This Matters for Massey

Althusser provides Massey with a model: it is possible to **abandon the homogeneous cross-section** without abandoning materialist analysis. Once you do, the "spatial" can no longer be the static synchronic slice — it must be something else. Althusser himself does not develop this consequence (he is interested in temporalities, not spatialities), but his critique opens the door.

Massey also reads Althusser's "synchrony=closed system" diagnosis as the most rigorous version of the **prison-house** complaint (see [[prison-house-of-synchrony]]). Where Lévi-Strauss "ruses" the move (substituting diachrony for history), Althusser exposes it.

## Where Massey Diverges

Althusser's own vocabulary still treats "spatial" as roughly synonymous with "structural-closed." He does not push toward a positive theory of the spatial as multiplicity. The conceptual work he opens, Massey takes up. See [[space-as-the-dimension-of-multiplicity]].
