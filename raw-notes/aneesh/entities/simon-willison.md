# Simon Willison

## Identity

British software developer, writer, and open-source practitioner. Creator of Datasette (a tool for exploring SQLite databases). Runs a personal blog and link blog known for consistently high-quality curation of technical and intellectual content.

## Link Blogging Model

[[Jan. 3, 2025]] cites Willison as the model for ethical link blogging. His principles:

1. **Always name the creator** — not just as courtesy but for future searchability
2. **Quote a key paragraph** — help readers decide if the source is worth their time
3. **Add original commentary** — your post should make reading *both* your commentary and the source better than reading either alone

> "My goal with any link blog post is that if you read both my post and the source material you'll have an enhanced experience over if you read just the source material itself."

This is a quality standard — an explicit commitment against pure re-posting or empty aggregation.

## Significance in this Wiki

Willison exemplifies [[Personal Media vs Platform Media]] at its best: high-ownership, quality-first curation that improves on the source rather than merely redistributing it. His model is the antithesis of AI-generated summary content.

He also connects to [[Dwelling as Philosophical Concept]]: his link posts create a place where readers can dwell with ideas, rather than a platform-optimized feed that processes them through an engagement algorithm.
