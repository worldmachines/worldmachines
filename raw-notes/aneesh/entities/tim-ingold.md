# Tim Ingold

Tim Ingold is a British social anthropologist whose work reworks perception, skill, movement, and life into a single relational vocabulary -- **correspondence**, **wayfaring**, **lines** and the meshwork, and the **education of attention**. In this wiki he enters through his 2023 essay [[on-not-knowing-and-paying-attention]], itself a collage of three of his books -- *The Life of Lines* (2015), *Anthropology and/as Education* (2018), and *Imagining for Real* (2022) -- so the essay's recurring formulations are restatements whose fuller versions live in that trilogy ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-back-matter-references]]).

## Signature concepts

**Correspondence.** Ingold's central term: the process "by which beings or things literally answer to one another over time" -- a longitudinal, side-by-side joining-*with* (letters, conversation, gifts, holding hands), sharply distinguished from **interaction**'s transverse, interest-bound back-and-forth. Correspondence unfolds in the grammatical "middle voice," in which "whatever you do is also achieved in you" -- "I am my walking, and my walking walks me" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-correspondence-interaction-understanding-undercommoning]]). It is kin to [[structural-coupling]] and to [[dwelling-as-philosophical-concept]] but insistently open-ended, with no end set in advance.

**Education of attention.** Adapting Gibson's ecological psychology, Ingold treats skill as a perceptual system becoming attuned to affordances -- the carpenter reading the grain of wood, the smith reading the ductility of iron -- so that learning is less the transmission of representations than the honing of attention ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-two-sides-of-attention-falling-forwards]]).

**Lines, wayfaring, and longitudinal life.** Ingold thinks a life as a line rather than a point-to-point sequence: any single act of doing is a transverse link between an intention and an objective, but the undergoing that accompanies it runs longitudinally along the way, so a life is a "becoming rather than being" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-potential-fulfilment-doing-undergoing]]). Compare [[wayfinding-as-practice]] and [[trajectories-as-stories-so-far]].

**Self and soul; against the "modernist onslaught."** Ingold sets the modern, self-enclosing "self" against an ancient, radically open "soul," and repeatedly draws his positive terms from "peoples ... uncrushed by the modernist onslaught" -- Pintupi cosmology, indigenous and childhood openness "yet to be subdued by adult disciplinary oppression." This gives his phenomenology an explicit counter-modern edge ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-man-in-the-moon-knowledge-and-wisdom]], [[ingold-2023-possible-world-passage-of-generations-astonishment-surprise]]).

## Method and register

Ingold's writing is essayistic and figural -- a frame poem, the moon, walking as a controlled fall -- rather than method-driven; the 2023 essay began as a keynote on "non-procedural methodologies" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-back-matter-references]]). His working lineage runs through phenomenology and process philosophy ([[henri-bergson]], [[michel-serres]], Merleau-Ponty), ecological and anthropological accounts of skill and place (Gibson, Myers), a pedagogy of attention (Dewey, Ranciere, Masschelein), and new-materialist theory (Barad, Manning, Harney and Moten).

## Relevance to World Machines

Ingold supplies a phenomenological counter-idiom to the WMP [[legibility-as-machine-core]] machine: his critique of knowledge-as-citadel and of "education, science and the state" as machines for the "production of ignorance" reads legibility from the position of the subject it processes (see [[not-knowing-as-capture-resistance]]), while his longitudinal ontology of becoming offers an alternative to accumulative, stratigraphic pictures of historical time (see [[longitudinal-becoming-versus-stratigraphic-time]]).
