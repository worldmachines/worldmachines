# Jane Bennett

## Biographical

American political theorist (born 1957). Professor at Johns Hopkins University. Associated with new materialism and political ecology.

## Major Work

*Vibrant Matter: A Political Ecology of Things* (Duke University Press, 2010). Argues that non-human things — electricity, food, metals, trash — have a kind of agency or "thing-power" that cannot be reduced to human discourse or social construction. Draws on Spinoza, Deleuze, and Whitman.

## Relation to Bryant

Bryant cites Bennett approvingly in the *Onto-Cartography* Introduction: "things have their power as well" (Bryant 2014, p. 3). Both are reacting against the discursivist tradition that renders physical agencies invisible. Both draw on Deleuze and a post-Marxist political concern for the efficacy of non-human actants.

The difference in framing: Bennett emphasizes *vitalism* and the quasi-agency of things (the "thing-power" that makes things more than passive matter). Bryant's approach is more systematically ontological — he builds a full machine taxonomy with precise distinctions between incorporeal/corporeal, rigid/plastic, inanimate/animate/cognitive. Bennett is more phenomenological and poetic in register.
