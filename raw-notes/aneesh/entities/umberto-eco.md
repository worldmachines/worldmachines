# Umberto Eco

**Umberto Eco** (1932–2016) was an Italian semiotician, philosopher, literary critic, and novelist. He is one of the most important theorists of signs, meaning, and the history of ideas in the 20th century.

## Key Works (Relevant to This Wiki)

- ***The Search for the Perfect Language*** (1993) — traces the history of attempts to create a universal language from antiquity through the Enlightenment, concluding that the dream was "largely abandoned" but may be revived in AI
- ***The Name of the Rose*** (1980) — novel set in a medieval library that engages themes of forbidden knowledge and the limits of encyclopedic accumulation

## The AI/Perfect Language Observation

Eco's key claim, cited in [[Universal Library in the River of Noise]]:

> "The dream of a perfect language has largely been abandoned. However, the work on artificial intelligence may represent its revival under a different name."

This is cited in the context of LLM embeddings: high-dimensional vector representations of words and concepts function as a statistical approximation of the [[Perfect Language]] — one location in a continuous space per concept, proximity as semantic relatedness.

## Intellectual Position

Eco belongs to a tradition skeptical of the perfect language dream as achievable through natural or constructed human language, but attentive to why it persists. His history of the perfect language is diagnostic: it shows *why* each era re-attempts the dream and *why* each attempt fails in characteristic ways.
