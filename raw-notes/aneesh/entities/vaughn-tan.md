# Vaughn Tan

## Biographical Sketch

Vaughn Tan is a consultant, academic, and author. He writes the *Not Knowing* series (vaughntan.org), which develops frameworks for how individuals and organizations can navigate genuine uncertainty more effectively. He runs workshops and training programs for organizations.

## Core Intellectual Contribution

### The Four Questions of Uncertainty (Not Knowing, 2023–2024)

Tan developed a practical framework for converting information about uncertain developments into actionable intelligence. The four questions:
1. What new outcomes are now possible?
2. What actions can I take in response?
3. What are the relative values of these outcomes to me?
4. What are the causal relationships?

See [[Four Questions of Uncertainty]] for full treatment.

Cedric Chin (Commoncog) adopted this framework directly in *How to Make Sense of AI* (2026), crediting Tan explicitly. The framework operationalizes [[Outcome Orientation]] for applied sensemaking contexts.

## Other Notable Work

- *The Uncertainty Mindset* (2020) — ethnographic study of culinary R&D teams; argues that elite kitchens achieve innovation through structural uncertainty (hiring for potential, open-ended briefs, regular team composition changes)
- Essays on "boring tiny tools" (BTTs) — the case for using AI tools to build small, idiosyncratic personal utilities rather than enterprise-scale applications

## Intellectual Position

Tan occupies a distinctive position: academic rigor (Columbia Business School affiliation) combined with practitioner orientation and accessible writing. His work tends to build frameworks that are useful in real organizational contexts rather than theoretical abstractions.
