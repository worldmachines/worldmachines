# Richard McElreath

Evolutionary anthropologist and Director of the Department of Human Behavior, Ecology, and Culture at the Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology (Leipzig).

## Key Work Referenced

**Statistical Rethinking: A Bayesian Course with Examples in R and Stan** (1st ed. 2015, 2nd ed. 2020).

A Bayesian statistics textbook notable for:
- Grounding statistical inference in causal reasoning and DAG construction
- Treating statistical models as scientific models, not just data processing tools
- Insisting on transparency about modeling assumptions
- The slogan: science as honest anarchy

## Key Quotation

> "There is no method for making causal models other than science. There is no method to science other than honest anarchy."

Cited by [[Aneesh Sathe]] in [[My Road to Bayesian Stats]] as the summation of why Bayesian transparency matters. "Honest anarchy" means: there is no single correct method, no authority who can hand you the right causal model — only public, contestable, revisable theorizing.

## Intellectual Position

McElreath belongs to a generation of applied Bayesians (with [[Judea Pearl]] as the causal inference theorist) who argue that:
1. Statistical analysis must be grounded in an explicit causal model (the [[Data Generating Process]])
2. That model must be testable and public — open to correction
3. Priors are not a weakness of Bayesian inference but its strength: they make background assumptions visible and auditable
