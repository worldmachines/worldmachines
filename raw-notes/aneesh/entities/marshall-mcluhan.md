# Marshall McLuhan

## Biographical

Canadian media theorist and literary critic (1911–1980). Professor at the University of Toronto. Major works: *The Gutenberg Galaxy* (1962), *Understanding Media* (1964), *The Medium is the Massage* (1967, with Quentin Fiore).

## The Central Claim

"The medium is the message." The medium — not the content it carries — shapes and controls the scale and form of human association and action. McLuhan's example: whether electric light is used for brain surgery or night baseball is "a matter of indifference." What matters is that electric light opened entirely new domains of night-time social relations — night-time labor, factory shifts, romantic strolls, baseball. The *content* of the electric light is irrelevant; its material properties transform what social life is possible.

McLuhan recovered the Latinate sense of *medium* as *medius* — "intermediary." For McLuhan, a medium is any intermediary that relates one thing to another; he extends the concept to include everything from forks to seeing eye dogs.

## Bryant's Use and Extension

Bryant takes McLuhan as a key positive source for *Onto-Cartography*. McLuhan correctly identifies:
- Media are formative of human action in ways that don't simply issue from human intentions
- The materiality of the medium — not its meaning or content — is what matters for social analysis
- Media are extensions of the human organism: writing extends the ear, the automobile extends the foot

Bryant extends McLuhan in two critical directions:
1. **Beyond sense-organ extension**: a machine functions as a medium for another machine not only when it amplifies a sense-organ, but whenever it modifies the activity or becoming of any other machine.
2. **Beyond humans**: McLuhan's account is anthropocentric — media are extensions of *man*. Bryant extends the category to all machine-to-machine structural couplings. Electric lights are media for insects and lizards; remora use sharks as media; the stoker in Kafka's *Amerika* is a medium *for the steam engine*.

The retained core of McLuhan's insight: "the materiality of the medium, its material properties and powers, substantially modify human activities and relations in ways that outpace the *content* of the medium." (Bryant 2014, p. 32)

## Relevance to This Wiki

McLuhan's "the medium is the message" operates in the wiki's [[technology-as-place-maker]] synthesis: what platforms *do* to social space is more important than what content passes through them. The infinite scroll is a structural feature with material effects — it is the message, not any particular post.

Bryant's extension of McLuhan into post-human territory opens the question: what is a platform doing as a medium for *non-human* machines? Data centers, advertising algorithms, and energy infrastructure are all media for one another in Bryant's expanded sense.
