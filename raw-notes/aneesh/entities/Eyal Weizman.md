# Eyal Weizman

**Born**: 1970, Israel
**Field**: Architecture; spatial politics; human rights
**Institutional affiliation**: Goldsmiths, University of London; founder of Forensic Architecture
**Key work in this wiki**: "Lethal Theory" (essay); *Hollow Land: Israel's Architecture of Occupation* (2007)

## Core Contribution

Weizman is the leading theorist of the **spatial dimensions of military occupation and conflict**. He documents how architecture, urbanism, and spatial practice are not neutral — they are instruments of power, control, and resistance.

*Forensic Architecture* (his research agency) uses architectural evidence — building damage, satellite imagery, spatial analysis — to reconstruct human rights violations.

## "Lethal Theory" and Walking Through Walls

The essay (unpublished at the time of Manaugh's 2010 post) documented the IDF's 2002 invasion of Nablus, in which soldiers moved:
- Horizontally through party walls
- Vertically through blasted floors and ceilings
- Using *none* of the designed circulation grammar of the urban fabric

Weizman's key insight: this is ancient, not new. The same technique was used by Paris Commune defenders, Kasbah of Algiers, Hue, Beirut, Jenin, and Nablus. What is new is its theorization as conscious military doctrine.

He also notes the IDF's explicit borrowing from critical theory and poststructuralist philosophy — soldiers reading Deleuze and Guattari on smooth vs. striated space.

## Manaugh's Use

[[Geoff Manaugh]] takes Weizman's military documentation and connects it to *Die Hard* to coin [[Nakatomi Space]] — a conceptual bridge between cinematic architecture and military spatial practice.
