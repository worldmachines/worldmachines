# James Somers

**James Somers** is an American programmer and writer. He has written for *The Atlantic* and maintains a personal blog at jsomers.net. His writing covers programming, tools, language, and intellectual life — usually through the lens of a specific object, practice, or person that most people overlook.

## Key Work (as referenced)

### Blog post on the 1913 Webster's
Referenced in [[A Good Dictionary]]: Somers wrote a post surfacing [[John McPhee]]'s *Draft No. 4* and its lesson on dictionary use, then provided installation instructions for Webster's 1913 on macOS. Those instructions were subsequently updated by Corey Ward and further updated by the source essay.

This is a rare **nested documentation chain**: McPhee → Somers → Ward → Sathe. Each step updates the previous for a new era of readers.

## Intellectual Style

Somers exemplifies what [[Small Web]] writing looks like:
- Personal domain
- Long-form, carefully researched
- Focused on tools and practices that enable better thinking
- No engagement-optimization; the audience is whoever needs the thing

He is cited as an exemplar of the "raw internet" ethos the author wants to return to.
