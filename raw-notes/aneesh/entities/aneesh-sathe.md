# Aneesh Sathe

Author of this wiki corpus. Computational biologist and data scientist; writes personal essays at [aneeshsathe.com](https://aneeshsathe.com).

## Socratic Thinking Series (2025)

A four-essay sequence developing a unified framework for thinking as place-making:

1. **[[Chatbots, Bats & Broken Oracles]]** (2025-07-05) — LLMs as Socratic partners; problems vs. questions; AI epistemics
2. **[[Thinking with Places]]** (2025-07-06) — Thinking as place-making; tools as micro-places; Socratic dialogue as cartography
3. **[[The Mind as Semi-Solid Smoke]]** (2025-07-07) — Mind as fractal insta-places; normative self-blindness; biological navigation
4. **[[Social Internet — Lost and Hungry]]** (2025-07-08) — Information hunger as biological need; signal-to-orientation ladder; Socratic internet

Core framework: [[Space and Place in Cognition]], [[Socratic Thinking]], [[Problems vs. Questions]], [[Normative Self-Blindness]]

Philosophical sources: [[Agnes Callard]] (*Open Socrates*), [[Thomas Nagel]] (*What Is It Like to Be a Bat?*), [[Yi-Fu Tuan]] (*Space and Place*)

## Intellectual Concerns

Across the essays in this corpus, recurring themes:

1. **Creative practice and habit**: how to sustain writing and intellectual work ([[On Protocols, Wagons, and Associated Acrobatics]])
2. **Ludic optimization**: finding work whose feedback cadence matches intrinsic motivation ([[Work or Play? Ludic Feedback Loops]])
3. **Data infrastructure for science**: making biotech research reproducible and scalable ([[Why Every Biotech Research Group Needs a Data Lakehouse]])
4. **Authenticity and narrative**: the essay form as resistance to commodification ([[Looking Forward to Montaigne]])
5. **AI epistemics and reliability**: the information-access cycle from lore → search → LLMs → possible regression to lore ([[My Grandfather Had Lore]])
6. **Taste as expertise**: the distinction between AI as tool (productive) and AI as advisor (dangerous) ([[On Being Good vs. Knowing Good]])
7. **Composite AI architecture**: Experts + LLM + KG as the path to [[Explainable AI]] in healthcare and beyond ([[Dancing on the Shoulders of Giants]], [[FHIR Knowledge Graphs Healthcare]])
8. **The open web and RSS**: protocols as minor gods; the personal web as village; platforms as dams ([[The Small God of the Internet]])
9. **Curiosity vs. performance**: genuine intellectual curiosity vs. ego-driven reputation-seeking ([[Better Company Than Caesar]])
10. **Books as spaces**: books as dwellings rather than information containers ([[Books Are Dwellings]])
11. **The Cloister Web and collaborative creativity**: semi-private creative rooms; authorship-as-courage in an LLM era ([[The Tortured Artist Is So Yesterday]])
