# Andrew Ng

**Andrew Ng** is an AI researcher, educator, and entrepreneur. Co-founder of Google Brain, former chief scientist at Baidu, co-founder of Coursera and deeplearning.ai.

## Data-Centric AI

Ng's most recent major intellectual contribution is the **Data-Centric AI** framework, which inverts the default assumption of ML practice:

> "Model-centric AI: improve the model, keep the data fixed. Data-centric AI: improve the data, keep the model fixed."

His practical prescriptions include tightening annotation guidelines, reducing labeling inconsistency, fixing sensor drift, and documenting data provenance. The claim: these changes typically yield more performance gain than architectural improvements.

See [[Data-Centric AI]].

## Relationship to the Data Generation Process

Data-Centric AI is a direct response to the [[Breiman's Two Cultures|two cultures problem]] — it reorients ML practice toward understanding the [[Data Generation Process]]. Tightening labels requires understanding what the label *means*; fixing sensor drift requires understanding the measurement instrument.

Aneesh Sathe cites Ng's approach as evidence that rigorous DGP analysis "often beat fancier models." See [[beyond-the-dataset|Beyond the Dataset]].
