# Geoff Manaugh

**Field**: Architectural criticism, speculative writing
**Platform**: [BLDGBLOG](https://www.bldgblog.com) — influential architecture blog since 2004
**Key work in this wiki**: "Nakatomi Space" (BLDGBLOG, January 11, 2010)

## Intellectual Style

Manaugh practices a kind of **speculative architectural criticism** that moves between film, military theory, urban planning, science fiction, and formal architecture. BLDGBLOG became influential in the 2000s–2010s for its ability to surface unexpected connections across these domains.

His approach: take a cinematic image (*Die Hard*), a military practice (IDF walking through walls), a theoretical concept ([[Eyal Weizman]]'s "Lethal Theory"), and synthesize them into a new concept — in this case [[Nakatomi Space]].

## Nakatomi Space (2010)

The essay connects:
- John McClane's non-architectural traversal of Nakatomi Plaza (*Die Hard*, 1988)
- The IDF's "walking through walls" in Nablus (2002), documented by [[Eyal Weizman]]
- The Bond/Bourne contrast (via [[Matt Jones]])
- The long historical tradition of non-grammar traversal in urban warfare

The synthesis: buildings reveal near-infinite interior topologies when the designed grammar is abandoned.
