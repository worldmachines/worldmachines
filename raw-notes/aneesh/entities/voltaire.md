# Voltaire

## Biographical Sketch

**Born**: François-Marie Arouet, 1694, Paris  
**Died**: 1778, Paris  

French writer, philosopher, playwright, and entrepreneur. Known for *Candide*, *Zadig*, *Letters Concerning the English Nation*, and polemical essays on religious tolerance and legal reform. Self-exiled to Ferney (French-Swiss border) from 1758.

## Role in the Modernity Machine

Per [[Ivo Velitchkov]], Voltaire was a key engineer of the [[Modernity Machine]], transforming two class relationships:

### Monarch ↔ Technocracy: Pet → Celebrity
Previous model: the intellectual as a subservient court pet. Voltaire's transformation: the intellectual as a **negotiated celebrity** — financially independent, operating outside patronage while exploiting it strategically.

Method: Lottery arbitrage (1729, ~500,000 livres with Charles Marie de la Condamine), share speculation, business investments, money lending. Financial independence meant he couldn't be silenced or starved.

He "became a creditor to the ruling class" — structurally parallel to Fugger's transformation of oligarchs.

### Epistemic Legibility (State → Public)
While the state was making subjects legible to itself, Voltaire inverted this:
- **Calas affair**: campaigned to expose secret French legal proceedings through direct requests and fictionalized factual narratives
- **Newton book**: made cutting-edge science legible to a wider public — starting a tradition that dominates nonfiction today
- **Celebrity as legibility**: If celebrity = "someone whose private life, personal opinions, and physical likeness are consumed as commodity by a broad public," Voltaire was the first European celebrity (after Erasmus and Luther in weaker versions of the concept)

## The Ferney Watchmaking Enterprise

At age 76, Voltaire founded a watchmaking social enterprise/business incubator at Ferney, serving Genevan *natifs* (artisans with limited rights in Geneva). See [[Ferney Watchmaking Enterprise]] for full detail.

**Quick facts**:
- Craftsmen: 40 (1770) → 600 (1773)
- Revenue: 450K → 600K livres/year at peak (1776)
- Markets: Spain, France, Russia, Turkey, Holland, Italy, Algeria, Tunisia
- Roles Voltaire played: manager, financier, virtual bank manager, sponsor, builder, buyer, international sales manager

## Contribution to the Divergence Machine

- ***Candide*** (1759): Ruthless parody of Leibniz's "best of all possible worlds" optimism — ridiculing prefigured moral ideology, shifting progress from theological certainty to evolving argument grounded in messy reality
- Tolerance campaigns: early steps in creating civilizational space for plurality
- "Attacked dogma with absurdity" rather than counter-dogma

## Moral Complexity (Pattern of Position Changes)

Voltaire's principled stances were often instrumentalized:
- **Turkey**: Previously supported Catherine the Great's military operations against Turkey; reversed when Turkey became a profitable market
- **Taxation**: Obtained tax exemptions for watchmakers; later proposed progressive taxation for political advantage
- **Religion**: Performed public Easter communion for social access while privately mocking religion; wrote letters to churchmen calling himself "true Catholic" to secure Académie Française membership
- **Prussia**: Congratulated Frederick the Great on a treaty that abandoned France; when scandal erupted, offered himself as a diplomatic spy; after falling from favor, forged letters to his niece to dramatize his side
