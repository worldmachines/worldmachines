# Tarn Adams

**Tarn Adams** (also known as "Toady One") is the primary developer of [[Dwarf Fortress]], which he has built alongside his brother [[Zach Adams]] since the early 2000s.

## Biography Relevant to DF

- Grew up making games with Zach as children
- Built DF without any commercial aim — the goal was simulation depth
- Completed a PhD in mathematics during DF's early development
- Sustained financially by donations from the community for years before going full-time
- Worked with publisher Kitfox Games to release DF on Steam; the game became a commercial success — "overnight millionaires — that night was 20 years long"
- Estimated DF at ~44% complete; this percentage likely has not changed as new ideas continually expand the target

## Design Philosophy

Tarn's stated goal: simulate enough that the game can "tell great stories." His approach is [[Simulation as World-Building]] — build the substrate rules faithfully, let stories emerge. No win condition, depth over accessibility.

See [[Emergent Complexity]] for the consequences of this philosophy.

## Legacy

- DF is in MoMA's permanent collection
- The acquisition changed how MoMA approaches software preservation
- DF is considered by many to be the most complex game ever created
- Community contributions (art, music, utility software) eventually became part of the official team

## Relationship with Zach Adams

Tarn and [[Zach Adams]] are brothers who began making games in childhood. Zach handles the business and community side; Tarn handles the code. The collaboration is lifelong and personal.
