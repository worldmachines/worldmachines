# Agnes Callard

**Agnes Callard** is a philosopher at the University of Chicago, working primarily in ethics, philosophy of action, and the philosophy of Socrates.

## Key Works

- *Aspiration: The Agency of Becoming* (2018) — on how agents can rationally pursue values they don't yet hold
- *Open Socrates* (2024) — revives Socratic dialogue as a live philosophical practice; distinguishes Socratic **questions** from ordinary **problems**

## The Problems/Questions Distinction

In *Open Socrates*, Callard draws a sharp distinction between:
- **Problems**: tractable obstacles with established solution methods; they disappear when solved
- **Questions** (Socratic): open quests whose answers enrich rather than close; the horizon recedes as you approach

This distinction is the conceptual foundation for Aneesh Sathe's extension in [[problems-are-places-questions-are-spaces|Problems are Places, Questions are Spaces]], where he maps it onto the spatial philosophy of places and spaces.

See [[Problems vs Questions]].

## Philosophical Position

Callard is a practitioner-revivalist: she argues that Socratic questioning is not a historical artifact but a live philosophical method, and she conducts Socratic dialogues publicly and in print. She is skeptical of the "pre-answered question" — questions we hold as settled without genuine examination.

## Relationship to Epistemology

The Q&P distinction has implications beyond philosophy: it describes two distinct epistemic postures toward any domain of knowledge. Scientists who ask Socratic questions about their field behave differently from those who solve problems within it.

## Normative Self-Blindness

In *Open Socrates*, Callard coins the concept of **normative self-blindness**: the built-in cognitive refusal to examine the beliefs on which we currently stand. We cannot saw off the branch we stand on, making pre-answered questions invisible and Socratic inquiry initially threatening.

See [[Normative Self-Blindness]].
