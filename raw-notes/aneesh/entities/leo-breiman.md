# Leo Breiman

**Leo Breiman** (1928–2005) was an American statistician and professor at UC Berkeley, known for foundational contributions to statistical machine learning and for a prescient diagnosis of problems in statistical culture.

## Key Contributions

- **CART (Classification and Regression Trees)**: co-developed the decision tree algorithm that became foundational to ensemble methods
- **Random Forest**: invented the random forest algorithm, one of the most widely used ML methods
- **Bagging (Bootstrap Aggregation)**: developed this ensemble technique

## "Statistical Modeling: The Two Cultures" (2001)

Breiman's most influential conceptual contribution: a paper in *Statistical Science* diagnosing a fundamental split between the **data modeling culture** (which fits interpretable models and cares about mechanisms) and the **algorithmic modeling culture** (which optimizes predictive accuracy without mechanistic assumptions).

His warning: analysts chasing algorithmic accuracy were skipping the question of how data were generated — a shortcut that compromised the integrity of their conclusions regardless of model performance metrics.

See [[Breiman's Two Cultures]].

## Relevance to Trustworthy Data Science

Aneesh Sathe cites Breiman's Two Cultures as the diagnostic framing for why the pillars of trustworthy data science ([[Data Generation Process]]) get skipped in practice. The tooling has gotten worse (more one-click automation) while Breiman's underlying critique has gotten more urgent.
