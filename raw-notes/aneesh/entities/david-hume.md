# David Hume (1711–1776)

**Location**: Edinburgh, Scotland
**Key works**: *A Treatise of Human Nature* (1739–40); *An Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding* (1748)

## Biographical Context

- The "infidel" in *The Infidel and the Professor*
- Friend and intellectual collaborator of [[adam-smith]] — key figures of the Scottish Enlightenment
- Managed to hold an institutional position while remaining effectively an undeclared agnostic — the more diplomatic Smith ("the Professor") was entirely undeclared
- By Hume's time, "it wasn't even a big deal to be an infidel" — a marker of how far the Spinoza tradition had traveled in two generations

## Core Philosophy

Hume was the most radical empiricist of the Scottish Enlightenment. His key moves:
- **Against causation**: we never *observe* cause-and-effect; we only observe constant conjunction. Causation is a habit of mind, not a feature of reality.
- **Phenomenological minimalism**: what is real is what can be directly experienced. Grand theoretical frameworks (clockwork universes, theological orders) are fictions imposed on the log files of experience.
- **The bundle theory of self**: there is no continuous "self" — only bundles of perceptions. The self is another habit of mind.

## Rao's Reading

Hume is an intellectual descendant of [[baruch-spinoza]], with [[gottfried-wilhelm-leibniz]] (as philosopher) already faded into irrelevance by Hume's time. He represents a radical continuation of the naturalist-empiricist tendency:

> "Hume, famously, went much farther than most people before or since, setting aside complex theories of causation in favor of near-pure phenomenology. In some ways, he's the original philosopher of the AI age, offering the first of what has now become an endless series of 'bitter lessons' delivered by the Argument of Progress."

The "log files of reality" framing: Hume was only willing to treat observed reality as reality. "Everything else was made up human conceits." The Kronecker parallel: "God created the integers, everything else is the work of man."

When Newton (1643–1727) had proposed his theories, the temptation of the time was to believe in a deterministic clockwork universe governed by immutable divine laws. Hume resisted this temptation in a way almost no one else did.

## Sathe's Reading: "The First Philosopher of the AI Age"

From [[the-end-of-identity-ai-plasticity-divergence-machine]]:

Hume went further than any contemporary in "abandoning complex theories of causation in favor of near-pure phenomenology." While others were tempted to build clockwork models, Hume was "only willing to treat the log files of reality as real."

**The AI parallel**: AI systems operate exactly like Hume's epistemology. They:
- Don't care about grand narratives or identity
- Process prompts — "the raw log files of our know-how"
- Extract pattern from data without positing underlying causes or meanings

This makes Hume not just historically interesting but **structurally predictive** of how AI cognition works.

## The Irony

Hume's stripping of metaphysics from experience was philosophically liberating. The same operation performed by AI on cognitive labor is *existentially threatening* — because it strips the last layer of the knowledge worker's legible identity.

## The Hume-Smith Pairing

Where Spinoza and Leibniz were in a partially adversarial relationship, Hume and Smith were "collaborating allies who influenced each other in their campaigns on different parts of the frontier of expanding reality."

Together, they represent a "bad-cop-good-cop assault on traditional moral and natural philosophy" — the beginning of the intellectual iconoclasm that peaks with Darwin.

## Darwin's Completion of the Hume Trajectory

Darwin's *real* accomplishment was "assaulting the *secular* philosophical foundations of the modernity machine" — by making divergence and variety a load-bearing feature of how reality itself operates, Darwin "put an end to the fetish for secular canonicity that marks world views before him." Literalist religion was "already down for the count by the time Hume was done with it."

## The AI Connection (Rao's Frame)

Rao's claim that Hume is "the original philosopher of the AI age" connects to the contemporary "bitter lessons" of machine learning: simple empirical models trained on large amounts of data repeatedly outperform models built on human-specified rules and theories. This is the Humean lesson applied to cognition itself.
