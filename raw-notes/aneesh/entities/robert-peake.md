# Robert Peake

Contemporary poet and essayist. Participated in the [[Summer of Protocols]] (SoP) initiative.

## Key Contribution in This Corpus

Talk: **"The Infinite Game of Poetry - Protocols for Living, Listening, and Transcending the Rules"** (Summer of Protocols town hall)

The talk applies [[James Carse]]'s infinite game framework to the practice of poetry. Peake derives a protocol for creative life:

1. Being a poet = observing change in self: even when not writing, you notice your inner environment, outer environment, and what you've read.
2. When you start to write, the change in self produces the writing (synthesis).
3. The writing becomes part of the change in self.
4. Sum of all noticing and synthesis = life's work.
5. The self is constructed continuously through all iterations of work.

He also provides two equations for doing life's work and building the self (not reproduced in the source essay — watch the talk).

## Significance

Peake's protocol formalizes [[Self-Construction Through Writing]] and provides the theoretical backbone for [[On Protocols, Wagons, and Associated Acrobatics]]. His insight that the poet is *always* working — even when not producing — dissolves the guilt of not writing and reframes "being a writer" as a perceptual mode rather than a production quota.
