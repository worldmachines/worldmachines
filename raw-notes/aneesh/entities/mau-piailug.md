# Pius "Mau" Piailug (c. 1932–2010)

Satawalese master navigator; *palu* in the traditional ranking of navigational authority. Born on Satawal in Yap State, Federated States of Micronesia, and trained from the age of four by his grandfather in the memorised star compass, wave-pattern recognition, cloud reading, and dead-reckoning that constitute long-distance Pacific wayfinding. By the mid-twentieth century, Piailug was among the few remaining practitioners of the craft anywhere in the Pacific, and arguably the last whose knowledge was complete enough to navigate an uninstrumented voyage over open ocean on a new route.

## The 1976 Hōkūleʻa Voyage

On 1 May 1976, Piailug departed Honolua Bay, Maui, on board the Hōkūleʻa — a replica double-hulled Polynesian voyaging canoe constructed by the Polynesian Voyaging Society. The canoe reached Papeete, Tahiti, on 4 June 1976, a passage of approximately 2,300 nautical miles in 34 days, navigated without instruments. The voyage was a controlled demonstration that traditional Polynesian navigation was capable of the inter-island transits attested in Pacific settlement history, and an operational refutation of the accidental-drift theory of Polynesian colonisation (Thor Heyerdahl's *Kon-Tiki* hypothesis).

Piailug carried the knowledge across cultures. Long-distance non-instrument navigation had been lost in Hawai'i centuries before European contact and preserved in only a few small Micronesian islands, including Satawal. Without his willingness to teach a practice his tradition held to be esoteric, the revival would have been impossible.

## The Curriculum

Piailug trained Nainoa Thompson and several other Hawaiian and Pacific navigators, who in turn built the training curriculum of the Polynesian Voyaging Society. The society now operates a structured apprenticeship, recognised by the Hawai'i Department of Education, that has produced a generation of practitioners. The 2013–2017 Mālama Honua worldwide voyage of Hōkūleʻa and Hikianalia was navigated entirely by this generation.

Piailug died on Satawal on 12 July 2010, aged about 78. Thompson is still sailing.
