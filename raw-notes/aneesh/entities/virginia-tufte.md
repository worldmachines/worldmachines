# Virginia Tufte

**Virginia Frances Tufte** (1922–2020) was an American linguist and professor at the University of Southern California. She is the author of *Artful Sentences: Syntax as Style* (2006) and the mother of data visualization theorist Edward Tufte.

The footnote in [[Divine Documentation]] notes: "Those dastardly Tuftes are always making me learn some kind of grammar" — a reference to both Virginia (sentence syntax) and Edward (visual syntax of data).

## Key Work (as referenced)

### Artful Sentences: Syntax as Style (2006)
A 270-page analysis of individual sentences dissected to reveal their mechanical and stylistic operations. It is a reference grammar for writers — highly sophisticated, assuming fluency with basic composition.

Referenced in [[Divine Documentation]] as immediately too advanced: "I was lost by page 5. The book is, temporarily, in my anti-library."

## Significance for [[Staged Learning]]

Virginia Tufte's book is the terminal node in the reading sequence — the book you arrive at after [[William Zinsser]] and [[Strunk and White]]. Its placement in the anti-library is not a dismissal but a deferral: the reader must first build the prerequisite foundation.

This is the clearest illustration in these essays of the [[Anti-Library]] concept: a book that marks the boundary of current capability rather than current interest.
