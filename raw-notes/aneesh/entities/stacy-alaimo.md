# Stacy Alaimo

American scholar in feminist environmental humanities. Associated with new materialisms and posthumanist approaches to ecology and the body. Key work: *Bodily Natures: Science, Environment, and the Material Self* (2010).

## Trans-Corporeality

Alaimo's central concept: *trans-corporeality* — the idea that human (and all) bodies are not bounded, self-contained entities but are always already permeated by their material environment. The "trans" in trans-corporeality indicates movement across different sites — bodies are constituted through their material entanglement with what surrounds them.

"Imagining human corporeality [and I would argue, *all* corporeality] as trans-corporeality, in which the human is always intermeshed with the more-than-human world, underlines the extent to which the substance of the human is ultimately inseparable from 'the environment.' It makes it difficult to pose nature as mere background . . . for the exploits of the political economy, for the exploits of science, since 'nature' is always as close as one's own skin — perhaps even closer. Indeed, thinking across bodies may catalyze the recognition that the environment, which is too often imagined as inert, empty space or as a resource for human use, is, in fact, a world of fleshy beings with their own needs, claims, and actions." (Alaimo 2010: 2, cited *Onto-Cartography* pp. 48–49)

## Relation to Bryant

[[levi-bryant]] cites Alaimo's trans-corporeality as a key concept for understanding how machines are constituted through the flows that pass through them — flows that are themselves machines with their own powers. Trans-corporeality captures the *reciprocal determination* between a machine and the machines that flow through it: neither the body nor its environment is fixed; both are constituted through their entanglement.

Bryant uses trans-corporeality to support his claim that *there is no structureless matter* — all matter is formatted by its own operations — and that machines are therefore not sovereign over the flows that pass through them. Jane Bennett's account of omega-3 fatty acids modifying behavioral dispositions (from *Vibrant Matter*) is cited alongside Alaimo as evidence for this mutual modification.

The concept also supports the political stakes of [[alien-phenomenology]]: if bodies are always already trans-corporeal, then the political divide between human subjects and their material environment collapses. Environmental politics is not just about protecting an external "nature" but about the constitution of embodied subjects themselves.
