# Quentin Meillassoux

## Biographical

French philosopher (born 1967). Student of Alain Badiou. Associate professor at the University of Paris 1 (Panthéon-Sorbonne). One of the four original figures associated with Speculative Realism following the 2007 Goldsmiths workshop.

## Major Works

- *After Finitude: An Essay on the Necessity of Contingency* (Seuil, 2006; English trans. Continuum, 2008) — the primary text. Introduces the concept of [[correlationism]] and argues for "speculative materialism": contingency is the one thing that is necessary; everything else (including the laws of nature) is absolutely contingent.
- *The Number and the Siren* (2011) — a speculative reading of Mallarmé's *Un Coup de Dés*.

## Key Concepts

- **[[Correlationism]]**: the post-Kantian assumption that we can only think the correlation between thought and being, never being in itself. Meillassoux argues this leads to absurdities when confronted with "ancestral" statements about events prior to human experience.
- **Factiality / Contingency**: the one thing we can think speculatively — that contingency itself is necessary. Nothing else is.
- **The Great Outdoors**: his term for being as it exists independently of human access.

## Relation to Bryant

Bryant adopts Meillassoux's term "correlationism" as a diagnostic label for the philosophical error underlying discursive materialism. Both are anti-correlationist. They differ: Meillassoux pursues the correlationism-break through mathematics (following Badiou); Bryant pursues it through a Deleuzian ontology of machines.
