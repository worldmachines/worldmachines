# Dwarf Fortress

**Dwarf Fortress** (DF) is a free, open-ended simulation game developed by [[Tarn Adams]] and [[Zach Adams]] under Bay 12 Games. It is widely regarded as the most complex game ever created and has been in continuous development since the early 2000s.

## What It Is

DF is a complete world simulation. A generated world includes:
- Geological history, biomes, civilizations
- Complete ecology (individual creatures with simulated body parts)
- Social dynamics including individual dwarf personalities, relationships, and needs
- Full fluid, temperature, and material simulation

The primary play mode is a **fortress mode**: guide seven dwarves establishing a home in a hostile wilderness. The simulation runs underneath every decision, producing unexpected emergent behaviors.

There is **no win condition**. The game's motto: "Losing is FUN!" See [[Failure as Generativity]].

## Cultural Significance

- In **MoMA's permanent collection** (Museum of Modern Art); its acquisition changed how MoMA approaches software preservation
- Community has produced extensive lore, webcomics, and fan fiction from emergent stories (Boatmurdered, Oilfurnace)
- A four-part documentary by No Clip covers the game's development history

## Technical Achievement

Tarn Adams built DF largely alone, while:
- Completing a PhD in mathematics
- Sustained by community donations
- Without a commercial roadmap or external funding

Estimated at ~44% complete by Tarn's own reckoning; the target expands as new ideas accumulate.

## The Steam Release

A collaboration with Kitfox Games brought DF to Steam with improved graphics and interface. The brothers became "overnight millionaires — that night was 20 years long."

## Design Philosophy

See [[Simulation as World-Building]] and [[Emergent Complexity]] for the core principles. The goal was always story-generation through simulation depth, not winning.

## Community-Built Infrastructure

Fans contributed:
- Art and tileset mods
- Music
- Third-party utility software (DFHack, Dwarf Therapist)
- Many are now full-time team members
