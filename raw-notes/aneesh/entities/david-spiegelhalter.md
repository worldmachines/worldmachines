# David Spiegelhalter

## Identity

British statistician, Emeritus Professor of the Public Understanding of Risk at the University of Cambridge. Fellow of the Royal Society. Prominent science communicator and author of *The Art of Statistics* (2019).

Known for making statistics legible to non-specialists while maintaining philosophical rigor about what statistical claims can and cannot establish.

## Relevant Work

### "Probability" in *Nature*

Cited in [[Jan. 3, 2025]]. Argues that numerical probability is not an objective property of the world but a construction from judgment and assumption. The key contribution:

- Outside statistical mechanics and Mendelian genetics, there is no "true" probability — only expressions of uncertainty
- Saying "the probability" implies an objectivity that usually doesn't exist; "a probability" would be more honest
- **De Finetti's exchangeability theorem** provides the practical escape: subjective belief + exchangeability assumption → justified behavior as if objective chances exist

See [[Probability as Epistemology]] for the full concept.
