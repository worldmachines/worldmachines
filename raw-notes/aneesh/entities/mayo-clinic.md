# Mayo Clinic

One of the largest and most respected nonprofit academic medical centers in the United States, known for integrated clinical care, research, and education. Founded in Rochester, Minnesota in 1864.

## Role in This Corpus

Sathe (see [[Dancing on the Shoulders of Giants]], [[FHIR Knowledge Graphs Healthcare]]) cites Mayo Clinic as a leading institution treating [[Knowledge Graphs]] as the path forward for clinical AI. In the context of the [[Composite AI Architecture (Experts + LLM + KG)]] argument, Mayo represents evidence that the most sophisticated healthcare institutions have independently arrived at the same architectural conclusion.

The significance: Mayo Clinic's endorsement is not merely technical but institutional. If the most prestigious medical institution in the US is investing in KG-based AI infrastructure, this validates the claim that basic [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation]] over flat document stores is insufficient for clinical use cases.

## Connection to Wright's Law Exception

Mayo Clinic, despite its size and resources, still faces the [[Wright's Law]] constraint: institutional learning is slow to diffuse to the broader healthcare system. The Mayo Platform initiative represents an attempt to create shared data infrastructure that could accelerate this diffusion — exactly the mechanism the [[Composite AI Architecture (Experts + LLM + KG)]] proposes.
