# Eric Chaisson

## Overview

Eric Chaisson is a cosmologist associated with Harvard's Wright Center for Science Education. He is the principal architect of the **cosmic evolution** framework — a unified account of change from the Big Bang to the present — and the inventor of **[[Energy Rate Density]]** as a cross-domain complexity metric.

## Core Contributions

### Cosmic Evolution Framework
Proposes that physical, biological, and cultural evolution are subsets of a single cosmic-evolutionary narrative driven by energy flow. The framework encompasses seven phases (particulate, galactic, stellar, planetary, chemical, biological, cultural) and three eras (Radiation, Matter, Life).

### Energy Rate Density
The quantitative metric that operationalizes complexity across domains: free energy flow per unit mass (erg/s/g). Key finding: energy rate density rises monotonically from galaxies (~0.5) through stars (~2), planets (~75), plants (~900), animals (~20,000), brains (~150,000), to modern society (~500,000+). This is the most rigorous attempt to quantify the claim that "complexity has increased over time."

### The Life Era Thesis
The provocative claim that technologically intelligent life is now crossing a threshold comparable to the transition from the Radiation Era to the Matter Era — life is becoming a cosmological actor, able to manipulate matter as matter once overwhelmed radiation.

## Relation to Rao

Chaisson and [[venkatesh-rao]] are working on parallel but non-overlapping questions:
- Chaisson: what *is* complexity, measured thermodynamically?
- Rao: what *is* liveness, constituted by autonomy and capture resistance?

[[Energy Rate Density]] may be necessary but not sufficient for [[Liveness]]: high energy throughput is a thermodynamic condition of life, but Rao's concept adds the autonomy dimension — the capacity to refuse instrumentalization.
