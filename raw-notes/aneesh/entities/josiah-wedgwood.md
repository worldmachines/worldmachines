# Josiah Wedgwood

## Biographical Sketch

**Born**: 1730, Burslem, Staffordshire, England  
**Died**: 1795

English potter who transformed a craft into an industry. Known as "Potter to Her Majesty" (Queen Charlotte). Grandfather of Charles Darwin. Member of the [[Lunar Society]] of Birmingham.

## Role in the Modernity Machine

Per [[Ivo Velitchkov]]'s "Three Engineers of Modernity," Wedgwood was a key engineer of the [[Modernity Machine]], transforming multiple class relationships:

### Monarch-Technocracy → Oligarchy-Technocracy Cascade
By securing Queen Charlotte's patronage ("Queen's ware") and Catherine the Great's patronage (944-piece dinner service), Wedgwood converted royal taste into a market signal:
- Monarch uses goods → signals prestige
- Nobles imitate → wealthy merchants follow → mass consumption
- "Cascade of desire" through class hierarchy
- Wedgwood **boosted consumer society** by making aspirational taste legible and scalable

### Technocracy-Worker: Division of Labor
Introduced division of labor to pottery: previously one potter made a pot start-to-finish; now specialized, replaceable workers. Worker time made legible through timetables and a primitive clock-in system.

## Industrial Legibility Innovations

- **Pyrometer**: One of the first temperature measurement devices; measured kiln shrinkage of clay gauge as proxy for temperature. Made the previously illegible kiln controllable.
- **Commonplace books**: Meticulous experiment records (coded to protect findings)
- **Illustrated catalogues**: First use — making product offering visually legible to buyers
- **Cost accounting**: Primitive system revealing that fixed costs can be reduced by high volume production — scale logic
- **Timetables and clock-in systems**: Workers' time rendered legible

## Contribution to the Divergence Machine

Unlike Fugger, Wedgwood also made early "commits" to the [[divergence-machine]]:
- **Relentless experimentation as end in itself**: Not just for commercial gain — signals shift from Progress as material plenty to Progress as growth of instrumental knowledge
- **Lunar Society**: Pluralist, multi-disciplinary gathering (Erasmus Darwin, James Watt, Joseph Priestley, etc.) for sharing "first hints of discoveries, current observations, mutual collision of ideas"

## Moral Complexity

Known as an abolitionist — created the famous Wedgwood anti-slavery medallion ("Am I Not a Man and a Brother?"). But his business depended on sugar production, which was driven by the slave trade. The contradiction between his public abolitionism and economic complicity in the slavery system was stark.
