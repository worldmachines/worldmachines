# Hank Green

## Biographical

Hank Green is an American YouTube creator, entrepreneur, and science communicator. Known as one half of the VlogBrothers (with John Green), co-founder of VidCon, SciShow, and Complexly. Publishes extensively on science, culture, and social media dynamics.

## Key Idea Referenced

In a video referenced (but not titled) in [[Social Internet — Lost and Hungry]], Hank Green argues:

> "We aren't addicted to content, but are instead starving for information."

The distinction:
- **Addiction**: Compulsive return to a pleasurable stimulus, despite known harm.
- **Starvation**: Unfulfilled biological need driving compulsive seeking behavior.

The thesis reframes the social media problem: the issue is not that platforms are too pleasurable but that they deliver *insufficient information* — enough signal to trigger orientation-seeking, not enough to satisfy it.

## Sathe's Extension

Sathe takes Green's intuition and grounds it in the [[Signal-to-Orientation Ladder]] framework:
- Signal → Information → Orientation → **Biology** (evolutionary survival need for accurate mental maps)
- Social platforms provide signal without completing the ladder to genuine orientation.
- Users are not addicted; they are lost and hungry.

## Position in the Social Media Discourse

Green's diagnosis is more charitable to users than addiction framing:
- Addiction framing implies weakness or pathology.
- Starvation framing implies a reasonable organism responding to unreasonable conditions.

It also points to a different solution:
- Addiction solution: reduce exposure, build willpower, detox.
- Starvation solution: find genuine nourishment — information-shaped content, Socratic engagement.
