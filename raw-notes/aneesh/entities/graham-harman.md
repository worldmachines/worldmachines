# Graham Harman

## Biographical

American philosopher (born 1968). Grew up in Iowa; longtime professor at the American University in Cairo; now at SCI-Arc (Southern California Institute of Architecture). One of the original four figures of Speculative Realism (2007 Goldsmiths workshop).

## Major Works

- *Tool-Being: Heidegger and the Metaphysics of Objects* (2002) — reads Heidegger's tool-analysis as pointing toward an object-oriented metaphysics.
- *Guerrilla Metaphysics* (2005)
- *Prince of Networks: Bruno Latour and Metaphysics* (2009)
- *The Quadruple Object* (2011)
- *Object-Oriented Ontology: A New Theory of Everything* (2018)

## Key Concepts

- **Withdrawal**: all objects withdraw from all relations, including relations with other objects. No entity is ever fully present to another. Objects have both "real" qualities (independent of relation) and "sensual" qualities (how they appear in relation).
- **OOP/OOO**: Object-Oriented Philosophy / Object-Oriented Ontology — the position that objects of all kinds have equal ontological status and irreducible depth.
- **Dormant objects**: objects that continue to exist but are not currently in active relation — adopted by Bryant in *Onto-Cartography* for "sleeping" incorporeal machines like the *Dead Sea Scrolls*.

## Relation to Bryant

Harman is Bryant's closest ally and significant foil within OOO. Both share the flat ontology and anti-correlationism. They diverge on *withdrawal*: Harman insists objects withdraw from all relation, making relations always external and indirect. Bryant's machines are, by contrast, fundamentally relational — they operate through structural couplings, and their operations are not sealed off from relations. This disagreement has significant consequences: for Harman, the interior of an object is always inaccessible; for Bryant, the operations of machines can be investigated through their structural couplings and outputs.

Bryant adopts Harman's "dormant objects" concept but re-frames it within the machine ontology as "dormant incorporeal machines."
