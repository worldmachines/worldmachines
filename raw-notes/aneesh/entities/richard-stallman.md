# Richard Stallman

**Richard Matthew Stallman** (b. 1953) is an American software freedom activist and programmer. He launched the GNU Project in 1983 and founded the Free Software Foundation in 1985. His work on the GPL (GNU General Public License) established the copyleft model.

## Key Contribution (as referenced here)

### Definition of Hacking
Stallman's definition, referenced in [[Hack Hacky Hacker]]:
> "Playfully doing something difficult, whether useful or not, that is hacking."

This definition is notable for:
1. Centering **play** as essential (not incidental) to hacking
2. Including **difficulty** as a requirement — trivial things aren't hacks
3. Rejecting **utility** as a criterion — the "whether useful or not" clause

The author endorses the first two elements but explicitly rejects the third. The "or not" clause conflicts with the [[Hacky Mindset]]'s insistence that hackiness should at minimum serve a prank — purposeless play is the tree falling in a forest.

## Intellectual Position on Hacking

Stallman's broader philosophy treats hacking as a way of life — a refusal to accept "This is how things work" as a final answer. This aligns with the [[Nakatomi Tunnel]] approach: systems have gaps, and exploring them is virtuous.

## Divergence from Source Essay

Stallman's own life is arguably more **hedgehog** than fox (see [[Fox-Hedgehog Distinction]]): decades of deep, narrow commitment to software freedom over all other concerns. This is interesting given that his definition of hacking is used to anchor a fox-friendly argument.
