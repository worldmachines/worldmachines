# Bertrand Russell

**Born**: 1872, Trellech, Wales
**Died**: 1970
**Nobel Prize**: Literature, 1950
**Key works**: *Principia Mathematica* (1910–13, with Whitehead), *The Problems of Philosophy* (1912), *The Conquest of Happiness* (1930), *A History of Western Philosophy* (1945)

## Significance

Russell is one of the founders of analytic philosophy and mathematical logic. He is unusual among major philosophers in combining foundational formal work with accessible popular writing on ethics, education, and the good life. In this wiki, he appears primarily through the latter strand.

## The Conquest of Happiness (1930)

Russell's popular guide to escaping misery and achieving happiness, notable for its empirical, non-moralistic approach. He treats unhappiness as a problem to be solved by examining its causes and addressing them — a pragmatic approach that anticipates cognitive behavioral frameworks.

The key passage cited in [[Better Company Than Caesar]]:

> "The secret of happiness is this: let your interests be as wide as possible, and let your reactions to the things and persons that interest you be as far as possible friendly rather than hostile."

**Width of interest** as a structural protection against the "bruising boundaries of the ego" — the more subjects you can dissolve into, the less the ego's suffering dominates.

## Intellectual Lineage in This Wiki

[[Maria Popova]] wrote an influential essay on Russell's *Conquest of Happiness*, introducing the concept of **"unselfing"** as the mechanism by which wide curiosity produces happiness. She is the mediating voice through which Sathe encounters Russell in [[Better Company Than Caesar]].
