# Florian Lohse

## Overview

Florian Lohse writes the *Notefields* Substack — "public record of throwing thoughts at walls." He is a German writer who participates in [[venkatesh-rao]]'s Contraptions Book Club, reading books on history with a mechanistic, engineering eye.

His writing style: personal, essayistic, beginning from concrete biographical situations (a layover in Singapore, a book club) and expanding toward civilizational-scale claims, then retreating to the personal again. Self-consciously provincial — he describes himself as a "Western bumpkin" in Singapore.

## Intellectual Contributions

### The Notes in the Margins Figure
Lohse's distinctive contribution to the history machine concept: the future is already present, "barely recognized if not for some dull notes in the margins." The court official who documented daily life in Majapahit and was ignored at publication is the model for how paradigm-level developments are documented before they are recognized as such.

### Provincial Self-Positioning
Lohse consistently uses his own provincial position — German, unfamiliar with Southeast Asia, sleeping in tents in Australia — as an epistemically productive stance. The outsider's bewilderment at a cosmopolitan hub (Singapore airport, Majapahit port) is not naivety but a form of comparative perception.

### The Shitty Castle Pattern (from [[Shitty Castles]], 2026)
Lohse's concept for elaborate constructions built by people who know they won't hold. Three cases: Leibniz's Monad theory (pragmatic defense against Spinoza's ideas), Château de Chambord (nostalgic power demonstration after gunpowder made such walls obsolete), OpenAI's Jony Ive acquisition (strategic signal to investors). The pattern: **functional → performative → ornamental → ridicule**.

Notable: Lohse offers a *more charitable* reading of [[gottfried-wilhelm-leibniz]] than [[venkatesh-rao]]'s "tragic reactionary genius" framing — Leibniz as strategic pragmatist rather than deluded optimist.

Self-implication: Lohse admits having written "fake moats" in his own essays.

### Panglossian Optimism Critique (from [[The Best of All Possible Worlds]], 2026)
A contribution to Rao's 2026 Book Club reading of *Candide*. Three-scene fable dramatizing the tension between zoom-out techno-optimism (everything is a stepping stone) and local emplaced care (the thicket gardener). Explicitly credits [[Aneesh Sathe]]'s essay "The Kernel and the Arc" for the thicket concept. References Gould & Lewontin's "Spandrels of San Marco" as the structural parallel to techno-optimism.

## Intellectual Position

Lohse tends toward *charitable complexity* over moral clarity. His essays typically:
1. Present a phenomenon that seems like hypocrisy or failure
2. Complicate the easy judgment by finding pragmatic logic within it
3. Turn the complication on himself
4. Leave the tension unresolved

This is structurally similar to [[Kulturkritik]] as Kempf describes it — not a cure but a regulative mode.
