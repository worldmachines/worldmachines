# Venkatesh Rao

## Identity

Author of the Contraptions newsletter (formerly Ribbonfarm). Self-described as a "lapsed and fallen technocrat" of Indian origin, technologically modern in a recognizably South Asian (specifically Madhva Brahmin) way. His writing applies engineering and mechanistic frameworks to historical, cultural, and philosophical questions.

## Intellectual Style

- **Machinic idiom**: Deliberately frames analysis in engineering terms — "world machines," "turning on," "moving parts," "compression algorithms." The goal is to model civilizational dynamics as systems, not narratives.
- **Horizontal history**: Reading history by time-boxing rather than following vertical identity streams. Cross-cultural synchronic comparison within a period.
- **Longue durée with engineering flavor**: 400-year arcs, succession of world machines, phase transitions rather than ruptures.
- **BDFL curatorial posture**: In the book club context, he acts as Benevolent Dictator For Life — setting interpretive frames, not just reading lists.

### Configurancy

Proposed as a new ontological primitive: "the ongoing, relational, temporally unfolding process through which agents and worlds co-emerge." Non-teleological; contrasted with Heidegger's Care. Sathe uses this as the engine of [[Place-Making as Epistemic Act|place-making]].
→ See [[Configurancy]]

### The Divergence Machine

A world-systems framework: the modernity machine (constructed ~1200, peak 1600–2000) is in disassembly; the divergence machine (constructed ~1600, fully deployed for ~25 years) is its successor. The divergence machine strips stable identities, canonical narratives, and centralized systems.
→ See [[divergence-machine]]

## 2026 Contraptions Book Club

Rao runs an annual book club organized around the world machine framework. The 2026 theme is the *divergence machine*, with a focal period of 1600–2000. The January reading is Voltaire's *Candide*; February is Henry Farrell's *The Underground Empire*; March involves choosing between a Leibniz-Spinoza book and an Adam Smith-David Hume book.

The book club is the institutional context for [[florian-lohse]]'s essays [[shitty-castles]] and [[the-best-of-all-possible-worlds]], both of which engage with Rao's framework.

### Late Modernity / Postmodernity / Metamodernity Taxonomy

In "The Divergence Machine" (Jan 2026), Rao proposes a working taxonomy for navigating post-modernity:
- **Late modernity**: energetic zombie — massive energy, little evolutionary intelligence
- **Postmodernity**: intelligent ghost — little energy, durable intellectual currents
- **Metamodernity**: ennervated necromancy — doleful adaptation, "modernism in drag"
- **Divergentism**: the alive thing — new forms of liveness turning on in the world

→ See [[divergence-machine]]

- **James C. Scott** (legibility, seeing like a state — though unacknowledged in these pieces)
- **McLuhan** (medium as machine, technology restructuring cognition)

## Notable Positions

- The Enlightenment and Renaissance are *products* of modernity, not its causes
- Westernization and modernization are largely decoupled; non-Western modernization happened via science/technology, not via European culture
- Traditionalists worldwide want to rewind to ~1100; Islamists to the First Caliphate; American reactionaries to ~1950s — all the same impulse with different timestamps
- AI is a "working-class positive singularity" — centuries of accumulated technocratic knowledge gifted to workers in one move, analogous to the longbow
- The 2025 political right's DEI/Woke attacks are temporary reactionary moves analogous to Philip IV vs. the Templars — they will ultimately fail against the secular trend

## On Authorship as Courage

In [[the-tortured-artist-is-so-yesterday]], Sathe cites Rao's argument that authorship in an LLM era is no longer about labor but about **courage** — the willingness to commit to a line of thought and accept the consequences of being wrong. In a world of infinite AI-generated variations, the decisive act is commitment, not creation. This is one of the key pillars of Sathe's argument that the tortured solitary genius is being replaced by courageous collaborative labs. See [[authorship-as-courage]].

## World Machines Project (2026)

Rao formalized the [[the-world-machines-project-rao|World Machines Project]] in March 2026 as a collaborative nonfiction extended universe growing out of the Contraptions Book Club. Key features:
- Open-source framework: anyone who has written at least one essay using the WM frame is eligible to self-select in
- Goal: collaborative book (or "LLMified monstrosity") — minimum viable scaffolding
- Governance: Rao as BDFL, "disagree but commit" on cybernetics/autopoiesis contributions
- Key starter notes: pull/push distinction, Liveness Machine as AI-associated Dawn Machine, psychohistory framing as long-range climate forecasting

Active contributors identified in WMP announcement: Ivo Velitchkov, Aneesh Sathe, Florian Lohse, Chor Pharn, Kyle Mathews.

## Liveness and New Nature Work (2025–2026)

From the *Contraptions* newsletter, Rao has developed a parallel thread on what it means for machines and technological systems to be *alive*:

- **[[liveness]]** — "a process condition in which time tells memory how to grow, and memory tells time which way to point." Foundational property: [[capture-resistance]] — the property of resisting instrumentalization and asserting control over one's own existence
- **[[new-nature]]** — "regimes of reality governed by technologically mediated laws that are nearly as inviolable, immutable, and persistent as those of nature"
- **[[gramsci-gap]]** — the interregnum between paradigms where monstrous liveness is required; connects to rewilding
- **[[rewilding-technology]]** — the prescription: civilization must re-entangle liveness with death rather than separating them

The useless machine (a device that turns itself off when switched on) serves as the minimal model of liveness — the "e. coli of artificial liveness."

Key influences in this thread: Brian Arthur (technology as harnessed nature), [[werner-herzog]] (the jungle as collective murder, illegible fitness), Albert Camus (suicide as the fundamental philosophical problem), Charles Darwin (tangled bank).

→ See [[engineering-liveness-venkatesh-rao]]

## Contradictions / What's Missing

- Rao claims a "machinic" approach but readers (see Reginald Raye's critique in Modernity Machine III comments) note the analysis is actually *idealist* — explanatory mechanism in the realm of ideas, not energy flows or information processing
- The non-Western cases (India, China, Ottoman Empire) are treated as footnotes rather than co-equal analysis threads
- No engagement with postmodernism as a prior tradition (Lyotard, Habermas) despite covering ostensibly the same terrain
