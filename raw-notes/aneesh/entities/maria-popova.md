# Maria Popova

## Identity

Founder and sole author of [The Marginalian](https://www.themarginalian.org/) (formerly Brain Pickings). Born in Bulgaria; based in New York. Writes long-form essays synthesizing ideas from literature, philosophy, science, and art — typically through close reading of books, letters, and archival sources. Her work is characterized by:
- **Deep quotation**: she excavates primary sources rather than summarizing
- **Thematic synthesis**: finding structural parallels across widely separated thinkers
- **An emphasis on how to live**: her favorite subjects are thinkers who thought seriously about suffering, happiness, and meaning

## "Unselfing"

The concept cited in [[Better Company Than Caesar]] comes from Popova's essay on Bertrand Russell's *The Conquest of Happiness*. She describes happiness as requiring **unselfing** — "some instinctive wakefulness to an aspect of the world other than myself":

> "In my darkest hours, what has saved me again and again is some action of unselfing — some instinctive wakefulness to an aspect of the world other than myself: a helping hand extended to someone else's struggle, the dazzling galaxy just discovered millions of lightyears away, the cardinal trembling in the tree outside my window."

This is offered as both personal testimony and philosophical claim: happiness requires dissolving the self into something beyond its own "bruising boundaries." See [[Curiosity as Ego Dissolution]] for the concept developed from this passage.

## Role in This Wiki

Popova serves as a **contemporary mediator** — she brings historical thinkers (Russell, here) into dialogue with present concerns. Her voice is between the historical source and the contemporary essayist.
