# Ivo Velitchkov

## Overview

Ivo Velitchkov writes *Link & Think*, a Substack newsletter on "systems and technologies." A systems thinker with interest in organizational learning, complexity, and the intersection of technology and society. Contributor to the [[World Machines Project]].

## Key Essays in the WM Corpus

1. **"Voltaire, the Entrepreneur"** (Feb 2026): Recovers Voltaire as a pre-industrial entrepreneur alongside Fugger and Wedgwood — the Ferney watchmaking enterprise as social enterprise and business incubator.

2. **"Three Engineers of Modernity"** (Feb 2026): Profiles [[Jakob Fugger]], [[Josiah Wedgwood]], and [[Voltaire]] as engineers of the [[Modernity Machine]], each transforming specific class relations. Argues Wedgwood and Voltaire also made early "commits" to the [[divergence-machine]].

## Theoretical Moves

- **Defense of "machine" metaphor**: Uses Varela's autopoiesis theory to argue that "machine" and "system" are equivalent, and that machines can be organic/living. (Interesting given Rao's resistance to cybernetics/autopoiesis.)
- **Legibility thesis**: Develops the MM's core as legibility — "to scale something, you need to control it; to control it, you need to read it"
- **Git metaphor**: Uses software version control language ("commits" to the Divergence Machine) to describe historical contributions
- **De-heroization**: Explicitly warns against making historical engineers into heroes — notability loops explain their prominence; "people are complicated and the world is messy"
