# Gary Klein

## Biographical Sketch

Gary Klein is an American cognitive psychologist and research scientist, founder of Klein Associates. He received his PhD in experimental psychology from the University of Pittsburgh (1969). He is the central figure in the [[Naturalistic Decision Making]] (NDM) research tradition.

Klein's work was primarily funded by the US military — particularly the Army Research Institute for Behavioral and Social Sciences — motivated by the practical need to understand and train expert decision-making in high-stakes conditions (military command, fireground operations, medical emergencies).

## Core Intellectual Contributions

### Recognition-Primed Decision (RPD) Model
Klein's first major theoretical contribution. Experts do not generate options and compare them; they recognize the situation as belonging to a type, retrieve a plausible action, mentally simulate it, and execute or adjust. The RPD model was developed from studies of fireground commanders and other expert practitioners.

### Data-Frame Theory of Sensemaking (2007)
Co-authored with Jennifer K. Phillips, Erica L. Rall, and Deborah A. Peluso. The theory that sensemaking is a recursive mutual construction of data and frames — frames filter data; data anchors and extends frames. The theory's key claims:
- ≤3–4 anchors per frame
- Experts hold ≤2–3 frames simultaneously
- Expert-novice difference: mental model richness and frame repertoire, not reasoning strategy
- What looks like confirmation bias is rational frame-guided information seeking
- Nine formal assertions (see [[Data-Frame Theory of Sensemaking]])
- Seven distinct forms of sensemaking with different dynamics and failure modes (see [[Sensemaking Forms]])
- People primarily rely on [[Just-in-Time Mental Models]], not comprehensive representations
- [[Abductive Reasoning]] predominates over deductive logic in naturalistic settings
- Early anchors corrupt sensemaking more severely than late anchors (see [[Anchoring in Sensemaking]])
- [[Fixation Error]] is the pathological form of frame preservation; "diagnostic vagabonding" is the opposite pathology

### Critique of Heuristics and Biases
Klein has been a consistent critic of the Kahneman-Tversky [[Heuristics and Biases]] tradition, arguing that its laboratory methodology with novices on toy problems produces findings that do not generalize to expert practitioners in naturalistic settings. With Daniel Kahneman, Klein co-authored a paper attempting reconciliation of the two traditions ("A Failure to Disagree," Psychological Science, 2009) — which found genuine methodological overlap but persistent disagreements.

## Intellectual Position

- Strongly empiricist and applied — findings must hold up in real-world conditions
- Skeptical of normative decision theories that prescribe how people *should* decide rather than describing how experts *actually* decide
- Advocates for expertise development over debiasing as the route to improved performance
- Part of a broader "ecological rationality" movement (alongside Gerd Gigerenzen, among others)

## Key Texts

- *Sources of Power: How People Make Decisions* (1998) — the popular account of NDM and RPD
- *The Power of Intuition* (2003)
- *Streetlights and Shadows* (2009) — critique of the H&B tradition's practical applications
- *Seeing What Others Don't* (2013) — on insight and frame-breaking moments
