# Chantal Mouffe (b. 1943)

Belgian political theorist, long associated with the University of Westminster. Her major theoretical work is developed in collaboration with Ernesto Laclau and in her own elaboration of "agonistic democracy."

## Core Position

Mouffe argues against liberal-consensualist models of democracy (Rawls, Habermas) that seek to resolve political conflict through rational consensus. For Mouffe, political antagonism is constitutive — it cannot be dissolved, only channelled. The challenge is to transform antagonism (where the opponent is an enemy to be eliminated) into agonism (where the opponent is an adversary whose right to exist is recognised while the struggle continues).

More relevant to the wiki's focus: Mouffe's work on the **relational construction of political identities**. Against liberal individualism (pre-given autonomous subjects entering social contracts) and certain identity-politics framings (pre-given group identities claiming rights), Mouffe argues that identities are constituted through their relations with others — the antagonist is not external to identity but partially constitutive of it.

Key works: *Hegemony and Socialist Strategy* (with Laclau, 1985/2001), *The Return of the Political* (1993), *On the Political* (2005).

## Massey's Use

[[doreen-massey]] invokes Mouffe in Chapter 1 of *For Space* (2005) as support for Proposition 1 (space as product of interrelations):

> "Chantal Mouffe (1993, 1995), in particular, has written of how we might conceptualise the relational construction of political subjectivities. For her, identities and interrelations are constituted together. But spatiality may also be from the beginning integral to the constitution of those identities themselves." (Massey 2005: 10)

Massey's move is to *extend* Mouffe: if identities are relationally constructed, and if spatiality is intrinsic to those relations, then space is not a container for politically-formed identities but co-constitutive of them. A fully relational politics must be a fully spatial politics.

## Divergence from Laclau

Mouffe appears in a complex relationship with Laclau in the wiki. Laclau's own use of "space" is problematic — he equates it with ideological closure (see [[coupure-and-causal-closure]]). Mouffe (at least in Massey's reading) is less committed to this equation and more useful as a political ally on the relational-construction point.
