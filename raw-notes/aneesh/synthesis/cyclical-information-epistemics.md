# Cyclical Information Epistemics

## The Core Pattern

Sathe's [[My Grandfather Had Lore]] uses the Dubai camel proverb as a structural frame: "My grandfather rode a camel, my father rode a camel, I drive a Mercedes, my son drives a Land Rover, his son will drive a Land Rover, but his son will ride a camel."

Applied to information access:
- Generation 1: Lore — oral expertise, social reputation, lived experience as epistemic authority
- Generation 2: Search — hyperlinked retrieval, citations, primary sources accessible to anyone
- Generation 3: LLMs — fluent generation, no citations, confident hallucinations
- Generation 4: Back to lore? — formal education and expert knowledge as the only epistemically defensible sources

## The Regression Mechanism

The regression is not inevitable but conditional on two failures compounding:
1. **Search degradation**: Google's quality decline (see [[Degradation of Search]]) created demand for alternatives
2. **LLM hallucination**: The alternatives (ChatGPT, etc.) replaced poor search with worse epistemic standards — no citations, no confidence signals, confident fabrications

If search quality had remained high, LLMs would have occupied a supplementary rather than primary role. The civilizational regression risk is a conditional: *if* LLMs displace search *and* LLMs remain unreliable, the fallback is expert lore.

## What Would Break the Cycle?

The [[Composite AI Architecture (Experts + LLM + KG)]] represents the technical path out: KGs provide the grounding search provided (traceable, auditable), LLMs provide the accessibility that makes them attractive. If citation-backed generation (see [[Perplexity AI]]) or KG-grounded retrieval succeeds at scale, the cycle is interrupted.

The cultural/institutional path is [[Taste as Epistemic Capacity]] applied to information consumption: users who can recognize hallucinations — who have internalized enough domain knowledge to audit AI outputs — are epistemically protected even in the LLM era. This is precisely [[Nick Cave]]'s implicit claim applied to epistemics rather than art.

## Essay Seed

**Thesis candidate**: The information technology cycle is not a smooth progress narrative but an oscillating dialectic between democratized and restricted access to epistemic authority. Each democratizing technology carries the seeds of its own epistemic degradation — search through SEO gaming, LLMs through hallucination. The question is whether structured retrieval (KGs) can finally break the cycle.
