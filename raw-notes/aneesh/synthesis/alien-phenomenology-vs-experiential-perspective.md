# Alien Phenomenology vs. Experiential Perspective

## A Genuine Disagreement

Most of the Bryant/Tuan synthesis pages in this wiki ([[place-as-the-human-form-of-worlding]], [[naming-as-anchoring]], [[the-gravity-of-infrastructure]], [[machinic-assemblage-and-world-machines]], [[temporal-topology-and-the-viscous-frontier]], [[architecture-and-books-as-crystallized-awareness]], [[visibility-touristic-place-and-algorithmic-curation]]) work out compatibilities and complementarities. This page does the opposite. Bryant and Tuan disagree at the foundation about what *perspective* is, and the disagreement does not resolve. The wiki has to hold both.

## Tuan: Perspective Is Human

[[experiential-perspective-tuan]] is the methodological core of *Space and Place*. Tuan's experience is "a cover-all term for the various modes through which a person knows and constructs a reality. These modes range from the more direct and passive senses of smell, taste, and touch, to active visual perception and the indirect mode of symbolization" (Tuan 2002, p. 8). The book that follows is built on this premise: that the experiential modes of the human animal — kinesthesia, emotion, memory, language, ritual — are the privileged site of geographical understanding.

Tuan is not naïve about this commitment. The book's Epilogue (Ch. 14) admits the limits of the experiential method: "What we cannot say in an acceptable scientific language we tend to deny or forget" (Tuan 2002, p. 200). The social scientist's categories assume a being "endowed with mind and vision but no other sense with which to apprehend the world and find meaning in it" (p. 201). Tuan's response to this limit is not to leave the human; it is to *expand the methodological repertoire within the human* — recover the kinesthetic, the affective, the symbolic, the temporal modes that disciplines have foreclosed.

The position is humanistic in the strict sense: human experience is not just the topic; it is the constitutive frame of the inquiry.

## Bryant: Perspective Is Machinic Closure

[[alien-phenomenology]] (Bryant Ch. 3, drawing on Bogost) starts elsewhere. Every machine — rock, mantis shrimp, insurance company, neutrino — is selectively open to particular flows and operates on those flows according to its own internal operations (Bryant 2014, p. 64). What makes the cognitive case special, for Kant and the philosophical tradition, is what Bryant claims is special about *every* machine:

> "What Kant says here about cognitive-machines holds for all machines. Rocks, neutrinos, insurance companies, and capybara no more encounter things as they are in themselves, than our own minds." (Bryant 2014, pp. 57–58)

This is the radicalization. Operational closure (the firewall behind which each machine relates) is not a feature of consciousness; it is a feature of *machinic operation as such*. The mantis shrimp's eleven-color visual world is not a less-developed version of the human five-color world; it is a different operational closure with its own structural openness. Alien phenomenology is the practice of asking what those closures are like — not by introspection (impossible) but by inference from biology, materials, structural relations.

Bryant is explicit that this is not panpsychism: "Alien phenomenology is not making a panpsychist claim to the effect that all beings, animate and inanimate, possess consciousness, awareness, perception, and cognition" (Bryant 2014, p. 64). The claim is structural, not phenomenal: every machine has a *for-itself* in the limited sense of a selectively open and operationally closed relation to its environment. Whether anything "feels" like anything to be that machine is a separate empirical question.

## Where the Disagreement Bites

The disagreement is not that Tuan denies non-human worlds. He doesn't. He acknowledges that animals have spatial experience (Ch. 3 on developmental psychology repeatedly references mammals); he discusses the cosmological worldings of cultures very different from his own; he is alive to historical and cultural variation in spatial cognition (Ch. 6, Eskimo/Temne; Ch. 7, Pueblo/Chinese/medieval European cosmologies).

The disagreement is about *the constitutive frame of geography*. For Tuan, geography is a humanistic discipline because place is a human achievement. The pause that converts space to place is *the pause of an embodied, symbolizing, mortal animal*. Without that animal — without that mode of being, with its specific kinesthetic, affective, symbolic capacities — there is no place. There is space (or worse, there is the abstract Newtonian grid that science substitutes for both).

For Bryant, the constitutive frame of geography is the machinic ecology, with the human as one machine among many. Bee worlds, river-system worlds, factory worlds are real geographies. To insist that "place" requires human pause is, in Bryant's vocabulary, to mistake one machine's structural openness for the world itself.

## The Wiki's Position: Hold Both

This synthesis argues against forcing a resolution. The wiki should hold the two frameworks as complementary domains:

- **Tuan for the inhabiting subject's interior.** When the question is *what is it like to dwell in this neighborhood, this city, this craft, this archive?* — Tuan's experiential apparatus is irreplaceable. The kinesthetic, affective, mnemonic, symbolic modes; the body as measure; the growth rings of accumulated sentiment. This is the territory of the wiki's [[the-shelter-as-epistemic-engine]], [[books-are-dwellings]], [[the-mind-as-semi-solid-smoke]], [[Place-Making as Epistemic Act]].

- **Bryant for the relational-material exterior.** When the question is *what gravitational fields are operating on the agents who inhabit this neighborhood, this platform, this energy regime?* — Bryant's machinic apparatus is irreplaceable. Bright objects, gravitational topologies, structural couplings, alien phenomenologies of platforms and corporations. This is the territory of [[protocol-gods-and-platform-demons]], [[the-gravity-of-infrastructure]], [[platforms-as-neo-bosses]], [[machines-as-energy-regimes]].

A complete account of any phenomenon needs both. Take algorithmic curation. The Bryant lens reveals: an alien phenomenology of the platform-as-machine, structurally open to engagement metrics and operationally closed around them, exercising gravitational pull regardless of user belief ([[the-gravity-of-infrastructure]]). The Tuan lens reveals: what it is like to be a user inside that field — the kinesthetic disorientation, the failure of the place-making pause, the loss of intimate place to manufactured visibility ([[visibility-touristic-place-and-algorithmic-curation]]). Either lens alone misses half the phenomenon.

## A Constructive Reading: Place as Maximum-Plasticity Alien Phenomenology

One way to hold the tension productively. Bryant's spectrum of [[rigid-plastic-machines]] (Ch. 2): mathematical theorems and rocks at the rigid end; organic bodies, great works of art, anarchic political movements at the plastic end. Plasticity is "pluripotency" — the capacity to produce genuinely different outputs across contexts of coupling.

The human animal, with its kinesthetic-affective-symbolic-temporal capacities, sits at the *maximum-plasticity end* of Bryant's spectrum among the machines we know. Operational transformation reaches its richest form here. Tuan's experiential perspective is therefore the most elaborated form of alien phenomenology accessible to investigation — because the human investigator can both undergo and report it from the inside.

This formulation does not collapse Tuan into Bryant. It locates Tuan's contribution within Bryant's broader frame without erasing the experiential primacy that Tuan's method requires *for human geography specifically*. The human's first-person access to its own machinic operations is asymmetric. We have it; we don't have it for the mantis shrimp. Tuan exploits this asymmetry; Bryant generalizes past it. Both are right about their respective domains.

## Connection to [[biology-of-meaning-making]]

The wiki's [[biology-of-meaning-making]] argues for a continuum from chemotaxis to philosophical dialogue: orientation is a biological imperative continuous across scales. This page sharpens the claim. Chemotaxis is the alien-phenomenological lower bound: an operational closure that registers chemical gradients and transforms them into directed motion. Philosophical dialogue is the experiential-perspective upper bound: maximum plasticity machines coupling through symbolic flows that fold time topologically (Bryant's [[temporal-topology-of-machines]]) and produce shared frames ([[frames-as-places-sensemaking-as-place-making]]).

The biological grounding does not erase the disagreement; it locates it. Where in the continuum does Tuan's "experience" begin? Bryant would say: in the limited sense, with any machine; in the rich sense, somewhere along the plasticity spectrum where mnemonic, symbolic, affective operations become possible. Tuan would say: with the embodied animal capable of pause and naming. Both can be right depending on what we want the word "experience" to do.

## Essay Seeds

**"Two Lenses for the Algorithm"**
A short essay analyzing a single phenomenon (the Instagram feed, say) through both lenses sequentially. The Bryant lens: alien phenomenology of the platform-as-machine, what flows it is structurally open to, what its operational closure produces, what gravitational pull it exerts on satellites. The Tuan lens: what it is like to be a user dwelling (or failing to dwell) in this manufactured visibility regime, what kinesthetic-affective-symbolic-temporal modes it engages or forecloses. Argue that neither lens alone is adequate.

**"The Plasticity Spectrum"**
A meditation on Bryant's rigid-plastic spectrum applied to the human as machine. What does it mean to be a maximum-plasticity machine? What does this say about the modal aspirations of [[Plasticity vs. Flexibility]] (Malabou) and the wiki's [[the-end-of-identity-ai-plasticity-divergence-machine]]? The human, as a maximum-plasticity machine, is the machine most at risk of being captured by *alien phenomenologies* it does not recognize as such — the algorithm's structural openness to engagement is alien, but most users do not register it as such because plasticity makes them coupling-prone.
