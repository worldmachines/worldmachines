# Longitudinal Becoming vs. Stratigraphic Time

Ingold runs a single opposition -- *accumulate-and-exhaust* versus *overflow-and-replenish* -- at two scales that his essay never explicitly joins: the individual life-course and the passage of generations. Seen together, they are one alternative ontology of time argued twice. This is the cross-cutting claim neither note makes on its own.

## The same figure at two scales

- **The life-course (the funnel).** The Western "fulfilment of potential" treats a life as a funnel -- a thousand possible lives narrowing to the one lived, potential "spent down" until "there is nowhere further to go." Against it Ingold sets the Pintupi "one-possibility thing," possibility walked into and continually replenished, and formalizes the contrast: doing is a *transverse* link (intention to objective), but the undergoing that accompanies it runs *longitudinally* -- the wavy line "P" of possibility -- so a life "continually overtakes itself," a "becoming rather than being" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-potential-fulfilment-doing-undergoing]]).
- **The generations (the stack).** At civilizational scale the same "fulfilment" logic reappears as **stratigraphic thinking**: generations as "layers of sedimentation," each inheriting a stock, using it up, and passing on "the discards." Against it Ingold sets the **intergenerational braid** -- overlapping strands where new strands are paid in as old ones give out, and transmission can break without extinction ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-passage-of-generations-astonishment-surprise]]).

The funnel and the stack are the same geometry -- a finite stock exhausted one way -- scaled down to a life and up to a lineage; the longitudinal P-line and the braid are the same alternative -- ongoing overflow, possibility renewed rather than spent.

## The micro-mechanism and the political payoff

At the scale of the single step, the same ontology appears as *falling forwards*: anticipation "opens a path" into an as-yet-unformed world while prediction "closes one," fixing an end point in advance ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-two-sides-of-attention-falling-forwards]]). And the stakes are explicitly political: the stack model "feeds directly into a rhetoric of extinction" -- the fear that the coming generation "might be the last" -- so re-imagining time as a braid is offered "to lift the curse of uncertainty, and to restore a sense of possibility."

## Relation to the World Machines frame

This is Ingold's contribution to the wiki's long argument over the shape of time -- [[linear-vs-cyclical-paths]], [[trajectories-as-stories-so-far]], and [[simultaneity-of-multiple-trajectories]]. His braid resists reading history as accumulated sediment or as potential progressively spent, cutting against a purely accumulative reading of progress ([[argument-of-progress]]) and against any [[dawn-day-dusk-machine-typology]] deployed as a stock-depletion story. The relational, non-teleological co-emergence he describes -- possibility generated through entanglement rather than aimed at a predetermined end -- is close to what the wiki files under [[Configurancy]].
