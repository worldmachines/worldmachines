# Interestingness as Frame Denial

## The Connection

Two sources in this wiki — [[davis-thats-interesting]] (1971) and [[data-frame-theory-of-sensemaking]] (2007) — describe the same pre-theoretical structures from opposite directions.

**Davis** calls them "assumption-grounds" — webs of taken-for-granted beliefs that constitute an audience's baseline reality. Interesting propositions work by *denying* specific strands of this web. His [[Index of the Interesting]] catalogs 12 logical dimensions along which denial operates.

**Klein** calls them "frames" — explanatory structures that make data visible, organize perception, and guide action. Sensemaking works by *fitting* data into frames and modifying frames around data. His theory identifies nine assertions about how frames are built, questioned, and replaced.

The structural isomorphism: both posit a pre-existing cognitive structure (assumption-ground / frame) that filters reality, resists disruption, and yields to targeted modification. Davis describes the *logic* of disruption (which dimension is being denied); Klein describes the *dynamics* (how disruption propagates through the cognitive system).

## The Synthesis

Combining Davis and Klein yields a richer model:

1. **Interestingness is frame disruption** — a proposition is interesting when it forces frame modification along one of Davis's 12 dimensions
2. **The "sweet spot" is frame modification, not frame destruction** — Davis's obvious/interesting/absurd spectrum maps onto Klein's frame preservation / frame modification / frame abandonment
3. **Expertise determines what's interesting** — Klein shows that experts have richer, denser frames. This means they have more assumption-strands available for denial, and can recognize subtler denials. Novices find fewer things interesting because their frames are thinner.
4. **Davis's categories predict the *type* of frame modification** — Organization (reframing pattern/chaos), Causation (reversing causal arrows), Function (reinterpreting latent function) each map onto specific types of frame revision in Klein's model

## The AI Homogenization Implication

This synthesis has a direct bearing on [[ai-homogenization-and-the-counter-enlightenment]]:

**If LLMs homogenize assumption-grounds, they collapse the space of what can be found interesting.**

Davis's model shows that interestingness is *relational* — it depends on the gap between a proposition and its audience's assumptions. When AI-mediated discourse compresses cognitive diversity toward a statistical mean, it homogenizes the assumption-grounds across populations. The result:

- **Fewer propositions register as interesting** — because the shared assumption-ground has fewer unique strands available for targeted denial
- **"Interesting" converges toward a single register** — the denial patterns that work for the modal WEIRD assumption-ground become the dominant mode of intellectual surprise
- **Non-WEIRD interestingness becomes invisible** — propositions that would be interesting *against a different assumption-ground* fail to register because the LLM has already flattened the diversity of baseline beliefs

This is the [[Counter-Enlightenment]] argument in Davis's vocabulary: Herder would say that each culture has its own assumption-ground, and the propositions that are interesting within one culture may be obvious or absurd within another. Homogenizing the assumption-ground doesn't make ideas universally interesting — it makes one culture's interesting ideas the only game in town.

## The Explorer Connection

The self-determined user (see [[the-explorer-and-the-tourist]]) maintains an idiosyncratic assumption-ground — built from personal experience, diverse reading, non-standard influences. This is what makes them capable of finding things interesting that others miss. LLM homogenization threatens this capacity not by preventing exploration but by subtly normalizing the explorer's assumption-ground toward the mean.

[[Novalis]]'s "self-determined beings" and Davis's "diverse assumption-grounds" are making the same structural claim: cognitive diversity is not just a feature of the collective but a precondition for individual intellectual vitality.
