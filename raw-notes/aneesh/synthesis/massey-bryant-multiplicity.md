# Massey and Bryant on Positive Multiplicity

A cross-source synthesis linking [[doreen-massey]]'s argument in *For Space* Chs. 4–5 (positive vs. negative multiplicity; the dimension of multiplicity) and [[levi-bryant]]'s machinic ontology in [[onto-cartography]]. There is an existing wiki page [[massey-bryant-relational-space]] (created in the Part 4 pass) that handles the *spatial* convergence. This page extends to the deeper ontological question: **how should plurality be grounded?**

## The shared starting point

Both Massey and Bryant insist on **positive multiplicity** — plurality as the *default* condition rather than the disrupted-residue of a presumed One. Both align themselves explicitly with the Spinoza–Bergson–Deleuze line of philosophical affirmation. Both reject the negative ontology that grounds difference in the constitutive expulsion from a One.

Massey's articulation (Ch. 4 of *For Space*; see [[positive-versus-negative-multiplicity]]):

> "It is in their rejection of this negativity, their emphasis on affirmation, that the line of philosophy Spinoza–Bergson–Deleuze has more to offer a re-thinking of space." (Massey 2005: 51)

Bryant's articulation (*Onto-Cartography*, Ch. 1 and *passim*): the world is constituted by **machines** — pluralities of operating entities — none of which is grounded in a prior One. Spinoza's *natura naturans* is the deep philosophical referent.

## Where the routes differ

### Massey's route: through the politics of difference

Massey arrives at positive multiplicity through her critique of [[jacques-derrida]] (the [[horizontalities-of-deconstruction]] argument) and her engagement with feminist geographers (Grosz, Irigaray, Rose). The decisive move is **political**: the negative ontology of deconstruction "leads too much to a focus on internal fragmentation and the contemplation of internal decentring rather than to an engagement with external relatedness" (Robinson 1999, quoted Massey 2005: 51). Positive multiplicity is required for politics — for recognising the actual co-presence of others.

### Bryant's route: through the metaphysics of operations

Bryant arrives at positive multiplicity through his account of **machines** as operationally closed entities that produce themselves through their operations. The decisive move is **metaphysical**: a One-and-its-residues ontology cannot account for the genuine **production** of new entities; only an ontology of plural operating machines can. See [[machinic-assemblage-and-world-machines]] (existing).

## The complementarity

Each route arrives at a partial articulation that the other completes.

### What Bryant gives Massey

A **mechanism**. Massey is content to say *that* space is the dimension of multiplicity, and to insist on its political consequences. Bryant specifies *how* the multiplicity is produced — through the operations of machines that bring new entities into being. Without this, Massey's multiplicity risks being a brute postulate.

### What Massey gives Bryant

A **politics**. Bryant's machinic ontology is anti-essentialist and anti-monist, but its political content is thin (the standard worries about flat ontology and political agency apply). Massey's [[power-geometry]] specifies how some machines produce inequalities of access, mobility, and voice — converting metaphysical plurality into political analysis. Without this, Bryant's pluralism risks being apolitical.

## Where they may genuinely disagree

Bryant in Ch. 6 of *Onto-Cartography* takes Massey as a near-ally but notes that she associates space too closely with **narrative** and **expression** rather than with **machinic-material processes**. The worry: Massey's "stories-so-far" formula imports a hermeneutic register that her own ontology should be able to do without.

Massey's reply (if she were to make one) would likely run: the term "story" in her usage is deliberately stripped of its narrative-hermeneutic baggage. A geological formation has a "story" (its formation, erosion, meeting with this glacier) without any narrator. See [[stories-so-far]]. The disagreement is then about whether the term carries unwanted residues — a matter of vocabulary rather than substance.

A second possible point of friction: Bryant's machines can be operationally closed; Massey's trajectories are constitutively open. But the disagreement may dissolve into the question of *what is closed to what* — every machine is closed to some operations and open to others; every trajectory is open to some meetings and closed to others.

## What this implies for the wiki

The two frameworks should be read as **complementary tools** rather than as competing accounts of the same ground:

- Use Massey when the question is **political and spatial**: who is positioned how, what power-geometries are in play, how does the negotiation of place go.
- Use Bryant when the question is **ontological and operational**: what kind of entity is this, what does it do, what are its powers, how is it produced.

The most powerful spatial-political analysis available combines both.
