# The Entrepreneur as Machine Engineer

## The Pattern

[[Ivo Velitchkov]] identifies three pre-industrial entrepreneurs — [[Jakob Fugger]], [[Josiah Wedgwood]], and [[Voltaire]] — as *engineers of the [[Modernity Machine]]*. The pattern across all three is:

1. Each operated within the MM's class structure
2. Each transformed one or more of the four class relations (not just within them)
3. Each's transformation was enabled by and further amplified legibility
4. Two (Wedgwood, Voltaire) also opened space for the [[divergence-machine]] — inadvertently or deliberately

## The Structural Template

Each engineer can be mapped against the same template:

| Engineer | Class Relation Transformed | Legibility Contribution | DM Commit? |
|----------|---------------------------|------------------------|------------|
| Fugger | Monarch ↔ Oligarchy (divine right → creditor-debtor) | Financial legibility (bookkeeping, auditing, consolidated statements) | No |
| Wedgwood | Monarch ↔ Technocracy → cascade; Technocracy ↔ Workers (division of labor) | Industrial legibility (pyrometer, timetables, cost accounting) | Yes (Lunar Society, experimentation) |
| Voltaire | Monarch ↔ Technocracy (pet → celebrity); State ↔ Citizens (transparency) | Epistemic legibility (public Newton, Calas affair, celebrity) | Yes (Candide, tolerance campaigns) |

## The Creditor Parallel

Both Fugger and Voltaire became **creditors to the ruling class** — a structural parallel that spans two centuries and two completely different contexts. This is remarkable: the mechanism by which oligarchs and intellectuals gained persistent agency against monarchs was the same — holding the monarch's debt. 

This suggests the creditor-debtor relation is a fundamental machine of power transformation — not a one-off but a recurring structure within the MM.

## The Notability Loop Problem

Velitchkov explicitly flags that these three engineers are partly prominent due to preferential attachment in information networks: notability attracts attention which generates more notability. There may have been equally significant contributors who were quieter or less legible.

This is not merely humility — it is structurally interesting. The MM's own legibility drive operated on *people* as well as things: those who were most visible (legible) to the network became the centers around which knowledge accumulated. The machine's human engineers were themselves selected by the machine's legibility logic.

## From Machine Engineering to Machine Spawning

Wedgwood and Voltaire didn't just engineer the MM — they inadvertently helped spawn its successor:
- Wedgwood: pluralist, exploratory knowledge-culture (Lunar Society) that treated progress as instrumental learning rather than material endpoint
- Voltaire: used absurdity against dogma, created space for ontological doubt, demonstrated that celebrity could exceed the grip of any single patron

Neither set out to build the DM. The DM emerged in the space the MM's own excess created — and these two engineers were among those who first occupied that space.
