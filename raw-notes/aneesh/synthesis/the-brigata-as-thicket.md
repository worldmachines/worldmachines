# The Brigata as Thicket

## The case

When the *Decameron*'s ten young Florentines flee the 1348 plague for a country estate, they are not merely escaping infection. They are *constructing* something: a small, deliberately designed society with a portal, an interior, a rule-set, and — crucially — a closure date. Read against [[The Thicket]] and the [[gramsci-gap]], the **brigata** turns out to be one of the most complete pre-modern depictions we have of the whole thicket life-cycle — formation, maintenance, and deliberate dissolution — and it is depicted by a participant-author who understood exactly what he was building.

This is what makes it worth a synthesis rather than a footnote. The wiki's existing thicket-and-garden material ([[gardens-thickets-and-the-illegibility-problem]]) reads gardens as *cultivated illegibility* — the place "you couldn't explain to someone not standing in it." The brigata adds a different axis: the thicket as a **society** with a temporal arc, born in an interregnum and ended on purpose.

## Formation: a thicket built inside a Gramsci gap

The plague description that opens Day One ([[dec-day-01]]) is, almost clause for clause, a description of a [[gramsci-gap]]. Boccaccio writes that "the reverend authority of the laws, both human and divine, was all in a manner dissolved and fallen into decay," because the ministers and executors charged with enforcing them are dead. The old legitimacy — civic law, canonical custom, kin obligation — has lost its force; no new order has crystallized to replace it. "Every one had license to do whatsoever pleased him." This is precisely the interregnum: the old is dying and the new cannot yet be born, and the gap fills with morbid symptoms (the *becchini*, the abandoned dead, the licentious and the despairing).

The brigata is the response. It does **not** attempt to resolve the gap — it cannot, and Pampinea's founding speech is careful never to claim it can. Instead it constructs a bounded interior in which a way of being can be held open while the surrounding order is suspended. Pampinea explicitly frames the withdrawal as creation rather than abandonment: remaining means watching the social order dissolve, while leaving "doth harm to no one," because the city is already abandoning itself. The thicket here is not an escape *from* the world so much as a place built *inside* the world's failure.

## Geometry: portal, interior, terroir, rule

The brigata reproduces the thicket's structural geometry with unusual fidelity ([[dec-day-01]], [[dec-day-03]], [[dec-day-06]]):

- **Portal.** The company leaves the city together, through a single decision and a single act of withdrawal; servants are handled as a separate, managed boundary. Membership is curated (they know one another; the three men join the seven women by deliberate invitation). The estate sits two miles beyond the walls, withdrawn "from the high way."
- **Interior as real Space.** Inside, the brigata is governed not by the city's collapsing institutions but by its own rules: a **rotating monarchy** (a king or queen per day), a daily schedule built from the repurposed canonical hours, divided household roles, and a single explicit rule — **no news from the stricken city** is brought inside. Within that frame the interior is genuinely open: each day's sovereign sets a theme, and the storytelling proceeds with real latitude (Dioneo even reserves the right to tell whatever he likes). This is Space honestly presented, not a terraformed plain — high-entropy discourse organized around a way of being rather than optimized to an external mean.
- **Terroir.** The walled Eden-garden of Day Three ([[dec-day-03]]) and the topographically exact **Ladies' Valley** of Day Six ([[dec-day-06]]) — "a round, naturally enclosed space entered by a single narrow way, interior meadows invisible to the outside, its own miniature hydrology" — are the non-portable local conditions that make this particular refuge what it is. They are sought, not advertised; legible only from inside.

## Maintenance: storytelling as world-maintenance

The brigata's central activity is not idleness but **storytelling**, and the *Decameron* repeatedly argues that this is the work that keeps the refuge alive. The Proem ([[dec-proem]]) casts stories as medicine for the enclosed; the hundred tales themselves are, in large part, a catalogue of [[capture-resistance]] — wit, opacity, disguise, and the well-timed word deployed against institutions, surveillance, and Fortune that would otherwise capture the protagonists (the convent-records that never identify Masetto, the surveillance husbands of Day Seven whose every legibility-trap collapses, Fra Cipolla's improvisation that converts a trap into a profit). The brigata, in other words, maintains itself by telling stories *about* the very capture-resistance that the refuge itself enacts. Storytelling is world-maintenance: the activity by which the interior way of being is rehearsed, transmitted, and kept in shape.

Dioneo states the poetics aloud at the close of Day Six ([[dec-day-06]]): because "the judges have forsaken the tribunals" and "the laws, as well Divine as human, are silent," full licence is conceded "for the preservation of life." The licence of the stories is coextensive with the borders of the refuge — a point Boccaccio makes explicitly in the Author's Conclusion ([[dec-conclusion]]), where he defends the whole book by appeal to the fact that the tales were told "amidst gardens, in a place of pleasance," to listeners of mature wit. Remove the thicket and the defence collapses.

## Dissolution: the thicket with a closure date

The brigata's most distinctive contribution to the thicket concept is its **ending**. On the morning after Day Ten ([[dec-day-10]]), Pamfilo dissolves the company — not because of conflict or failure, but because the refuge is becoming **legible to outsiders**: "our company, already known to several others of the neighbourhood, may multiply after a fashion that will deprive us of our every commodity." The moment the interior's opacity erodes, its value collapses, so the brigata closes itself before that happens and returns to Florence, the plague still waiting.

This sharpens the thicket concept in two ways. First, it confirms that a thicket is **defined by controlled access**, not by permanence: when opacity fails, the thicket ends, and a wise community ends it deliberately rather than letting it be captured or diluted. Second, it distinguishes the thicket from mere flight. The brigata is never a permanent secession; it is a temporary holding-open of a way of being *during* an interregnum, with a built-in return. The thicket maintains a way of being through the gap; it does not pretend to abolish the world the gap belongs to.

## Why this is a genuine extension, not a forced fit

The connection is grounded in the text's own structure, not imposed on it. Boccaccio supplies, unprompted, every element the concept requires: a precisely-drawn interregnum, a curated portal-bordered interior, an explicit internal rule-set, a defended terroir, a central activity that functions as world-maintenance, and a deliberate dissolution triggered by loss of opacity. What the brigata adds to existing canon is the **full temporal arc and the social register**: where [[gardens-thickets-and-the-illegibility-problem]] reads the thicket-garden as a problem of cultivation and illegibility, the brigata shows a thicket that is a *society*, born in a *gap*, that knows it is temporary and ends itself on its own terms. It is the historical case study that lets the thicket be read across time, not only across the cultivated/illegible axis.
