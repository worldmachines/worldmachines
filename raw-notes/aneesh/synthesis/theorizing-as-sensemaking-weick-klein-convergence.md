# Theorizing as Sensemaking — The Weick-Klein Convergence

## The Parallel

Karl Weick's [[Theory as Process vs. Product]] and Gary Klein's [[Data-Frame Theory of Sensemaking]] arrive at the same epistemological claim from different directions:

| Weick (organizational theory) | Klein (cognitive science) |
|---|---|
| Theorizing is an ongoing process; theory is a frozen product | Sensemaking cycles through elaboration, questioning, and reframing; congruence is a temporary resting state |
| "Interim struggles" toward theory are valuable and should be shared | Just-in-time mental models assembled from fragments are more effective than comprehensive models |
| Premature freezing of theory truncates developing insight | Premature congruence (frame preservation) blocks anomaly detection |
| The ultimate tradeoff: "between doing it and freezing it" | Sensemaking ceases when data and frame reach congruence — but this cessation can be premature |

Both are arguing against the same opponent: the view that knowledge is a product to be accumulated rather than a process to be sustained.

## The Common Enemy: Premature Freezing

Weick's version: publication biases toward freezing theory. Journals want finished products, not interim struggles. The result: theories are published prematurely, defended rigidly, and treated as completed objects rather than developing processes. The "keep theorizing" injunction is structurally discouraged.

Klein's version: sensemaking ceases when data and frame reach congruence. This is functional — you need to stop deliberating and act. But the preservation cycle can protect an inadequate frame by explaining away anomalous data, producing false congruence. The "keep sensemaking" injunction is cognitively expensive and easily abandoned.

The structural parallel: in both cases, the system has a strong attractor toward premature closure (published theory / congruent frame) and a weak attractor toward continued revision (ongoing theorizing / continued sensemaking). The normative claim in both cases: the discipline of continued revision is where the real epistemic work happens.

## Weick's Sensemaking as Klein's Macrocognition

Weick is explicitly part of Klein's intellectual context — the data-frame paper cites Weick's 1995 definition: sensemaking is "a continuous alternation between particulars [data] and explanations [frames]." But the two frameworks operate at different levels:

- **Weick**: sensemaking is organizational — groups construct shared meaning retrospectively through enactment, selection, and retention
- **Klein**: sensemaking is individual — analysts construct frames from anchors through elaboration, preservation, and reframing cycles

The convergence: whether the sensemaker is a person or an organization, the epistemic dynamics are the same. Frames are constructed from minimal anchors, elaborated through active inquiry, defended against anomalies, and occasionally replaced. The difference is the medium: individual cognition for Klein, organizational routines and narratives for Weick.

## The JIT Mental Model and the Interim Struggle

Klein's "just-in-time mental models" — assembled from fragmentary causal knowledge on demand rather than pre-built as comprehensive systems — are the cognitive equivalent of Weick's "interim struggles."

Both claim:
1. Comprehensive models are rarely available and often counterproductive
2. Working knowledge is assembled on the fly from fragments
3. The fragments are more valuable than any single assembled model
4. Training should expand the fragment repertoire, not build comprehensive models

The practical implication is the same: invest in accumulating diverse fragments (frame elements, causal relationships, partial theories, rich cases) rather than in constructing comprehensive systems. The comprehensive system is a byproduct of rich fragment libraries, not a prerequisite for them.

## Connection to the World Machines Project

The wiki's [[World Machines as Interim Theory]] synthesis page already identifies the World Machines Framework as explicitly Weickian — "sensemaking triage and shared conceptual language, not a Grand Unified Theory." The Klein connection deepens this: the WMP's epistemic posture is not just theorizing-as-process but sensemaking-as-frame-construction. The collective book project is an attempt to build a shared frame from diverse anchors contributed by different participants.

The risk Klein's theory identifies: the WMP could succumb to frame preservation — defending its initial framing against anomalies rather than reframing when the data warrants it. Weick would call this "theoretical rigor mortis"; Klein would call it the preservation cycle.

## The Starbuck Inversion and Expert Sensemaking

Weick cites William Starbuck's claim that the best theorists prescribe directly from symptoms without theorizing as an intermediary — theory comes last, summarizing observed treatment-symptom relationships. This is structurally identical to Klein's account of expert sensemaking: experts commit to frames from a few anchors (symptoms), elaborate through action, and only later articulate the frame explicitly.

Both describe a practice where explicit theory/frame articulation *follows* rather than precedes competent action. The fragment library enables direct pattern-to-action mapping; the articulated theory/frame is a retrospective reconstruction. This connects to [[Outcome Orientation]] in the Commoncog material: the emphasis on functional understanding (knowing what to *do*) over abstract understanding (knowing what is *true*).

## Essay Seed

**"The Process All the Way Down: On Never Finishing a Theory"**

Thesis: Weick and Klein, from organizational theory and cognitive science, both describe knowledge as inherently processual — a cycle of construction, elaboration, and revision that is truncated, not completed, when it produces a "finished" product. The essay explores what it would mean to take this seriously: to design institutions, tools, and practices that sustain theorizing/sensemaking rather than optimizing for theory/congruence. The wiki itself is an attempt at this.
