# World Machines as Interim Theory: A Weickian Defense

## The Tension

The [[world-machines-framework]] is frequently in tension with its own epistemic ambitions. Rao explicitly says it is "not a Grand Unified Theory" — but it has the *form* of a GUT (big claims, historical sweep, predictive typology). Critics (including Rao's own internal critique) note that it risks becoming exactly the kind of cyclic history model (Kondratriev, Turchin, Toynbee) it claims to avoid.

[[Karl Weick]]'s "What Theory Is Not, Theorizing Is" provides a principled response to this tension.

## The Weickian Defense

Weick: the question is not "is this a theory?" but "is this an *interim struggle* moving clearly from one stage toward the next, with past and future articulated?"

By this standard, the WM framework is:
- A **general orientation** (Merton's Type 1): broad framework specifying types of variables (machines, class relations, legibility, pull/push) without fully specifying relationships
- Moving toward **analysis of concepts** (Merton's Type 2): the legibility concept, the pull/push distinction, the four class relations are being developed but not yet interrelated into a testable system
- Explicitly committed to staying close to **raw historical phenomenology** — the "data as symptoms" mode (Starbuck/Weick): don't theorize prematurely; let patterns emerge from historical cases

## The Collaborative Book as Institutionalized Process

The [[World Machines Project]] book is not attempting to produce a final theory. It is attempting to institutionalize and publish the *theorizing process itself* — the "interim struggles."

This is Weick's prescription: "If the five are part of an interim struggle that is moving from one text to another, a struggle whose past and future is made clear by the author, then I would be tempted to give the author another shot."

The WMP protocol (book club → essays → collective synthesis) makes the theorizing process traceable. Each essay is a staged step, not a standalone product. The 30% shared reading requirement ensures shared reference base for synthesis.

## Where the Analogy Breaks

Weick's framework is for individual papers in a low-paradigm academic field. The WMP is:
- Collective, not individual
- Interdisciplinary, not field-specific
- Operating at civilizational rather than organizational scale
- Explicitly anti-paradigmatic

This means Weick's "revise and resubmit" model doesn't apply directly. The WMP's quality control mechanism is different: rough consensus and running code (open-source metaphor), not peer review.

## The Risk: Diagrams Preceding Diagrams

Weick's warning: if "diagrams preceded this paper and diagrams will be the focus of the next paper — then the theorizing is less robust." 

Applied to WMP: if the framework is applied to case after case without the cases changing the framework, the project has stalled. The test of robust WM theorizing is whether historical cases *modify* the framework, not just illustrate it.

The starter questions Rao poses (full inventory of WMs, necessary/sufficient conditions, micro-macro fractal structure) are the right kind of self-challenge: they could reveal that the 400-year constant is wrong, or that the typology is inapplicable to pre-modern eras.
