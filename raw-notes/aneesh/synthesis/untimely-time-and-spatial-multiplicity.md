# Untimely Time and Spatial Multiplicity

[[bayo-akomolafe-the-untimely]] adds a new axis to the wiki's existing time/space thread. [[doreen-massey]] reopens space by rejecting static synchrony in favor of [[dynamic-simultaneity]]: the spatial now is a coexistence of ongoing trajectories. [[levi-bryant]] reopens time by pluralizing machine temporalities in [[temporal-topology-of-machines]]. [[bayo-akomolafe]] reopens time differently: not by multiplying trajectories or machine rates, but by noticing the cracks in the clock's count.

## Three Anti-Modern Temporal Moves

1. **Massey: difference is coeval** — modernity converts spatial difference into a temporal queue; Massey restores simultaneous multiplicity. See [[cosmopolitan-time-queue]] and [[coevalness]].
2. **Bryant: time is operational rhythm** — every machine has its own temporality, and politics often happens through mismatched rates. See [[pace-and-rhythm-as-political]].
3. **Akomolafe: discipline is incomplete** — the clock disciplines bodies, but its count secretes intervals, upbeats, and fugitive excess. See [[clock-as-incomplete-discipline]].

## The New Contribution

Akomolafe is less interested in adding another temporality than in refusing the premise that time's discipline ever closed. This matters because many alternatives remain inside the grammar of mastery: longer horizons, deeper pasts, better clocks, better plans. The [[untimely]] is not a better clock but the failure of clockhood to exhaust what bodies are doing.

This complicates the wiki's enthusiasm for deep time. [[deep-time-and-place-identity]] treats geological scale as a destabilizer of essential identity. Akomolafe agrees only conditionally: deep time destabilizes only if it also decenters the calculating subject. Otherwise it becomes [[deep-time-without-decentering]].

## Essay Seed

**"The Crack in the Clock"** — An essay comparing three escapes from modern temporality: Massey's coeval spatial multiplicity, Bryant's operational temporal pluralism, and Akomolafe's untimely excess. Thesis: modernity is not defeated by having more time, deeper time, or plural time alone; it is defeated where its own timekeeping fails to complete the capture of bodies.

## Open Tension

Akomolafe's anti-programmatic posture is potent but risky. Massey and Bryant still offer analytic maps: power-geometry, machine topology, temporal mismatch. Akomolafe offers sanctuary and enlistment by the unknown. The synthesis question: can the crack be mapped without being sealed?
