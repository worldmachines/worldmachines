# AI Homogenization and the Counter-Enlightenment

## The Connection

Two sources in this wiki — [[Isaiah Berlin — Against the Current]] and [[USC AI Homogenization Research]] — are separated by two and a half centuries but are working on the same problem from opposite ends.

[[Isaiah Berlin]] spent his career excavating the [[Counter-Enlightenment]] thinkers who challenged the Enlightenment's monist, universalist assumption that there is one correct way to reason, one correct set of values, one universal human nature that all peoples share (or should share). [[Johann Gottfried Herder]] argued that each culture embodies its own incommensurable form of excellence. [[Giambattista Vico]] argued that each historical civilization can only be understood from within. Their collective warning: impose universal reason, and you destroy irreplaceable human particularity.

The USC researchers argue that LLMs are accomplishing exactly this — but technologically rather than philosophically. By training on data that overrepresents WEIRD societies and dominant languages, and by optimizing for chain-of-thought linear reasoning, LLMs quietly redefine what counts as "credible speech, correct perspective, or even good reasoning." The result is a technological enactment of Enlightenment universalism: one way of thinking, scaled to billions.

## The Structural Parallel

| Enlightenment project | LLM homogenization |
|---|---|
| Claims universal reason applies to all cultures | Trains on dominant culture, applies outputs universally |
| Dismisses non-Western epistemologies as "pre-rational" | Marginalizes non-WEIRD reasoning styles as statistically rare |
| Imposes single standard of good argument | Redefines "credible speech" toward WEIRD academic norms |
| Treats cultural difference as temporary imperfection | Treats cognitive variance as noise to be reduced |

## What the Counter-Enlightenment Would Say

Herder's objection to LLMs would be: you are training a machine on the output of one civilization's dominant voices and then deploying it globally as if it represents universal human cognition. You are not building a tool; you are building an instrument of cultural hegemony that cannot be recognized as such because it operates through the apparently neutral mechanism of statistical inference.

Vico's objection would be epistemological: the imaginative capacity (*fantasia*) through which humans understand each other across cultures cannot be captured in training data. The most culturally diverse LLM imaginable still approximates understanding from the outside; it cannot achieve the inside view.

Berlin's objection would be political: if [[Value Pluralism]] is correct, then the attempt to reduce all human reasoning to a single statistical model is not just intellectually impoverished but politically dangerous. It creates the conditions for a soft epistemic authoritarianism — not the brutal kind Maistre's followers enacted, but the well-intentioned kind: everyone reasons better once they reason like us.

## The Individual-Group Paradox as Counter-Enlightenment Data

The USC finding that individuals reason better with LLMs but groups reason worse is itself a counter-Enlightenment result. It shows that [[Cognitive Diversity]] — the diversity of reasoning styles across individuals — is a *group resource* that the Enlightenment tradition (with its focus on individual rational capacity) misses. Herder made exactly this point about cultures: their value is not reducible to the individuals within them.

## The Constructive Implication

The USC researchers' proposed remedy — deliberate diversification of LLM training data to reflect actual human cognitive diversity — is in effect asking for a Herderian AI. Not a universal rational agent, but a tool that can participate in many different cognitive traditions without reducing them to one.

Whether this is achievable is an open question. But framing it this way makes clear what is at stake: not just better AI performance metrics, but the preservation of the cognitive diversity that makes collective human intelligence adaptive.
