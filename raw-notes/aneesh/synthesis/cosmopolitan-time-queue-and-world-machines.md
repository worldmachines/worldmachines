# Cosmopolitan Time Queue and World Machines

A cross-source synthesis between two independent diagnoses of modernity's signature operation: **the conversion of spatial coexistence into temporal sequence**. The first comes from human geography ([[doreen-massey]], *For Space*, 2005, Part Three); the second from Venkatesh Rao's "World Machines" cluster (*Engineering Liveness*, *The Divergence Machine I–II*, *The Modernity Machine I–III*, *The World Machines Project*; see [[engineering-liveness]], [[divergence-machine]], [[the-modernity-machine]], [[world-machines-framework]]).

## The convergent diagnosis

Both accounts identify a specifically modern operation:

| Massey (geographer's lineage) | Rao (machinic-historian's lineage) |
|---|---|
| "Spatialising modernity" — the move by which different coexisting places are read as different stages of one development | "History Machine" — the machinery of modernity that processes messy spatial coexistence into a single legible narrative |
| "Cosmopolitan time queue" — societies ranked on one developmental line, "ahead" or "behind" | "Modernity Machine" / "Divergence Machine" — the engine that produces "history" (in the singular) out of contingent coexistence |
| Foreclosure of alternative futures — the future is already known by the queue | The machine has been running, but it is *a* machine — it could have been otherwise, and may yet be different |

Both diagnose the **denial of coevalness** as the operation's hallmark — Massey via [[johannes-fabian]] (anthropology); Rao via his reading of how modernity rewrites the temporality of its "others." See [[coevalness]].

## Where they differ

The accounts emerge from different traditions and have different emphases:

- **Massey's lineage** is post-Marxist, feminist, and post-structuralist (Mouffe, Laclau, Fabian, Bryant). The diagnosis is **political-ontological**: modernity is a particular way of *imagining* space that has political effects. Politics requires philosophical work.
- **Rao's lineage** is more pragmatist / systems-theoretic / cultural-historical (with literary and design-thinking influences). The diagnosis is **machinic**: modernity is a working mechanism — an engineered apparatus of sense-making, story-telling, and coordination — that can be analysed, redesigned, and replaced.

Where Massey says "the cosmopolitan time queue is an *imagination* of space with political consequences," Rao would say "the History Machine is a *running mechanism* with components that can be specified."

The vocabularies are different; the structural diagnosis is the same.

## What each adds to the other

### Rao for Massey

Massey's ontology of space as multiplicity is powerful but **abstract**. Rao's machinic vocabulary specifies *how* the cosmopolitan time queue gets implemented: which institutions, which narratives, which protocols, which built environments do the work. The cosmopolitan time queue is not a free-floating ideology; it is *engineered* by particular machines (newspapers, universities, world-historical sociology, GDP-rankings, "developing country" categories). Rao's history machine gives Massey's diagnosis its operational gears.

See [[history-machines-in-the-margins]], [[the-modernity-machine]], [[the-divergence-machine]].

### Massey for Rao

Rao's machinic ontology is structurally agile but **politically thin**. Massey's [[power-geometry]] gives Rao's machines their political content: which agents the machine empowers, which it dispossesses, which trajectories it makes audible, which it silences. The Divergence Machine has been processing spatial coexistence into temporal sequence — but not neutrally; the queue has been *Western*, the front of the line has been *European*, and the cost has been borne by the back. Massey's framework forces the machinic diagnosis into political accountability.

See also [[power-geometry]], [[aspatial-globalisation]].

## The shared political stake

Both accounts argue that the diagnosed operation is **not inevitable** and is open to political challenge. Rao's machinic framing makes this concrete: machines can be modified, replaced, decommissioned. Massey's ontological framing makes it constitutive: space is *actually* the dimension of coexisting multiplicity; the queue is *always* a misrecognition; the political work is to **recognise** what is in fact the case.

The convergent political claim:

> Modernity has run as a machine for processing spatial difference into temporal sequence. The queue it has produced — Europe at the front, the rest "developing" — is neither true nor inevitable. **Other globalisations are possible**, and recognising this is part of the work of building them. (cf. Massey 2005: 99; Rao throughout the World Machines essays)
