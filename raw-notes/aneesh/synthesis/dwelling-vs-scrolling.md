# Dwelling vs. Scrolling — Two Modes of Inhabiting Text

## The Opposition

Across [[Books Are Dwellings]] and [[The Small God of the Internet]], two fundamentally opposed relationships to text emerge:

| **Dwelling** | **Scrolling** |
|---|---|
| Books, RSS feeds | Infinite scroll, algorithmic feeds |
| Bounded space | Formless, no edge |
| Entered by choice | Entered by default |
| Demands devotion | Demands only reflexive attention |
| Author's effort creates the space | Platform's algorithm creates the stream |
| Memory is possible | Memory is discouraged |
| Silence is part of the experience | Silence is impossible by design |

## Spatial Ontology

[[Aneesh Sathe]] in [[Books Are Dwellings]] argues that books are *places*: "Books are dwellings. Books are places." The spatial metaphor is the key move. A dwelling:
- Has an interior you can inhabit
- Was built with effort for someone to live in
- Can be returned to
- Can be shared with others who then find "doorways between rooms"

The [[The Gloomwheel|Gloomwheel]] is the anti-dwelling: it has no interior, no boundaries, no quiet rooms. It is a highway, and the billboard flashes past before you can inhabit it.

## RSS as Managed River

RSS is an intermediate form. The "River of Posts" is spatial (a flowing body of water) but bounded (a finite daily bundle). The feed arrives in the morning with "twenty-seven items"; you read two, save three, "let the rest drift by with the calm certainty that rivers do not take offense." This is *managed* flow — not formless scrolling but a contained current.

The RSS feed is more dwelling than billboard: it has a rhythm, an edge (Mark All As Read), and a silence (tomorrow's bundle has not yet arrived). It is a room that floods gently each morning and drains by evening.

## The Book Club as Architecture

In [[Books Are Dwellings]], Sathe adds a social dimension: "each book becomes a room I walk through with others — and when we meet to talk, it feels like discovering a doorway between those rooms." The book club transforms individual dwellings into a connected architectural space — something like the [[The Cloister Web|Cloister Web]] at a domestic scale.

## Implications for Creative Work

If we accept the dwelling/scrolling opposition, it has implications beyond reading:
- **The Cloister Web** is where ideas are *housed* before being released — a dwelling for half-formed thoughts
- **The tortured artist's studio** is a dwelling; social media is a highway. The [[The Tortured Artist Is So Yesterday|tortured artist]] suffered in a dwelling; today's creator suffers in traffic.
- **Authorship as courage** ([[Authorship as Courage]]) is the act of inviting others into your dwelling — committing a specific space to public inhabitation

## Essay Seeds

1. **Reading Environments as Architecture**: Compare the phenomenology of the library, the bookshelf, the RSS reader, the Twitter feed, and the Kindle as architectural forms — what does each one do to attention?
2. **The Bachelard Connection**: Gaston Bachelard's *Poetics of Space* theorizes nests, corners, drawers as intimate spaces. Map his taxonomy onto digital reading environments.
3. **Can Substack Be a Dwelling?**: Long-form digital writing exists between book and billboard — when does it become inhabitable?
