# Healthcare as Wright's Law Exception

## The Pattern

Industries that follow [[Wright's Law]] share a structural feature: learning compounds because knowledge diffuses rapidly across practitioners through shared infrastructure — factory floors, supplier networks, engineering communities, open publication norms. Every doubling of cumulative output builds on the collective learning of the previous doubling.

Healthcare has the opposite structure:
- Individual clinicians develop effective hacks through years of practice
- These hacks stay private or spread slowly through informal networks
- Formal diffusion requires publication, guideline revision, training program update, and deployment to new practitioners — a generational lag
- Regulatory barriers add additional friction at each step
- Outcome: the field does not inherit the efficiency gains of individual excellence

## Why This Matters Beyond Economics

The Wright's Law exception in healthcare is not merely a cost problem — it is an equity problem. The most sophisticated clinical knowledge is concentrated at research centers (like [[Mayo Clinic]]) and individual expert practitioners. Patients in underserved areas or who cannot access elite institutions effectively receive care from an earlier era of medical knowledge.

The insight from [[Dancing on the Shoulders of Giants]]: "the broader field doesn't inherit those gains until knowledge filters down into education, training, and regulation — a generational delay." This is the mechanism behind healthcare's learning rate anomaly.

## The Composite AI as Intervention

The [[Composite AI Architecture (Experts + LLM + KG)]] is the proposed mechanism for breaking the Wright's Law exception:

1. **Expertise extraction**: [[Cognitive Task Analysis]] and related methods surface tacit expert knowledge
2. **Externalization**: [[Knowledge Graphs]] encode this knowledge in a form that doesn't die with the expert or stay siloed at one institution
3. **Accessibility**: LLMs provide natural language navigation — practitioners at any institution can traverse the accumulated expertise of the field
4. **Accumulation**: As more expert knowledge is extracted and encoded, the KG compounds — every practitioner's contribution is immediately available to all others

This is the shared knowledge infrastructure that manufacturing sectors have through physical co-presence on factory floors and inter-firm labor mobility.

## Essay Seed

**Thesis candidate**: Healthcare's resistance to Wright's Law is not a fundamental property of the domain but a consequence of specific institutional structures — private knowledge, generational diffusion, regulatory friction. The [[Composite AI Architecture (Experts + LLM + KG)]] is the first technically plausible proposal to change those structures. The question is not whether it works technically but whether the institutional incentives align to build it.
