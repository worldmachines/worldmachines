# Not-Knowing as Capture-Resistance

A cross-cutting reading of four separated moments in Ingold's [[on-not-knowing-and-paying-attention]] that, assembled, describe the World Machines [[legibility-as-machine-core]] machine *from the inside* -- from the position of the subject it processes -- and name the counter-move. No single section of the essay states this; each supplies one part.

## The assembled mechanism

1. **Knowledge as citadel.** Knowledge "adds another stone to the walls with which it shores itself up," fixing the world within concepts "so they no longer trouble us" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-man-in-the-moon-knowledge-and-wisdom]]). This is legibility's *cognitive* form: to make the world readable is to make it a walled, defended interior.
2. **Extraction ethics.** Normal science takes data "by force or subterfuge" and then "cuts and runs, turning our backs on the world" -- a protocol Ingold calls "deeply unethical" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-turn-towards-world-uncertainty-possibility]]). Legibility as *harvest*.
3. **Immunising methodology.** Scientific method is a set of protocols for holding the world "at a distance," meant to "immunise the investigator from any infection arising from too close a contact with the phenomena" ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-passage-of-generations-astonishment-surprise]]). Legibility as *quarantine*.
4. **Manufactured ignorance.** Following Ranciere, "education, science and the state" are "all-powerful machines for the production of ignorance": children, laypeople, and indigenous people are made to feel ignorant "as a prelude and an excuse for their education," with educational success measured by how faithfully that ignorance reproduces in the next generation ([[ingold-2023-possible-world-real-problems-real-freedom-conclusion]]). Legibility as *self-reproducing institution*.

Read across these four notes, legibility is not merely imposed on the world; it is reproduced *inside the subject* as manufactured ignorance, and the "certain knowledge" and "fake freedom" the institutions offer are exactly what the WMP names as legibility -- measurable, orderable, predictively certain knowledge that promises control ([[legibility-as-machine-core]], [[legibility-vs-complexity-the-master-tension]]).

## Why "not-knowing" is capture-resistance, not deficit

Ingold's counter-move is not more knowledge but a *stance*: attention, correspondence, and the deliberate holding-open of "not-knowing," which he insists is "entirely different" from ignorance. That stance is structurally the WMP property of [[capture-resistance]]. The soul's undefended openness is precisely what cannot be fixed, accounted for, and put safely away; and Ingold's "real freedom" -- attunement to "the ways things want to go" rather than willed mastery of them -- is the Spinozist [[Conatus]] move that capture regimes try to flatten. Where the legibility machine buys predictability by immunising itself against contact, not-knowing keeps the living process responsive by staying exposed. It is the same trade the wiki tracks under [[legibility-vs-complexity-the-master-tension]] -- rendered here as a phenomenology of the person rather than a sociology of the state.
