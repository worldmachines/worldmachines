# Dwelling as Philosophical Concept

## Heidegger's Original Formulation

From *Building Dwelling Thinking* (in *Poetry, Language, Thought*, 1971):

> "Man's relation to locations, and through locations to spaces, inheres in his dwelling. The relationship between man and space is none other than dwelling, strictly thought and spoken."

Dwelling is not a passive state — it is the *primary* way of being in relation to the world. We don't first exist and then dwell; dwelling is the mode of our existence.

Developed in [[Jan. 7, 2025: Building Dwelling Thinking]].

## Place, Location, Space

[[Martin Heidegger]] distinguishes:
- **Locations**: specific, ordered sites of human activity
- **Spaces**: domains opened up from locations (reinterpreted in the source as "AI-space," "biotech-space")
- **Boundary**: not where something stops, but "where something begins its presencing"

The reinterpretation in the source is deliberate: places are anything with order and purpose *as thought of by the user*. Spaces emerge from thought extended through the affordances of places.

## The Building-Thinking-Dwelling Triangle

All three are necessary and mutually dependent:
- **Building** without **thinking**: produces structures that constrain rather than enable
- **Thinking** without **building**: remains abstract, unable to realize dwelling
- **Dwelling** as the synthesis: only achieved when building and thinking "listen to one another"

For knowledge workers: the analysis, visualization, or product they build is a place where others will dwell. The ethical demand follows from this.

## Sparing as the Positive Obligation

Heidegger's definition of "to free" is the normative core: *to spare* — to leave something in its own nature, to return it to its being. Good dwelling preserves and protects.

Applied to product design (see [[Sparing and Freeing]]): a product's primary obligation is to enable the user's freedom, not the product's own logic.

## What Dwelling Is Not

- Mere presence or occupancy
- Compliance with a tool's structure
- The constriction that comes from products that demand users conform to them
