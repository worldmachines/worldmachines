# Virtual Proper Being

## Definition

[[levi-bryant]] introduces *virtual proper being* as one of the two fundamental dimensions of any machine. The virtual proper being of a machine is the system of operations of which it is *capable* — its powers, in [[george-molnar]]'s sense. These constitute the "proper being" of the machine in that machines are what they are capable of doing.

"The virtual proper being of a machine is the operations of which it is *capable*. These constitute the 'proper being' of the machine in that machines are what they are capable of doing. They are 'virtual' in the sense that a machine can possess these operations without *exercising* them." (*Onto-Cartography*, p. 40)

## Virtual vs. Local

The split between virtual proper being and *local manifestation* is the fundamental ontological division within any machine:

- **Virtual proper being**: pure operations in their formal being; the powers a machine possesses regardless of whether they are exercised
- **Local manifestation**: the results produced by these operations when they actually operate on particular inputs

This split follows from [[george-molnar]]'s account of powers: powers are actual, intrinsic, and objective even when unexercised. A sleeping cat does not engage its operations of vision because its eyelids are shut — but the power of vision is still actual (p. 41). Iron in outer space (no oxygen) does not produce rust — but its power to oxidize remains (p. 41).

## The "Virtual" in Deleuze

The term "virtual" is deliberately Deleuzian. For Deleuze, the virtual is fully real — it is not merely potential or merely possible. The virtual is the real but not-yet-actualized. The virtual proper being of a machine is its real system of powers, which may never be fully actualized in any particular encounter.

This Deleuzian virtual is distinct from the digitally "virtual" (simulated). Virtuality here means: genuinely real, possessed by the machine as an intrinsic feature, but not reducible to any particular exercise or manifestation.

## Why "Proper" Being

The word "proper" signals that the virtual proper being *belongs to the machine* — it is not dependent on relations with other machines, on being observed, or on being exercised. This is the machine's own being, its depth, its interiority. It cannot be read off from any particular encounter.

This connects to the [[withdrawal-harman]] thesis: the virtual proper being of a machine is always more than what any particular coupling reveals. No single manifestation exhausts the machine's powers. Harman: the real object always withdraws from its relations. Bryant: the virtual proper being always exceeds the local manifestation.

## Implications

1. **Experimentation**: Since the virtual proper being of a machine exceeds any particular manifestation, knowledge of machines requires experimentation — varying couplings to reveal different powers. Spinoza: "we do not know what a machine can do." (p. 46)

2. **Plasticity**: Powers are not fixed. The virtual proper being of a machine can change through couplings, damage, evolution, and learning (pp. 45–46). See [[rigid-plastic-machines]].

3. **Pluripotency**: The virtual proper being of a highly plastic machine (like a great work of art) is characterized by pluripotency — the capacity to produce genuinely novel manifestations across different contexts. See [[pluripotency-and-exaptation]].

4. **Alien phenomenology**: The alien phenomenologist investigates what a machine's virtual proper being consists of — what powers it has and which flows trigger which operations. See [[alien-phenomenology]].
