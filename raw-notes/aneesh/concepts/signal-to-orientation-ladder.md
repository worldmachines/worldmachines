# Signal-to-Orientation Ladder

## The Framework

A four-level analytical ladder connecting raw environmental input to evolutionary need:

```
Signal → Information → Orientation → Biology
```

| Level | Definition | Social Media Analog |
|---|---|---|
| **Signal** | Any pattern standing out from background noise | Headline, notification badge, unexpected data point |
| **Information** | Signal parsed and interpreted; meaning attached | "This headline matters to my work / contradicts my belief" |
| **Orientation** | Updated internal map — "where I stand, what to do next" | Clearer sense of how new knowledge fits with prior understanding |
| **Biological need** | Evolutionary pressure toward accurate mental maps | Organisms that orient accurately survive; disorientation = discomfort |

## Why the Ladder Matters

Social media companies are accused of being addictive. The ladder reframes the diagnosis:
- The problem is not addiction (compulsive return to a pleasurable stimulus).
- The problem is **starvation**: platforms provide signal that gestures toward information but never delivers genuine orientation.
- Users keep scrolling not from pleasure but from unfulfilled biological need.

This is [[Hank Green]]'s thesis rendered mechanistically.

## The Broken Ladder

Social platforms optimize for engagement at the *signal* level. They provide:
- High-arousal signals (outrage, novelty, urgency).
- Enough information-shape to trigger the orientation-seeking response.
- Not enough genuine information to complete the orientation cycle.

Result: users are trapped in an unfulfilled loop — signal fires the biological drive, information fails to deliver, orientation never arrives, hunger persists.

## The Fix: Information-Shaped Diet

An "information-shaped content diet" — engaging with curiosity and [[Socratic Thinking|Socratic posture]] — completes the ladder. Each exchange that delivers genuine orientation satisfies the biological need, reducing compulsive signal-seeking.

## Relation to Place-Making

Orientation = finding one's place. The ladder is the [[Space and Place in Cognition]] framework restated in biological terms:
- Signal = noise in space.
- Information = signal that plants a map-point.
- Orientation = knowing which place you are in.
- Biology = the evolutionary pressure to maintain accurate place-knowledge.
