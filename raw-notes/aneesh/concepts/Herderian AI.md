# Herderian AI

Herderian AI is a proposed architectural response to LLM homogenization, named for [[Johann Gottfried Herder]]'s argument that each culture embodies an incommensurable form of human excellence.

## Properties

- **Plural by design** — not one model but many, each trained predominantly within a specific linguistic-cultural tradition
- **Genuinely polyglot** — different architectures reflecting different commitments about what reasoning *is*, not just different language masks on a single model
- **Refuses lossless translation** — flags what doesn't survive the crossing between traditions
- **Resists universalization** — produces *this tradition's* answer, not *the* answer

## Failure Mode

Balkanization. Each model becomes a silo. Translation between them is lossy and nobody bridges the gap. This is [[The Jena Circle]]'s disintegration at scale: diversity without productive contact collapses into mere separation. Herder himself was a pacifist pluralist, but the political inheritors of his ideas built [[Nationalism]]. The gap between "each culture is incommensurable" and "our culture is supreme" turns out to be tragically small.

[[Humboldtian AI]] — the compositional alternative. Neither resolves the problem alone. See [[the-cosmos-and-the-model]].
