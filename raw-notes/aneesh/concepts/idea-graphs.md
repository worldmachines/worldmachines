# Idea-Graphs

**Idea-Graphs** are LLM-consumable knowledge structures designed to encode political and intellectual ideas in ways that large language models can process, adapt, and propagate to individual recipients. The term is used in the [[Cloister Web]] essay to describe a new genre of political writing optimized for LLM intermediation.

## What Makes an Idea-Graph Different from Regular Text

Regular political text is written for human readers: it uses rhetoric, narrative, and affect. An **idea-graph** is structured for machine processing:
- Entities (concepts, arguments, historical figures) are explicitly named
- Relationships between them are made explicit (rather than implied through prose)
- The structure enables LLMs to traverse the graph and generate *personalized* versions adapted to specific recipients

This is the political application of [[Knowledge Graphs]]: the same way a biomedical KG structures medical knowledge for AI reasoning, an idea-graph structures political ideas for LLM-mediated persuasion.

## The Cloister Web Mechanism

From [[Cloister Web — Reshaping the Political Maidan]]: future intellectuals will create:
> "LLM-consumable idea-graphs and knowledge structures. These structures will allow for a more nuanced and interconnected understanding of complex concepts."

The intellectual plants the idea in the [[Cloister Web]] as an idea-graph. The LLM then:
1. Traverses the graph's latent connections
2. Generates personalized presentations for different recipients
3. Propagates the idea in "ways previously unimaginable"

## Relationship to Ontologies

The idea-graph is the political analog of a [[Domain Ontology]]: both are formal, machine-readable representations of a knowledge domain. The ontology structures scientific or technical domains; the idea-graph structures argumentative or political domains.

This convergence suggests that future political intellectual work will require skills currently associated with knowledge engineering: defining entities, specifying relationships, structuring arguments for machine traversal.

## Implications for Political Communication

- **New genre**: Political writing bifurcates into human-readable rhetoric and machine-readable idea-graphs
- **Attribution challenges**: When LLMs adapt and propagate idea-graphs, where does the original idea end and the LLM's contribution begin?
- **Power asymmetry**: Actors who understand idea-graph construction will have significant advantages in the [[Cloister Web]] ecosystem
