# IEMP Model

Michael Mann's *The Sources of Social Power* (1986–2012, 4 vols.) proposes that societies are constituted by four overlapping, interacting networks of power:

1. **Ideological** — control over meaning, norms, and aesthetic/ritual practices
2. **Economic** — control over extraction, transformation, distribution, and consumption of resources
3. **Military** — control over organized lethal violence
4. **Political** — control over centralized, territorial regulation

The critical move is Mann's refusal of mono-causal explanation. Against Marxist economic determinism (the base determines the superstructure "in the last instance") and Weberian cultural determinism (ideas and rationalization drive historical change), Mann insists that the four sources are genuinely autonomous. None is ontologically prior. Different historical configurations place different sources in the leading position: ideological power dominated the Axial Age religious revolutions; military power dominated the Roman imperial expansion; economic power dominates the modern capitalist era — but never exclusively.

## Diagnostic Utility

The IEMP model functions as a diagnostic tool for identifying what each civilizational "machine" actually runs on. Applied to the [[world-machines]] framework:

- The [[bronze-age-palace-economy]] ran primarily on political power (centralized redistribution) backed by ideological power (divine kingship)
- The [[axial-age]] represents a revolution in ideological power
- The [[modernity-machine]] is characterized by the ascendancy of economic power, with military and political power increasingly subordinated to its logic
- The [[divergence-machine]] may represent a reconfiguration in which ideological and economic power decouple from territorial political power

## Limitations

Mann's framework is better at diagnosis than prediction. It describes *what* power sources are operative but does not specify *how* they interact dynamically or what drives transitions between configurations. For that, one needs complementary frameworks like [[secular-cycles]] or the world-machines periodization itself.
