# Hunter vs. Dancer Curiosity

## The Distinction

Sathe (see [[Dancing on the Shoulders of Giants]]) draws on cognitive research to distinguish two styles of intellectual exploration:

- **Hunter curiosity**: tight conceptual clusters, goal-directed exploration, efficient at finding answers within known territory. Characteristic of STEM education and professional expertise.
- **Dancer curiosity**: associative, ranging, high *forward flow* — significant conceptual distance between successive thoughts. More likely to generate genuinely novel connections.

"Forward flow" is a technical term from curiosity research: the degree to which one thought leads to a conceptually distant next thought, rather than a nearby one. High forward flow is closer to [[Paul Feyerabend]]'s epistemological "anything goes" — the claim that scientific discovery is not governed by a single method but requires methodological anarchy.

## Why This Matters for Expertise

The hunter/dancer distinction complicates the standard picture of expertise: deep expertise optimizes for hunter-style efficiency, which is productive within a paradigm but poor at generating cross-domain connections that produce paradigm shifts.

Novel breakthroughs require dancer-style exploration — what the author calls "walking" rather than climbing. This is the metaphor behind "Dancing on the Shoulders of Giants" vs. the traditional "standing on the shoulders of giants": standing is static accumulation, dancing is active traversal.

## Implications for AI

LLMs, in their current form, mimic a kind of hyper-hunter style: they retrieve and recombine in statistically predictable ways, producing outputs close to the mean of their training data. This makes them useful for execution within known territory but not for generating genuinely novel conceptual leaps.

The [[Composite AI Architecture (Experts + LLM + KG)]] leaves dancer-curiosity with the human expert and gives the LLM the hunter role: navigating structured knowledge efficiently.
