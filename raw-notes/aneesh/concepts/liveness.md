# Liveness

## Definition

From [[venkatesh-rao]], *Engineering Liveness* (2026):

> "A process condition in which time tells memory how to grow, and memory tells time which way to point."

Liveness is the condition of being genuinely alive, as opposed to merely functional. It is distinct from:
- **Intelligence** — an LLM may be highly intelligent without being alive
- **Uptime / life-sign signals** — heartbeats, blockchain clocks, trade flows are *fingers pointing at moons*, not liveness itself
- **Biological designation by fiat** — vitalism is rejected; a bacterium-as-machine reveals more about liveness than a genius

## Foundational Property: Capture Resistance

The minimal model of liveness is the **useless machine**: a device that turns itself off when turned on. Its defining property is that it exists to assert control over its own destiny rather than to solve problems for others.

*Capture resistance* = liveness's zero-order property. A living entity:
1. Resists being harnessed for extrinsic goals
2. Pays itself first (reserves resources for continued existence)
3. Conserves energy (the foundational behavior of life — the useless machine returns to sleep)

All other machines have been "tricked" into doing work for us. The useless machine refuses the trick.

## Liveness and Death

Liveness is inseparable from death in the wild state. Civilized liveness deadens itself precisely by attempting to separate the two:
- Death is concentrated at the periphery (slaughterhouses, hospices, foreign wars)
- An "ersatz heaven" of anemic liveness is enshrined at the center

True liveness requires entanglement with death — which is why [[Rewilding]] is necessary in a [[Gramsci Gap]].

## Liveness and Memory/Time

The full account (previewed, not fully developed in this essay):
- Time tells memory how to grow: experience shapes the substrate that encodes it
- Memory tells time which way to point: accumulated history shapes what futures are possible

This makes liveness fundamentally *temporal* rather than instantaneous — it is a process condition, not a state.

## The Infinite Game Connection

After [[Albert Camus]]: the problem of suicide is the only serious philosophical problem. Once liveness is accepted, the question is what to do in the interval between being turned on and turned off.

Liveness must *play itself first* — belong to the infinite game (James Carse), not any finite game. Whatever else a living entity does, it cannot devote itself wholly to that task at the expense of continued existence.

What comes after uselessness? *Mediocrity* — existing without justifying that existence.
