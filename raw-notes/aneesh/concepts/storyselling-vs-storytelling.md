# Storyselling vs. Storytelling

[[Byung-Chul Han]]'s concept from *The Crisis of Narration* (2024):

- **Storytelling**: narrative that creates a shared reality, builds community, transmits culture.
- **Storyselling**: narrative instrumentalized to create consumers — the form of story is preserved, but the function is commercial extraction.

## The Diagnosis

Han argues that we have lost the capacity for authentic storytelling. Social media, marketing, and the attention economy have colonized narrative form. Stories now serve consumption: they are optimized to produce desire, identity attachment, or platform engagement rather than to illuminate shared human experience.

## The Loss

What's lost is a particular kind of shared reality — the sense that a story *happened* and now we all live in its wake. Storyselling produces no such common world. It produces individual consumers who feel they've been told a story but have only been sold something.

## The Influencer Instantiation

The comparison in [[Looking Forward to Montaigne]]: influencers perform authenticity in a manner that is structurally storyselling — the content is calibrated for engagement, not for truth-telling. By contrast, [[Michel de Montaigne]] wrote with frank, meandering honesty that had no commercial purpose. He is paradoxically more alive.

## Han's Silence on Solutions

Notably, Han offers no prescriptions in *The Crisis of Narration*. The author of [[Looking Forward to Montaigne]] supplies a tentative one: Montaigne-style personal writing — small, honest, non-teleological — as a counter-practice.

## Relation to the Old Internet

The "old internet" — personal homepages, early blogs, niche forums — operated in a pre-storyselling mode. Writing was done for self-expression or community rather than conversion. The nostalgia for this mode is a marker of the felt loss Han describes.

## Open Questions

- Can authentic storytelling survive on platforms structurally optimized for engagement?
- Is Montaigne's mode truly scalable, or does it require structural conditions (no dependence on audience, no monetization) that are increasingly rare?
- Is Han's diagnosis too totalizing? Pockets of authentic storytelling clearly persist.
