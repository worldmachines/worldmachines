# Argument of Progress

**Originator**: [[venkatesh-rao]] (Divergence Machine II, Feb 2026)

## Definition

The **Argument of Progress** is a dynamic, pluralist discourse reflecting a changing understanding of a rapidly *expanding* scope of experienced reality. It is the [[divergence-machine]]'s alternative to [[fiat-progress]].

Key features:
- **Non-stationary**: The "value" (y) of the function is not a stationary distribution — it's always moving because the data (x) keeps expanding
- **Phenomenological, not metaphysical**: Rooted in history moving fast enough to require significant accommodations of novelty within single lifetimes
- **Not a debate**: Not a thesis-antithesis-synthesis spiral, not a zero-sum game with winners and losers
- **Partially cooperative**: A "partially cooperative mutual exploration of novelty as it emerges on an expanding frontier"

> "The Argument of Progress is the data, *and* the process of making sense of it, through the discovery or construction of new patterns of thought."

## The y=f(x) Framework

- **x** = the rapidly expanding scope of stuff you have to make sense of ("the world" in "world view")
- **f** = the sense-making process — must be *constructed*, not merely received
- **y** = the resulting understanding of reality / value

In the 17th century: if you heard that Galileo had seen moons around Jupiter (new x), you had to come up with *new* ways of making sense of it (new f), perhaps by considering Copernican vs. Ptolemaic ideas. You would not find x or f in the Bible or Aristotle. And y might not be a religious confirmation, but a new yardstick *for* value — calling for ontological updates.

## Contrast with Fiat Progress

| Fiat Progress | Argument of Progress |
|---------------|----------------------|
| x is fixed (received tradition) | x expands within lifetimes |
| f is received (interpretive tradition) | f must be constructed |
| y is sacred/profane per existing ontology | y is newly constructed per expanding reality |
| Debate-structured: optimism vs. pessimism | Not a debate: mutual exploration |
| State-underwritten, religious backing | Rooted in expanding experiential reality |
| Institutionalized 1200-1600 | Seeded 1600-2000, mainstream ~2020-2025 |

## What It Is NOT

The Argument of Progress is not:
- A Hegelian dialectic (presupposes societal moral concerns that the AoP bracket)
- A Marxist dialectic (similar problem — "class struggle" as fixed frame)
- Whig history
- Leibnizean optimism ("best of all possible worlds")
- Progress Studies (Collison-Cowen, Thielen determinate optimism, a16z American Dynamism)
- An infinite game in Carse's sense (though closer — the AoP is about continuing the game, but requires genuine ontological dread, not conversion to "keeping the game going" as a positive value)

All of these are "Fiat Progress narratives in disguise" — degenerate cases where x is treated as constant and f as received.

## The Modernist Error and the Divergentist Move

**Modernist error** ([[gottfried-wilhelm-leibniz]]): When ontological dread arrives (reality expands faster than your frameworks), commit prematurely to existing scaffolding. Resolve dread into optimism or pessimism. This is "not even wrong" — it fails to register that the question has changed.

**Divergentist move** ([[baruch-spinoza]]): Accept the dread. Avoid premature commitments. Live in ontological doubt while the nature of reality shakes itself out. Rebuild functional equivalents of good/bad categories from scratch every time reality expands sufficiently abruptly.

> "The fundamental divergentist move is to simply accept the dread, and avoid premature commitments, choosing instead to live in ontological doubt while the nature of reality shakes itself out in your mound."

## Intellectual Genealogy

- **Spinoza** (1632-1677): first divergentist, accepted ontological doubt as a state of being
- **Hume** (1711-1776): radical naturalist-empiricist, set aside causation for near-pure phenomenology; "original philosopher of the AI age"
- **Adam Smith** (1723-1790): applied same orientation to economics — the invisible hand as emergent from noisy messy reality, not divine design
- **Darwin** (1809-1882): made divergence and variety load-bearing features of reality itself; completed the Hume arc by assaulting the *secular* philosophical foundations of the modernity machine
- **Covid + AI** (2020-2025): made the Argument of Progress mainstream

## Progress as Knowing and Doing

One reason to use the word "progress": root it in the *growth of knowledge* — appreciative and instrumental — rather than material plenty or spiritual well-being.

> "Leibniz and Spinoza were among the first humans in history to embody Progress as a state of *knowing about* and *doing in* the real world, accessed through increasingly capable instruments."

Whether or not they were materially better off or spiritually fulfilled is irrelevant. What matters: did they have better ways of knowing and doing?
