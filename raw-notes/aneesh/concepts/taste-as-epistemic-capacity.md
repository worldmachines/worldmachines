# Taste as Epistemic Capacity

## Definition

"Taste" in Sathe's usage (see [[On Being Good vs. Knowing Good]]) is not aesthetic preference but the cognitive capacity that defines genuine expertise: the ability to make good judgments about which options are better, which questions are worth asking, and when the stated goal is itself the problem. It is what separates a good advisor from a knowledgeable reference book.

Taste is the dimension along which the [[Expertise]] concept splits into two components:
- **Knowledge**: propositional, transmissible, partially approximated by LLMs
- **Taste**: experiential, embodied, not yet approximable by current AI

## Why AI Lacks Taste

Sathe's argument: "ChatGPT/LLMs and their generative counterparts exhibit confidence but, by design, lack real-world experience." The mechanism is direct — taste develops through feedback loops between action and consequence in the world. An LLM trained on text has processed descriptions of experiences but has not had them. The distillation loses the most important signal: what it felt like to be wrong.

This connects to [[Nick Cave]]'s objection to AI creativity — not merely that AI can't make great art, but that the process of making imperfect art is constitutive of the judgment required to recognize greatness.

## Implications

- **AI as Tool, not Advisor**: The appropriate use of LLMs is to eliminate tedious execution while the human retains advisory judgment. See [[AI as Tool vs. Advisor]].
- **The Knowledge Ladder**: Sathe invokes the arithmetic → calculator → spreadsheet progression: each tool assumed internalized competence at the prior layer. Jumping to LLMs without building the judgment layer is analogous to using a spreadsheet without understanding arithmetic.
- **Cultural formation**: A "new culture" around AI tools must cultivate taste in users — the capacity to recognize when AI output is wrong, which is itself a form of expertise.

## Open Questions

- Is there a threshold of complexity where knowledge compression begins to approximate taste? (The [[Composite AI Architecture (Experts + LLM + KG)]] implicitly argues no — taste stays with the human.)
- What would a training regime for taste look like in AI contexts? RLHF attempts something like this, but Sathe does not engage with it.
- Can [[Cognitive Task Analysis]] extract taste-adjacent tacit knowledge into forms KGs can represent?
