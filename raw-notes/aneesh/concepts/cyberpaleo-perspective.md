# Cyberpaleo Perspective

## Definition

The **cyberpaleo perspective** (coined/used by Patrick Atwater) is a way of seeing the contemporary digital world by asking what it would look like to a Paleolithic ancestor — revealing the strangeness of arrangements we take for granted.

The canonical example: "Imagine explaining to a Paleolithic ancestor that you drive a machine to sit in another cave, stare at a glowing rectangle, and then — get this — you drive back to a different cave full of metal weights and artificial lighting in order to 'exercise.' They might laugh. Or simply blink in quiet confusion. Why not just move and make and carry and dance and walk and lift and live where you already are?"

## Core Move

The perspective shift: seeing "our blurred world of bits" through the lens of embodied, place-based, integrated living. The move makes the arbitrary visible by contrasting it with the baseline.

Applications:
- Remote work: are our current arrangements of separated work/life/exercise caves sane by any long-run standard?
- Digital communication: what is lost when embodied, in-person social fabric is replaced by text/video?
- Food, health, rhythm: what would Paleolithic ancestral physiology make of contemporary routines?

## Connection to the Liveness Machine

Atwater proposes cyberpaleo as a "slow take" lens for understanding what the [[Liveness Machine]] might be pointing toward: "just based on vibes the cyberpaleo approach of taking a broader look at work and play seems apropos."

The intuition: if the LM is about *liveness* — self-organization, vitality, aliveness — then cyberpaleo is a way to identify what has been deadened by modernity and divergence, and what the LM might revivify.

## Methodological Parallel: Paul Salopek's Walk

Patrick Atwater explicitly pairs cyberpaleo with [[Paul Salopek — Out of Eden Walk]]: a man walking the migration routes of *Homo sapiens* as slow journalism. Salopek's method is physically embodied, temporally stretched, and worm's-eye — a direct counter to the satellite-view, headlines-only mode of understanding the world.

Both cyberpaleo and the Out of Eden Walk operate at the level of **grounded, embodied particularity** rather than abstract civilizational analysis. They are slow takes that may reveal what fast takes miss.

## What's Missing

- The cyberpaleo frame is compelling but underdeveloped as a research program
- It risks nostalgia — the Paleolithic baseline was not uniformly good (violence, disease, short lives)
- The translation from "Paleolithic wisdom" to "appropriate 21st century arrangement" is unclear
- No connection made to the literature on evolutionary medicine, ancestral health movements, or embodied cognition
