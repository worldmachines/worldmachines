# Configurancy

**Coined by**: [[venkatesh-rao]]
**Appears in**: [[The Shelter as Epistemic Engine]] (Sathe, 2025-12-17)

## Definition

Configurancy is "the ongoing, relational, temporally unfolding process through which agents and worlds co-emerge." It is:
- **Non-teleological**: has no inherent goal or endpoint (unlike Heidegger's Care/*Sorge*)
- **Relational**: concerns how elements align with each other, not intrinsic properties of things
- **Process-based**: it is always unfolding, never a static state

The universe's configurancy shuffles relations without asking why. It is simply the structural logic of how things fit together.

## Sathe's Extension

Sathe synthesizes Configurancy with [[Yi-Fu Tuan]]'s phenomenological geography to produce a theory of place-making:

> **Place-making is the manual application of configurancy.**

Where the universe's configurancy is unconscious and goal-free, human place-making consciously engineers relational alignment to achieve intelligibility. When we build a home, write a paper, or establish a startup thesis, we are forcing the high-entropy flow of Space into a relational pattern that can be navigated.

"High configurancy" = a relational structure stable enough to evolve but fluid enough to move — the condition of the [[Explorer vs. Tourist|Explorer]], not the Tourist.

## The Three-Layer Stack

Sathe maps Configurancy onto a three-layer model of any epistemic home:
1. **Physical layer** — the hard interface with raw reality
2. **Protocol layer** — the relational instructions (Configurancy in action)
3. **Interface layer** — where alignment registers as understanding / meaning

## Contrast with Heidegger

Heidegger's *Sorge* (Care) is teleological — Dasein is always already oriented toward its ownmost possibilities. Configurancy is the same kind of relational-structural concept but stripped of teleology. It describes *how* things align without presupposing *what they're aligning toward*.
