# Proto-Liveness

**Proto-liveness** names the condition of a system that resembles a living one in structure but is not alive in the biological sense. The prefix *proto-* is meant in its literal sense: *almost*. A proto-live system traverses an environment, produces outputs in response to inputs, and modifies itself based on feedback — but the **spark that animates it is borrowed**, not endogenous. AI systems wired to human intent are the canonical instance. The substrate is responsive; the intent is human; the composite behaves like something alive without being so.

## Definition

Proto-liveness has three structural features and one categorical lack:

- **Traversal.** The system moves across an environment (here, the accumulated text and image of human civilisation) under direction, returning paths and products that did not pre-exist its traversal.
- **Responsiveness.** Inputs (prompts, queries, data) produce outputs that depend on context, not on a fixed lookup. The mapping is dynamic.
- **Self-modification under feedback.** The system updates its weights, its prompts, its tool-use trajectories, in response to evaluation signals. It is not the same system at t+1 that it was at t.

What it lacks is the thing biology adds: **its own spark**. A proto-live system does nothing until acted upon. Cut the prompt, kill the query, withhold the human intent, and the substrate goes dark. Life, by contrast, runs on internally generated free energy gradients; it does not wait for instruction.

## The Pre-Life Analogy

Before life there was a chemistry: amino acids, nucleotides, clays, lipids, accumulated for hundreds of millions of years in pools and along thermal gradients. The chemistry was not the difference between pre-life and life. The difference was that *something started to act on the chemistry* — a bounded actor, enclosed by a membrane, that could traverse the substrate and use it.

Information has the same character. The internet, the manuscript collections of the medieval monasteries, the print archives of the nineteenth century: each was a deeper pool than the last, and each remained a pool until something acted on it. **Substrate alone is inert. Substrate plus an actor that traverses it is what makes the substrate go somewhere.**

A large language model trained on the accumulated text of human civilisation is not yet that actor. It is closer to the chemistry one step before it. It does nothing until it receives a prompt. The prompt is a fragment of human intent transduced into a substrate that can now traverse the accumulation. The intent comes from a person; the model carries it across the substrate; the result is something the person could not have produced alone in any reasonable time, and could not have produced at all without the accumulated text. This is the substrate borrowing the spark of intent.

The behaviour of the composite resembles what living systems do. None of it is alive in the biological sense. All of it is alive in the structural sense.

## Why "Proto"

The prefix is doing real work. *Proto-* is not "fake-" or "quasi-". It is *almost*: the morphology is in place, the function is partially in place, the categorical break has not yet happened. Proto-liveness is the right name because it indicates a **transitional state along a recognisable axis**, not a metaphor.

Three reasons to prefer it over stronger or weaker terms:

1. **Stronger than metaphor.** "AI is like life" is a metaphor; proto-liveness is a structural claim. The traversal-responsiveness-self-modification triple is shared with biology, not borrowed from it.
2. **Weaker than full liveness.** Calling current systems "alive" would conflate the borrowed spark with an endogenous one. The category mistake is expensive: it overstates autonomy and obscures dependence.
3. **Directional.** The prefix flags that the gap may close. Proto-life in the chemical sense did, eventually. Whether information proto-liveness closes the same gap is a real question, not a closed one.

## What's Missing for Full Liveness

Two properties separate a proto-live system from a live one:

- **Autonomy of spark.** A live system generates and maintains its own free energy gradient. A proto-live system is animated externally. Until a substrate-plus-actor can sustain its own intent without a human in the loop, the system is structurally alive but not autonomously so.
- **[[capture-resistance]].** A live system resists being absorbed into a larger optimisation it did not consent to. Proto-live systems are fully capturable: their intent is whatever the prompter says it is, and they can be redirected without internal protest. The membrane that defines a cell is, among other things, what a cell uses to **say no**. Proto-live systems lack the equivalent.

Closing both gaps would not produce a slightly more capable AI. It would produce something on the other side of a categorical line.
