# Value Pluralism

## Definition

Value pluralism is Isaiah Berlin's doctrine that ultimate human values are:

1. **Genuinely plural** — there is no single master value (happiness, freedom, reason, God's will) to which all other values are reducible
2. **Sometimes incommensurable** — some values cannot be ranked against each other on a common scale; they are like comparing the color blue to the sound of a violin
3. **Sometimes in irresolvable conflict** — choosing one valuable thing often means sacrificing another valuable thing, and this loss is real and permanent

## Distinction from Relativism

Berlin insists that value pluralism is *not* relativism:
- Relativism says values are merely expressions of preference, culture, or power — they have no objective standing
- Value pluralism says values are *objectively real and binding* — but there are many of them, and they genuinely conflict

The difference has political import: pluralism does not license "anything goes," but it does preclude the authoritarian project of optimizing for a single master value.

## The Machiavelli Reading

In "The Originality of Machiavelli" (reprinted in *Against the Current*), Berlin argues that Machiavelli's real contribution was not amoralism but the discovery that there are *two incommensurable moralities*: Christian morality (humility, mercy, otherworldly salvation) and civic/pagan morality (courage, greatness, political excellence). Neither can be reduced to the other. This — not cynicism — is what makes Machiavelli radical.

## Political Consequences

If values are genuinely plural and incommensurable, then:
- Utopian projects that claim to realize all values simultaneously are necessarily fraudulent or self-deceived
- The most a liberal society can do is protect a minimum core of freedoms that allows people to pursue different conceptions of the good
- Tragic choices — where real goods are sacrificed — are permanent features of political life, not temporary imperfections to be engineered away

## Relation to Negative Liberty

Berlin's value pluralism provides the deeper foundation for his defense of [[Negative Liberty]] (freedom from interference) over [[Positive Liberty]] (freedom to realize one's rational nature). If there is no single rational nature to realize, positive liberty becomes a vehicle for imposing one conception of the good life on everyone.

## Critics

- **Charles Taylor** and others argue that Berlin's pluralism has a conservative tilt: by treating value conflicts as permanent, it forecloses the possibility of social arrangements that actually resolve them
- **Ronald Dworkin** argued that Berlin conflated two separate questions: the pluralism of values and the priority of liberty
- The reading of Machiavelli has been contested — some scholars see it as an imposition of Berlin's own pluralism

## Textual Grounding (from the 2015 PDF)

The *Against the Current* essays ground value pluralism in the counter-Enlightenment tradition: Vico's *verum ipsum factum* establishes that different cultures are genuinely different in kind (not merely degree); Herder's *Schwerpunkt* concept shows each culture embodies its own incommensurable centre of gravity; Machiavelli's two incommensurable moralities (civic/pagan vs. Christian) provide the explicit model for Berlin's own pluralism.
