# Einfühlung

**Translation**: 'Feeling into'; loosely, empathy or imaginative participation
**Coinage**: Associated with [[Johann Gottfried Herder]]; later formalized as a philosophical-aesthetic concept by Robert Vischer and Theodor Lipps in the 19th century

## Definition

*Einfühlung* is Herder's name for the capacity required to understand another culture, historical period, or artistic tradition: the ability to imaginatively enter into the *Schwerpunkt* (centre of gravity) of that culture's experience, rather than applying external criteria of evaluation.

To understand a culture from within, you must ask what framework of human ideas, feelings, and acts *could have generated* the poetry, monuments, mythology, and social forms that were its natural expression. You cannot judge Homer by Aristotelian rules or evaluate the Bible as an 18th-century French critic would — you must become, in imagination, a Greek warrior or a shepherd in the Judean hills.

## Herder's Formulation

"To understand anything was to understand it in its individuality and development, and this required the capacity of *Einfühlung* ('feeling into') the outlook, the individual character of an artistic tradition, a literature, a social organisation, a people, a culture, a period of history."

- To understand a religion, a work of art, or a national character, one must "enter into the unique conditions of its life"
- Those who have been storm-tossed on the North Sea can fully understand the songs of the old Skalds as those who have never seen grim northern sailors never will
- The Bible can be understood only by those who attempt to enter into the experience of primitive shepherds in the Judean hills

## Relation to Vico

[[Giambattista Vico]]'s *fantasia* (imaginative reconstruction) is the parallel concept: the method required to understand Homer is not Cartesian analysis but imaginative projection into the mental world of the Homeric Greeks. Herder's *Einfühlung* and Vico's *fantasia* are twin foundations of the hermeneutic tradition.

Both concepts challenge the Enlightenment's "view from nowhere" — the idea that reason provides a universal standpoint from which to judge all cultures. Herder and Vico insist that understanding is always perspectival and participatory.

## Distinction from Modern "Empathy"

The contemporary psychological concept of empathy (feeling what others feel) is weaker than *Einfühlung* in Herder's sense. *Einfühlung* is not primarily an emotional response to another's emotional state — it is a cognitive-imaginative *method* for reconstructing the internal logic of an alien world. It requires effort, learning, and imaginative stretch; it cannot be achieved by mere sentiment.

## Philosophical Genealogy

- **Vico** → *fantasia* (imaginative participation in historical cultures)
- **Herder** → *Einfühlung* (feeling into cultures, periods, artistic traditions)
- **Dilthey** → *Verstehen* (understanding, as distinct from causal explanation in natural science)
- **Gadamer** → *Horizontverschmelzung* (fusion of horizons)
- **Collingwood** → re-enactment (the historian re-thinks the thoughts of past actors)

## Contemporary Relevance

*Einfühlung* as a critical concept applies to the limits of LLMs as cultural interpreters. A language model trained on texts about a culture approximates understanding from the *outside* — exactly what Herder says is insufficient. The inside view, achieved through *Einfühlung*, requires participatory engagement that statistical pattern-matching structurally cannot provide.
