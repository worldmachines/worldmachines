# Life Era

## Definition

From [[Eric Chaisson]] (2009):

The **Life Era** is the third major cosmological epoch (following the Radiation Era and Matter Era), defined by the emergence of technologically intelligent life as a dominant force capable of manipulating matter and energy on cosmological timescales.

> "Some 14 billion years after the beginning of space and time, the Life Era has now begun, at least locally. Here, the emergence of technologically intelligent life, on Earth and perhaps elsewhere, heralds a whole new era — one where life, in turn, has gradually come to dominate matter."

## The Three Eras

1. **Radiation Era** (~0-500,000 years post-Big Bang): Radiation dominates. Matter exists but cannot form structures — radiation is too energetic, constantly destroying neutral atoms. Uniform, symmetrical, "boring."

2. **Matter Era** (~500,000 years - present): Matter wins. As the universe expanded and cooled, charged particles became neutral hydrogen atoms. Matter became dominant; galaxies, stars, planets, and eventually life emerged.

3. **Life Era** (beginning now): Technology-equipped life begins to overwhelm matter. Just as matter evolved to control radiation, life is now able to manipulate matter at scales that matter alone could not achieve. Books, machines, museums, cities — artifacts no nonsentient nature could have built.

## The Cosmological Claim

This is a strong claim: the emergence of life is not merely biologically significant but *cosmologically* significant. Life is becoming a new category of force in the universe — not just a product of cosmic evolution but an agent of it.

The logic: technology enables life to manipulate matter and energy in ways that transcend biological processes alone. The result will likely be a "symbiotically functioning technoculture" — civilization + machines — representing the highest known concentration of complexity (by [[Energy Rate Density]]) in the universe.

## The Alternative

Chaisson acknowledges the dark alternative: the Life Era "is either... the onset of [the Life Era]... or it will be a passing event in spacetime whereby human life on Earth and its great cultural experiment end."

No guarantee of success. High energy rate density is no protection against self-destruction.

## Relation to New Nature

[[venkatesh-rao]]'s [[New Nature]] is the ecological version of the Life Era thesis: both describe technology becoming as inviolable as physical law. The difference:
- **Chaisson**: The Life Era is (potentially) a positive development — complexity at its highest
- **Rao**: New Nature is an ecology — neither good nor bad, as inhumanly indifferent as the jungle

## Tension with Anti-Anthropocentrism

Chaisson formally disclaims anthropocentrism — humans are not the culmination of the arrow of time. But the Life Era thesis implicitly re-centers intelligence: the transition is cosmologically significant *because* technologically intelligent life is its agent.

This tension is unresolved in Chaisson's account and represents a genuine gap in the framework.
