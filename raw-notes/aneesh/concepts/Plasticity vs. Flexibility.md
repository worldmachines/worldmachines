# Plasticity vs. Flexibility

**Coined by**: [[Catherine Malabou]], *What Should We Do with Our Brain?* (2008)
**Deployed by**: [[Aneesh Sathe]], [[The End of Identity: AI, Plasticity, and the Divergence Machine]] (2026-03-08)

## The Distinction

| | Flexibility | Plasticity |
|--|------------|-----------|
| **Direction** | Receiving form from outside | Giving *and* taking form |
| **Agency** | Passive | Active |
| **Malabou's formulation** | "Plasticity without its genius" | The active form of plasticity |
| **Modernity machine** | Required this | Insufficient for divergence era |
| **Divergence machine** | Insufficient | Required for survival |

**Malabou**: "Flexibility is simply the taking of forms due to external forces; it is plasticity without its genius."

## The Historical Shift

Under the modernity machine, workers were *flexible* — they bent to operate within a set, centralized system. The feedback loop was stable: daily routines gave form (work) and took similar forms (payment, identity, social role) repeatedly.

AI breaks this feedback loop. The comforting repetition of "giving form" (doing one's job) is automated away. What remains is raw plasticity — the capacity to give new forms without a centralized system telling you what those forms mean.

## The Neurological Background

Malabou grounds this in neuroscience: the brain is literally plastic — capable of forming new pathways, not just adapting existing ones. Flexibility describes a brain that responds to stimuli; plasticity describes a brain that *sculpts itself*.

Sathe: we are now "externalizing the brain's pure plasticity" — the modernity machine was keeping it internal and domesticated.

## The Stakes

Under the divergence machine, agents must "diverge not just from each other, but even from [their] immediate past selves, very quickly." This is a different cognitive demand than anything the modernity machine required.

The failure to distinguish flexibility from plasticity leads to a misdiagnosis: thinking the problem is to *adapt better* (flexibility) when the actual demand is to *invent new forms* (plasticity).
