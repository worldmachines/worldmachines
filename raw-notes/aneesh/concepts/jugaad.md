# Jugaad

## Definition and Origin

*Jugaad* (Hindi: जुगाड़) is a term popularized in management literature (notably *Jugaad Innovation* by Radjou, Prabhu, and Ahuja) as "frugal innovation" or "creative improvisation" — the ability to do more with less, find unconventional solutions, make do.

## The Critique

[[Hack Hacky Hacker]] explicitly rejects the jugaad framing:

> "I want to draw a distinction here from the oversubscribed idea of jugaad. Jugaad was once framed as creative improvisation. It is not. I do not care for jugaad. To make something substandard and expect people to accept it is no way to be in the world."

The critique is that jugaad, as actually practiced, rationalizes **substandard output** as acceptable. It conflates route efficiency with destination quality.

## Contrast with [[Hacky Mindset]]

| Dimension | Jugaad | Hacky |
|---|---|---|
| Path | Improvised shortcuts | Improvised shortcuts |
| Destination | Substandard is acceptable | Good output required |
| Attitude to quality | Degraded by design | Preserved despite route |
| Moral framing | "Good enough" | "Build good stuff" |

The distinction is entirely in the attitude toward final quality. The hacky mindset routes imperfectly *toward* a good result; jugaad accepts that the result itself is compromised.

## Why the Distinction Matters

The jugaad critique has teeth in the Indian tech and startup context, where "jugaad culture" has sometimes been used to excuse genuine technical debt, safety shortcuts, or underinvestment in reliability. The essay is arguing against a cultural normalization of low standards dressed up as entrepreneurial agility.
