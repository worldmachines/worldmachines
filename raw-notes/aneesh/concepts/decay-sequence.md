# Decay Sequence

Florian Lohse's model of how canonical infrastructure dies. The claim, from
[[Canonic infrastructure decays in hilarious drainage]]:

> Convergent canonicity does not collapse through revolution or paradigm shift;
> it dissolves through a long draining — a decay sequence (Functional →
> Performative → Explicitly Allegorical → Ornamental → Invisible → Absent) that
> runs identically across domains.

It describes the *mechanism* of Dusk-phase machine conversion — what actually
happens to a world machine's ([[world-machines]]) institutions as it goes from Day
to Dusk, as against the lifecycle's timing and shape. Published as "Shitty
Castles" (Notefields, March 2026).

The load-bearing word is **identically**. A sequence that ran differently per
domain would be a metaphor; one that runs the same in castles, metaphysics and
software is a claim with a shape, and can be checked.

## The moat ladder

The worked case, and the source of the "shitty castle" name — each stage is a
real building:

| Stage | Case |
|---|---|
| Functional | Kenilworth — a moat that stops attackers |
| Performative | Plessis-Bourré — defensible, but built to be seen defending |
| Explicitly Allegorical | Chambord — the fortification as statement |
| Ornamental | Dampierre — water as decor |
| Invisible | the ha-ha — the boundary you cannot see |
| Absent | Tower of London — the moat drained entirely |

Two further domains carry the cross-domain claim:

- **Leibniz's monads** — functional defense → ridiculed by Voltaire → repurposed
  by Deleuze.
- **AI competitive moats** — compute and data barriers → "We Have No Moat" →
  moat-as-lifestyle-brand.

## The afterlife corollary

[[The afterlife of canonical structures is culturally richer than their main act]]
was deliberately split out of the hub, because it is **separately falsifiable**:
the sequence could run exactly as described while cultural generativity stayed
flat or front-loaded. Its claim is that the ridiculed and repurposed stages
out-produce the functional one — Pangloss is more famous than monad theory, the
ha-ha reshaped garden design, Bodiam became "Castle Swamp" in Monty Python, and
the Tower moat went parade ground → allotments → poppy memorial.

The split is the interesting move. It keeps the mechanism and the aesthetic
payoff independently checkable rather than letting the second smuggle in the
first.

## The diagnostic: ornament as evidence of death

[[It takes connoisseurship for ornamental baggage to identify dead frameworks]]
operationalizes the sequence into an instrument:

> You can assess the aliveness or deadness of a framework by examining it for
> functional flaws — the more decorative, non-functional features you find, the
> more likely you face a dead facade than a working fortification.

It is how you apply Rao's four-stage filter in practice: to tell a late-modern
zombie or a postmodern ghost from genuine divergence, **check the moats**. It is
grounded historically in the licence to crenellate — 550 granted in England
between 1194 and 1589, mostly token battlements on manor houses, which Coulson
called "passports to respectability." A fortification permit issued as a status
good is the Performative stage with a paper trail.

The note raises its own objection, which this page does not resolve: decoration
and function are not mutually exclusive in living things. The diagnostic may
over-read ornament as death.

## Visibility

[[World Machines become more visible as they decay]] adds a stage-property the
ladder implies but does not state: you only see the plumbing when it breaks, so
structure can become *more* visible precisely as it loses function — scaffolding
noticed only when the building starts to crumble. Its cases are the "Underground
Empire" (Farrell and Newman), powerful because hidden until exposure and
overreach triggered decline; the Soviet system (Yurchak), simultaneously eternal
and stagnating, its collapse "completely unexpected and completely unsurprising";
and the [[modernity-machine]]'s own relationships, most legible now, in their end
state.

Flagged in the note as the least battle-tested of the six claims, with a real
gap: a Dawn machine under construction is also conspicuous, so the claim needs a
sign condition to separate *visible because decaying* from *visible because being
built*.

## The tension this page is required to hold

[[Frameworks can turn performative as they become more sophisticated]] stands in
declared live tension with the hub, and the tension is aimed at us:

> Sophisticated frameworks can replicate the same passive deferral structure they
> are supposed to help overcome.

Pangloss defers to God's plan; contemporary tech optimism defers to Progress's
trajectory; and potentially the divergence thesis itself defers to historical
machines, with any present dysfunction "explained" by the periodization. The
sting is self-reflexive: a theory that no evidence can falsify may itself be a
performative moat — which would put the World Machines apparatus, and this page,
somewhere on its own ladder.

Florian's instruction is explicit: *hold both as a branch; do not reconcile.*
This page keeps that instruction rather than resolving it. See also
[[world-machines-as-interim-theory]].

## Candidate instances from the corpus

Surfaced by the [[adams-education]] ingest. These are **candidates**, not part of
the original evidence base — Adams never states the sequence, and each is a
reader's reading:

- **The Cross** ([[adams-education-ch-33-a-dynamic-theory-of-history]]) is the
  closest thing to the full ladder in the corpus: literal battlefield force under
  Constantine, contested physical protector at Alaric's sack, defended as purely
  spiritual wealth by Augustine once its mechanical claims fail, and finally an
  object visited by "great masses of idle and ignorant tourists" with "vague
  notions about the force that created them." Functional → allegorical →
  ornamental, narrated by a contemporary who thought he was writing physics.
- **The Virgin** ([[adams-education-ch-25-the-dynamo-and-the-virgin]]) runs the
  sequence on a symbol rather than an institution: a force that built Chartres and
  Amiens, reduced in America to "at most as sentiment." See
  [[the-dynamo-and-the-virgin]].
- **Harvard** ([[adams-education-ch-04-harvard-college]]) is the Functional →
  Performative step alone, caught in one phrase: the college's advantages were
  "vulgarly called social, rather than mental."

The Adams cases are worth something specific to the visibility claim — they are
all *contemporary* readings, made from inside, by a man who consistently
mistook design for what was actually drift ([[witness-bias-toward-design]]).

## What would weaken this

Taken verbatim from the notes' own falsification sections, which are better than
anything this page would invent:

- Modernity institutions disappearing abruptly rather than draining
  re-iteratively; or not decaying at all; or decaying in domain-specific ways
  rather than one universal pattern.
- *(afterlife)* Canonic ruins with no interesting cultural afterlife; or living
  structures with more vivid cultural relevance than ruins.
- *(diagnostic)* Structures that are both genuinely alive and purely decorative.

And the sharpest one, which is the hub's own: "more generative" is asserted, not
defined — generative *of what*, and by what measure?

## Open questions

- More detailed testing against the medieval → modern Dusk transition.
- Anticipation toward the divergence → liveness transition.
- Whether the sequence's stages are discrete at all, or a continuum the six names
  merely sample. The moat ladder reads cleanly because each stage was assigned its
  own building; the monad and AI-moat cases each collapse to three.
