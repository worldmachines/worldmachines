# Prenotions

**Source**: [[aneesh-sathe]], *We Need Homes in the Delta Quadrant* (2025), §I

## The Concept

Sathe introduces *prenotions* to name the earliest conditions of orientation — the circumstances of birth (geography, culture, body) that are not mere facts but active constraints that allow a consciousness to find its footing:

> "The conditions of your birth — your geography, your culture, your body — are not mere facts. They are *prenotions*: early constraints that allow orientation. They interrupt the blur of everything into something — a horizon, a doorway, a room." (§I)

The prenotion is not propositional knowledge. It does not tell you what something is; it gives you a ground from which to ask. It is prior to perception in the same way that grammar is prior to sentences — not a rule you consult but a structure you inhabit.

## Why "Prenotion" Rather Than "Assumption" or "Background"

Standard epistemology speaks of background assumptions, prior beliefs, or tacit knowledge. Sathe's term emphasizes the temporal and constitutional priority: prenotions are not conclusions that have been absorbed into the background. They are *conditions of possibility* for having conclusions at all. A prenotion does not influence how you think about the world; it determines whether you can think about it as *a world* rather than as undifferentiated chaos.

The Baconian register is also present: Francis Bacon's claim that memory without direction is indistinguishable from wandering echoes through prenotions. Structures of orientation are not optional enhancements to cognition — they are constitutive of it.

## Prenotions as Interruption

The key image is interruption: prenotions interrupt "the blur of everything into something." This makes them generative constraints rather than limitations. They are the first act of place-making — the carving of a horizon out of infinity. In this sense, prenotions anticipate Tuan's [[place-as-pause]] (pause as the event that constitutes place from space) but operate at a more fundamental level: they are the conditions that make pause possible in the first place, not the pause itself.

## Relation to Artificial Memory

Kei Kreutler's concept of artificial memory as a "deliberate break in the surface of infinity" extends the prenotion logic. If prenotions are the natural, inherited constraints of birth and body, artificial memory — rituals, technologies, stories — is the deliberate construction of secondary prenotions. We continue the work of orientation-by-interruption throughout life, not only at birth.
