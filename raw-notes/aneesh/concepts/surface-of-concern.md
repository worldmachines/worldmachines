# Surface of Concern

**Source**: [[Aneesh Sathe]], [[Not My Lever — The Last Container]] (Section III), 2026.

## Definition

The **surface of concern** is the set of causal channels through which a single person can act on the world. It is not the set of things a person *cares* about emotionally; it is the set of things a person can plausibly *do something about* — the levers their substrate has actually wired up for them, plus everything that crosses their screen with enough fidelity to register as a claim on their action.

The concept is structural, not motivational. A person's surface of concern is set by their *position in the substrate* — their reach, their tools, their information access — not by their temperament. Two people in similar substrate positions have similar surfaces of concern whether they want to or not.

## The Expansion

The central observation is that the surface has grown by roughly **five orders of magnitude in twenty centuries**, the same expansion documented in [[the-lightening-of-intent]] as the fall in cost-of-intent.

| Era | Surface of concern |
|--|--|
| Medieval peasant | The parish |
| Marcus Aurelius (~170 CE) | The empire |
| Modern essayist (2026) | The planet, the species, the substrate, the next century, the algorithm, the war that is now in the feed because someone with a phone is in it |

Marcus could write to himself in the tent and the writing reached his own mind. A modern essayist publishes the same thought in an afternoon and reaches more readers than Marcus ever had subjects. The substrate that delivers the reach also delivers the obligation — every channel out is a channel in.

## The Psychological Mismatch

The natural psychological response to an expanded surface should be *more* totalizing responsibility, not less. If you can affect the climate by what you eat, the supply chain by what you click, the discourse by what you post, you are a lever for all of it.

> "The medieval peasant could not blame himself for the king's war. You can blame yourself for almost anything that crosses your screen, because there is almost always something you could have done." — Sathe, 2026.

This is the mismatch the essay treats as foundational. There is no biological capacity that can keep pace with a five-orders-of-magnitude expansion of the action surface. The cohort psychology — guilt, responsibility, agency — was tuned to the parish or the empire, not the planet.

## Dispersal as the Default Failure Mode

Without an active intervention, the natural response to the expanded surface is **dispersal**: attention spread thin across every claim, intent fragmented across every channel, action effectively zero on each lever.

> "Dispersal across everything produces effective action on nothing." — Sathe, 2026.

The dispersal is not a moral failing. It is the structural consequence of asking a parish-tuned psychology to operate on a planetary surface. The person who refuses to disperse does so by intervening — by drawing the bounds the receded containers no longer draw for them.

## The Protistant Correction

The intervention is the [[protistant]] move: enclose a bit of the world in a membrane you draw yourself, the way a protist encloses a bit of ocean. The membrane does not control the surface; it controls what crosses into the bounded interior. The interior is a region you can actually act on — a [[The Thicket|thicket]], a project, a craft, a community.

The operational form of this bounding is [[not-my-lever|lever discipline]]: an audit of which causal channels are inside the membrane, with the rest refused via the sentence *"not my lever."*

## Why the Surface Cannot Be Shrunk

A common objection is that the surface could simply be made smaller — log off, narrow your inputs, withdraw. Two reasons this does not work as stated:

1. **The substrate is sticky.** The channels that bring the surface to you are also the channels you use to act. Cutting the inflow cuts the outflow; full withdrawal is the [[capture-resistance|capture-resistance]] failure mode of refusing the substrate entirely, which leaves you with no levers at all.
2. **The dark mirror is available.** The form of refusal that simply *vacates* the surface — the citizen who calls opt-out freedom, the influencer who launders extraction as personal boundary — is identical from outside to the generative bounding. The [[dark-forestry|dark-forestry]] diagnostic ("who pays the energy cost, and who captures the value?") is the only reliable distinction. See [[not-my-lever]] §"The Dark Mirror".

The protistant move is therefore *not* "make the surface small" — it is "draw a real interior inside an unchanged surface, and act on the interior."

## Historical Asymmetry: Containers Receded While the Surface Expanded

The surface-of-concern thesis is the complement to the [[receding-containers|receding-containers]] thesis. The two have moved in opposite directions for two millennia:

- **Containers**: thinned monotonically. Cosmic logos → personal God → Romantic sublime → modern ideology → postmodern pluralism → non-mandatory.
- **Surface**: expanded monotonically. Parish → empire → nation → planet → planet-plus-substrate-plus-future.

The conjunction is what produces the modern psychological condition. A person with a Marcus-sized container and a parish-sized surface is in equilibrium. A person with a non-mandatory container and a planet-sized surface is in structural strain. The strain is not pathology — it is the load profile of the era.
