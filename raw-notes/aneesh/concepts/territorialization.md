# Territorialization

## Core Definition (Deleuze and Guattari)

Territorialization, deterritorialization, and reterritorialization (the T/D/R triad) describe how assemblages consolidate, loosen, and reform their internal organization and their relations to their environment. The vocabulary comes primarily from Deleuze and Guattari's *A Thousand Plateaus* (1987).

- **Territorialization**: the process by which an assemblage stabilizes its elements, boundaries, and functions — fixing relations between components, establishing regularities, consolidating identity. To territorialize is to produce a relatively stable, organized, bounded machine.
- **Deterritorialization**: the process by which an assemblage's organization is disrupted — components are freed from fixed relations, lines of flight open, the assemblage's boundaries become porous. Can be forced from outside (environmental disruption) or endogenous (internal tensions reaching a threshold).
- **Reterritorialization**: the re-stabilization of an assemblage after deterritorialization — but typically into a new configuration, not a return to the prior state. The assemblage may reterritorialize around different organizing principles.

These are not stages but simultaneous tendencies: every assemblage is always simultaneously undergoing territorialization (maintaining its organization) and deterritorialization (its organization is always under pressure, its components always pursuing their own trajectories).

## Bryant's Usage

Bryant does not use the T/D/R vocabulary as extensively as Deleuze and Guattari, but it underlies his account of assemblage dynamics. Bryant's [[entropy-and-assemblage-maintenance]] analysis is a restatement of the territorialization problem: maintaining an assemblage (keeping it territorialized) requires ongoing negentropic work. Entropy = deterritorializing tendency; negentropic operations = territorializing processes.

The machinic problems that afflict all assemblages (the semi-autonomous trajectories of component machines) are deterritorializing forces internal to the machine. Revolutionary politics — Bryant's extended application — involves deliberately introducing deterritorialization into overly rigid (over-territorialized) social machines.

## Stratification and Double Articulation

In *A Thousand Plateaus*, Deleuze and Guattari describe territorialization through the concept of *stratification* and *double articulation*:
1. **First articulation**: selects and stabilizes materials, forms populations
2. **Second articulation**: organizes the populations into functional structures

The geological metaphor: the first articulation deposits sediment; the second articulation hardens it into rock. Every stratum (every stable assemblage) involves both selections of content and organizations of expression.

## Political Implications

The T/D/R triad is explicitly political in Deleuze and Guattari. The *State apparatus* is a territorializing machine par excellence: it strives to capture flows, fix populations, regularize land use, and stabilize social functions. *War machines* (in their technical D&G sense) are deterritorializing — they operate on lines of flight, escape capture, produce novelty.

Bryant follows this in arguing that emancipatory politics often requires deterritorialization of oppressive social machines. But he adds a corrective: deterritorialization without reterritorialization is dissolution, not liberation. The entropy of New Orleans after Katrina was not emancipatory; it was catastrophic. Productive political deterritorialization must enable reterritorialization into better-functioning, less oppressive assemblages.

## Relation to Entropy/Negentropy

The T/D/R triad maps onto Bryant's entropy framework:
- **Territorialization** = negentropic operation; maintaining low entropy
- **Deterritorialization** = entropic drift; increase in disorder/probability
- **Reterritorialization** = reconstitution of a new low-entropy state; new negentropic organization

Plasticity (Bryant's term for adaptive capacity) requires some deterritorialization — some entropy reserve — to enable reterritorialization into forms adequate to novel environments.
