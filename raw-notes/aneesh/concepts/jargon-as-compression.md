# Jargon as Compression

## Core Idea

Jargon is not obfuscation — it is *precision*. Domain vocabulary compresses complex, multi-conditional statements into single terms. "EBITDA" is not gatekeeping; it is a pointer to a specific calculation with specific implications that would require a paragraph to unpack in plain language.

The concept appears in [[Jan. 3, 2025]] as the epistemic mechanism behind [[AI Democratization Gap]].

## How It Applies to AI

When a user queries an AI system:
- An expert can compress their question into precise terms, extracting a correspondingly precise answer
- A novice queries in plain language, getting an answer calibrated to that lower resolution
- The AI system cannot reconstruct what the user didn't know to ask

The practical outcome: jargon fluency is a *prerequisite* for full composability (see [[Composable Knowledge]]). Access to a tool doesn't grant access to its full capability.

## Implications

### For Democratization Narratives

The common claim that AI democratizes expertise assumes the bottleneck is *access*. Jargon as compression suggests the real bottleneck is often *query quality* — which correlates with domain expertise, not access.

### For Product and Interface Design

See [[Sparing and Freeing]] and [[Building Thinking Dwelling as Product Design]]: good interfaces reduce the burden of query precision, helping users ask better questions without requiring full domain fluency. This is one way to genuinely close (rather than widen) the [[AI Democratization Gap]].

### For Knowledge Transmission

In [[Knowledge Transmission]], jargon plays a dual role:
- Within communities: enables high-bandwidth exchange
- Between communities or expertise levels: creates friction and stratification

## Tension

There is a productive contradiction between:
1. Jargon enables precision → experts compose better
2. Jargon excludes → novices are systematically disadvantaged

This is not resolvable by simply "explaining jargon." Once explained, the novice still lacks the practiced intuition for *when* and *how precisely* to apply the term.
