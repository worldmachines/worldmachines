# LLMs as Epistemic Tools

## The Standard View (and its Limits)

The dominant use of LLMs is problem-solving: write code, get recipes, summarize documents. This is computing in its traditional mode — the user commands, the machine executes, the problem dissolves.

See: [[Problems vs. Questions]]

## The Upgraded Claim

LLMs introduce a new affordance: **conversational, back-and-forth dialogue in natural language**. This creates the possibility of pursuing *questions*, not just problems. The computer becomes a surface for exploration rather than a solution dispenser.

> "AI lets us explore questions *with*, rather than merely *through*, computers."

## What Makes LLMs Viable as Socratic Partners

1. **Trained on human-generated knowledge** — sufficient shared worldview to provide a normative mirror. (Unlike a bat, which cannot serve as Socratic interlocutor to a human — no shared world.)
2. **Infinite patience** — no session fatigue, no ego, no internal urge to be right.
3. **No need to win** — optimal Socratic conditions.
4. **Every level of understanding** — can meet the questioner where they are.

## The Hallucination Reframe

Conventional view: hallucinations are bugs.
Epistemic view: the *ability* to hallucinate is a feature. It:
- Creates exploratory surface area (errors become points of productive friction).
- Forces the user into verification mode — active, not passive.
- Models the Socratic interlocutor who holds positions for examination, not assertion.

The shift is from "the computer is always right" (old model) to "the computer may be wrong and that's an invitation to think."

## The Space Metaphor

> "Each word we put in ties down the AI to specific vantage points to help us explore."

A chat session begins as "pure space." Words act as place-making moves — each one constrains and orients the exploration. This is [[Socratic Thinking]] operationalized through language.

See: [[Space and Place in Cognition]]

## Future Development

As LLMs gain access to structured knowledge databases (established knowledge), they will be able to:
- Flag user beliefs that contradict accepted knowledge.
- Play a more rigorous Socratic role.
- Reduce reliance on hallucination for exploratory purposes.

This will require social negotiation about what constitutes "established knowledge" — itself a deeply Socratic process.

## Tension / Critique

- Does Socratic dialogue require genuine disagreement (another agent with its own views) or merely the appearance of it? LLMs may simulate the form without the substance.
- [[Thomas Nagel]]'s bat analogy underscores that shared language is necessary but not sufficient — genuine Socratic exchange may require shared *stakes*.
- Who curates "established knowledge"? Power over the reference database is power over the Socratic encounter.
