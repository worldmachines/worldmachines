# Bureaucratic Diffusion of Responsibility

**Bureaucratic diffusion of responsibility** is the structural tendency of large hierarchical organizations to distribute decision-making authority across so many roles that no single actor can be clearly identified as responsible for any given outcome.

## The Mechanism

In complex organizations:
1. Decisions require sign-off from multiple layers
2. Formal titles describe roles, not actual power (the "grand secretary who merely records")
3. Interpretive authority (applying rules) is separated from legislative authority (making rules)
4. Reporting structures create matrix accountability that makes single attribution impossible

## Historical Cases

### Mughal Matrix Structure
Provincial officials reported to central government independently of the provincial governor (subahdar). This made the locus of any specific decision untraceable to external observers and even to other officials. See [[Mughal Administration]].

### Ming Grand Secretaries
After the Hongwu Emperor abolished the Central Secretariat, workload forced him to appoint secretaries who "merely recorded imperial decisions." The word "merely" did enormous work: they shaped what decisions were made while formally owning none of them. See [[Ming Dynasty Grand Secretaries]].

### Yes, Minister Formulation
> "In industry if you screw things up, you get the boot. In the civil service, if you screw things up, I get the boot."

Sir Humphrey's exchange with Hacker captures the fully modern version: the minister (memetically visible) absorbs accountability; the civil servant (antimemetically invisible) retains power. See [[Yes, Minister]].

## Functional vs Dysfunctional Forms

| | Functional | Dysfunctional |
|---|---|---|
| **Intent** | Efficiency, specialization | Evasion of accountability |
| **Result** | Better decisions through distributed expertise | No one owns outcomes |
| **Democratic compatibility** | Compatible with oversight | Resistant to oversight |

## Relationship to Antimemetics

Diffusion of responsibility is the mechanism by which administrative [[Antimeme|antimemetics]] is achieved. The more diffuse the accountability, the harder the structure is to see, attribute, or reform.
