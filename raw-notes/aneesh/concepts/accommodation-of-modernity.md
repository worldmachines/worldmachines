# Accommodation of Modernity

In [[bayo-akomolafe-the-untimely]], an **accommodation** is a house or field where bodies gain legibility. The accommodation of modernity is a machinic operation that hates cracks because cracks frustrate categorization. Modern power wants to finalize what a body is: once legible, the body becomes archivable, surveillable, incarceratable.

## Features

- **Categoricity** — modernity wants things nailed down, named, completed, and known.
- **Pathologization of monsters** — what cannot be categorized is treated as defective or dangerous, unless it can be given a seat at the table and rendered legible.
- **Recognition as capture** — being seen by the emperor is a curse; visibility can renew the accommodation rather than subvert it.
- **Misnaming failure** — lateness, African time, disability, grief, and awkwardness are treated as deficiencies rather than possible sites of [[untimely]] perception.

## Political Implication

If modern power works through legibility, then liberation cannot be equated with recognition. This is the bridge to [[parapolitics-of-the-untimely]]: the task is not simply to be seen by power, but to inhabit cracks where different forms of relation become possible.
