# Socratic Thinking

## Definition

> "Thinking is a social quest for better answers to the sorts of questions that show up for us already answered." — [[Agnes Callard]], paraphrased

Broken down:
- **Social**: Done with a partner who disagrees because they hold their own views.
- **Quest**: Both parties are genuinely seeking knowledge, not winning.
- **Pre-answered questions**: The target is not ignorance but settled conviction — reopening what we think we know.

## Core Structure

Socratic thinking proceeds by:
1. Identifying a question that appears already answered.
2. Applying pressure to the apparent answer via counterexample, analogy, or alternative framework.
3. Generating subsidiary problems that can be agreed upon (knowledge-places).
4. Iterating until the parties reach a satisfactory stabilization of the question-space.

The quest stops when parties are satisfied — not when Truth is found. Knowledge is *place-stabilization*, not absolute arrival.

## Cartographic Metaphor

Socratic dialogue is **interrogation as cartography**. Each exchange plants a map point:
- "Which show?" (when someone claims to love TV) forces specification — a place is created.
- Disagreement over a list opens the meta-question: "What is it to be entertained?"

See: [[Space and Place in Cognition]], [[Thinking with Places]]

## Why Pre-Answered Questions Are the Target

Pre-answered questions are the hardest to examine because our entire operating stance depends on them. Gravity is obvious — until someone asks why. The first step is admitting that everyone's guiding landmarks may be [[Normative Self-Blindness|semi-solid smoke]].

## Tools as Socratic Constraint

Even solo thinking with a tool is quasi-Socratic: the tool interrupts pure space and forces specification. Netflix's algorithm, a hammer, a spreadsheet — each enforces a kind of interrogation on possibility-space.

See: [[Tools and Worldview Commitment]]

## AI as Socratic Partner

Conditions under which LLMs can serve as Socratic co-pilots:
1. Sufficient shared worldview (trained on human knowledge)
2. Infinite patience; no urge to win
3. Ability to hold question-space open without dissolving it into a problem

See: [[LLMs as Epistemic Tools]], [[Chatbots, Bats & Broken Oracles]]

## Social Internet Application

Social internet currently privileges winning. Socratic inquiry as corrective posture: collaborative, willing to challenge and accept. "The dialogue is the real post."
