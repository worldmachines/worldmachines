# Personal Media vs Platform Media

## The Distinction

**Personal media**: high-ownership, self-hosted or self-managed publishing (blogs, personal sites). The author controls the structure, style, and permanence.

**Platform media**: content published within a platform's structure (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Medium). The platform controls distribution, interface, and ultimately the archive.

Source: [[Jan. 3, 2025]]

## The Degradation Argument

As AI-generated content floods the open web, it homogenizes and degrades the signal-to-noise ratio of platform-mediated content first (because platforms optimize for engagement, not quality). Personal blogs, controlled by individuals who stake their identity on the writing, are more resistant to this degradation.

The author's claim: blogs will become the **refuge** for people who still want to find and share good things.

## The Maximalist Blog as Aesthetic Object

The best blogs create a sensation of totality — "I don't even know where to look, or where to begin." The cited example is Ribbonfarm (Venkatesh Rao), whose accumulated volume makes it feel like gaining access to a person's full intellectual life. This is a qualitative experience unavailable on platforms, which truncate and flatten.

## Connection to Dwelling

Blogs, on this account, are places where both author and reader can *dwell*. The [[Dwelling as Philosophical Concept]] framework applies: a blog is a place with order and purpose defined by the owner, opening onto spaces the reader can explore. Platforms are the opposite: spaces defined by the platform's logic, into which users are fitted.

## Link Blogging as Practice

[[Simon Willison]]'s link blogging model operationalizes personal media: each post is a genuine act of curation and commentary, not re-posting for engagement. The standard — *reading my post and the source together should be better than either alone* — is an explicitly quality-first metric incompatible with platform incentives.

## Connection to Studiolo

The personal blog as a *public studiolo*: a space for self-cultivation that also opens outward. Compare [[Studiolo and Self-Cultivation]] where the studiolo is private; the blog is the outward-facing version of the same intellectual stance.
