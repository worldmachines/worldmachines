# Insta-Places

## Definition

Insta-places are the mind's internal analogs to geographical landmarks: cognitive reference points conjured from experience that we treat as solid and reliable, regardless of how well they are actually grounded in knowledge.

They are "insta-" because they are conjured on demand — not laboriously constructed but rapidly assembled from available experience. They are "places" because they function as navigational anchors in the [[Space and Place in Cognition|cognitive space-and-place]] framework.

## How They Work

1. We encounter novel territory (a question, a situation, a decision).
2. The mind rapidly assembles a reference point from relevant experience and prior belief.
3. This reference point is *treated as solid* for the purpose of current navigation.
4. [[Normative Self-Blindness]] protects the insta-place from in-the-moment revision.

## Epistemic Status

- **Past errors**: We can accept that an old insta-place was poorly formed — that was then.
- **Future unknowns**: We can accept that we don't yet have a place for some future territory.
- **Present operation**: We cannot accept that a currently active insta-place is false — that would dissolve the stance from which we operate.

## Relation to Identity

From the outside, the entire mind appears as a single place (our identity). Identity is itself the outermost, most load-bearing insta-place. Probe it and it fractures into smaller insta-places — a fractal structure of semi-solid smoke.

See: [[Identity as Place]], [[The Mind as Semi-Solid Smoke]]

## Examples

- The scientist who knows "what makes a good scientist" without having examined the belief.
- The viewer who knows "what counts as good TV" before anyone asks.
- The expert whose field-level assumptions are never interrogated.
