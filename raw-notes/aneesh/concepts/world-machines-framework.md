# World Machines Framework

> **Canonical public reference**: [[worldmachines-org-theory]] (worldmachines.org/theory). Defer there for the project's canonical phrasing of the definition, lifecycle, and the five foundational open questions.
>
> **Distinct from [[world-machines]]**, which covers the concept-object level: what world machines *are*, how they behave, the three-machine simultaneity problem, and the current machine landscape. This page covers the meta-level apparatus — how the framework works as an intellectual tool, its epistemological stance, and what it still needs to answer.

## Definition

A **World Machine** (WM) is a long-lived civilizational configuration characterized by:
- ~400 years of construction
- ~400 years of stable operation
- Relatively rapid decline and collapse

Three WMs operate in parallel at any given time: a **Dawn Machine** (growing), **Day Machine** (mature), and **Dusk Machine** (dying/dead). The naming follows the Cleons genetic dynasty from Asimov's *Foundation* TV adaptation.

More precise term: **configurancy** — but "machine" is preferred for evocativeness and its engineering connotations (development, installation, configuration, upgrade, release, phase-out).

## Epistemological Status

Explicitly **not** a Grand Unified Theory of history. Not a cyclic history model in the tradition of Kondratriev, Perez, Turchin, Sorokin, Toynbee, Gibbon, or Ibn Khaldun. Rather: "sensemaking triage and a shared conceptual language" — an "opinionated cognitive coworking space."

Rao's protocol: raw historical phenomenology first; don't force-fit to the framework. Loose ends are fine.

Connection to [[Weick — Theorizing as Process]]: WM is an explicit "interim struggle" toward theory, not a finished theory. This is a feature, not a bug.

## Key Properties

- **Overlap**: At any time, three machines co-exist and interact. Overlap periods show the most interesting dynamics — when the Dawn Machine is strong enough to produce heretics within the Day Machine (e.g., Hume and Smith as DM heretics in Modernity, DM founders in Divergence).
- **Pull vs. Push**: The [[Modernity Machine]] was a *pull* machine (telos of Progress, convergent). The [[divergence-machine]] is a *push* machine (individual/small-scale forces, divergent).
- **Fractal structure**: Open question — is there a micro-to-macro fractal structure? Can there be "thin" WMs at only one scale?
- **Planetary connectivity condition**: May be a necessary/sufficient condition for a WM to be meaningful. Bronze Age tin trade as test case.

## Genealogy

Rao positions WM as "Strauss-Howe for civilizations" at 1000-year cycle time, but explicitly resists the determinism and cyclism of that comparison.

## What's Missing

- No formal inventory of all WMs since the Neolithic Revolution
- No specification of necessary/sufficient conditions
- The Axial Age (800 BCE–800 CE, ~1600 years) suggests the time constant was slower before modernity
- No theory of *why* the cycle time is ~400/400 years for contemporary machines
