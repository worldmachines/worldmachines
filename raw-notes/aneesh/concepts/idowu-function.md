# Idowu Function

The **Idowu function** names [[bayo-akomolafe]]'s conceptual use of Yoruba twin cosmology in [[bayo-akomolafe-the-untimely]]. In the story, Taiwo comes out first and Kehinde second, but within the womb Kehinde is older and sends Taiwo to inspect the world. The child who comes after twins is Idowu. Akomolafe says Idowu is, in a deeper sense, older than the twins because Idowu holds the twins' excessive charge.

The philosophical move: Idowu is not a synthesis of a binary. Idowu is the third that comes after the binary but is older than it; the host of the binary's unresolved energy.

## Conceptual Work

- **Excess, not resolution** — Idowu does not solve Taiwo/Kehinde; Idowu holds, hosts, and composts them.
- **Afterwardness as priority** — the one who comes after may be older than what came before, scrambling linear sequence.
- **Untimeliness** — Idowu becomes Akomolafe's image for [[untimely]]: surplus temporality, residue, what cannot be counted.
- **Anti-alternative logic** — instead of creating another twin to oppose the first term, Idowu breaks the count itself.

## Essay Use

This is a strong figure for thinking beyond binary oppositions without prematurely reconciling them. It may be useful for essays on:

- modernity / anti-modernity as twins requiring an Idowu rather than a counter-modern program;
- human / AI, work / play, agency / structure as binaries that need holding rather than synthesis;
- [[hack-hacky-hacker]]'s middle position, though Idowu is stranger than compromise or lateral play.
