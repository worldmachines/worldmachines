# The Gloomwheel

## Definition

Coined in [[The Small God of the Internet]]. The Gloomwheel is the **infinite scroll** as an extractive mechanism: an "endless thumb-crank that insists there is more, and worse, if you'll just keep scrolling." Sathe explicitly contrasts it with a millwheel: the Gloomwheel "turns and turns without making flour."

The metaphor frames platform feeds not merely as distracting but as *architecturally wasteful* — they consume kinetic attention without transforming it into anything the user values.

## Contrast with RSS

The Gloomwheel is the antithesis of the [[RSS]] model:

| RSS / River of Posts | Gloomwheel |
|---|---|
| Pull-based (you fetch) | Push-based (it sends) |
| Chronological order | Algorithmic ranking |
| Finite daily bundle | Infinite, no stopping point |
| Silence is possible | Silence is penalized |
| You own the subscription | Platform owns the graph |
| No "You Might Also Like" | Constant injection of adjacent content |

## Mechanism

Platforms "threw dams across [RSS's] tributaries and called them platforms." Good water (open feeds) disappeared behind walls; the rest was routed into "ornamental channels that loop the palace and reflect only the palace." The Gloomwheel is what happens when engagement metrics replace editorial judgment.
