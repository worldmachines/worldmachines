# Philosophy as Maintenance

## Core Idea

Philosophy is not a destination or a civilizational high score — it is **periodic maintenance on the place called identity**. We reach for philosophical frameworks when the [[Identity Crash]] disrupts normal functioning, and the framework we choose is determined by the *degree* of identity disruption, not by our intellectual sophistication.

> "Crashing and philosophy sampling are maintenance actions on the place called identity." — [[The Plato Plateau]]

## Implications

1. **No hierarchy of philosophies**: The complexity of one's chosen philosophy does not mark superiority. A rung's usefulness matches your identity state and environment.
2. **Instrumental use of philosophy**: We pick the philosophy that provides just enough of a toehold in the abstract to spur action in the actual.
3. **Temporary residence**: Philosophical engagement is meant to restart the [[Anxiety as Generative Force]] motor, not replace it.

## The Taxonomy as Maintenance Schedule

The [[Levels of Philosophical Engagement]] map directly to maintenance needs:
- Survival-level disruption → Survival philosophies (laws, tactics)
- Internal crisis → Self-knowledge, meaning, meta-systems

## Tension with Traditional Philosophy

Traditional philosophy presents itself as the pursuit of permanent truths. The maintenance view is aggressively instrumental — philosophy as wrench, not cathedral. This tension is worth exploring: does the instrumental view devalue philosophy, or does it finally give it a defensible purpose?
