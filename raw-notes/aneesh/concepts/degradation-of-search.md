# Degradation of Search

## The Phenomenon

Research has confirmed what many users had experienced anecdotally: Google search results have measurably worsened over time. The degradation is structural, not accidental:
- **Advertising incentives**: high-quality organic results compete with paid placements; the incentive is to show ads, not to surface the best answer
- **SEO gaming**: content farms optimize for ranking signals rather than user value, flooding results with low-quality pages that technically satisfy algorithmic criteria
- **Personalization and filter bubbles**: results are tuned to engagement rather than accuracy

## Significance for AI Epistemics

Sathe (see [[My Grandfather Had Lore]]) treats search degradation as the necessary backdrop to the LLM adoption story. The sequence matters:
1. Google quality declines → users lose confidence in search
2. ChatGPT and similar tools emerge as alternatives
3. LLMs are more fluent and accessible than degraded search
4. But LLMs [[Hallucination|hallucinate]] without any citation trail
5. Result: users adopted a less reliable tool because the reliable tool had already failed them

This is not a technology failure story — it's an incentives story. The degradation of search was commercially rational for Google (advertising revenue) even as it was harmful to users. LLMs arrived not as an improvement on good search but as a successor to already-bad search.

## The Opportunity Cost

Had search quality remained high, the pressure to adopt generative AI as a primary information source would have been much lower. The [[Lore vs. Search vs. LLM]] typology would show search holding its position. The civilizational regression risk (back to lore) is conditional on search having failed first.
