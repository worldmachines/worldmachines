# Herder's Schwerpunkt

**German**: *Schwerpunkt* = centre of gravity, focal point
**Source**: [[Johann Gottfried Herder]], used throughout his writings, including *Ideas on the Philosophy of the History of Humanity* (1784–91)

## The Concept

Every culture has its own *Schwerpunkt* — its own unique centre of gravity, its own constellation of values, sensibilities, practices, and forms of human excellence. This centre is not a formula or a doctrine; it is the living spirit that pervades the culture's language, art, religion, law, and everyday customs.

To understand a culture, you must grasp its *Schwerpunkt*. This means you cannot evaluate it by the standards of your own culture's centre of gravity — doing so is "vanity and blindness." The Parisian *arbiters de goût* who applied classicist rules to all literature were making precisely this error: imposing their own culture's centre of gravity as if it were a universal standard.

## The Political Implication

"To grade the merits of cultural wholes, of the legacy of entire traditions, by applying a collection of dogmatic rules claiming universal validity, enunciated by the Parisian arbiters of taste, is vanity and blindness. Every culture has its own unique *Schwerpunkt*, and unless we grasp it we cannot understand its character or value."

This entails:
- No culture is a mere means towards another; every human society is to be judged by its own internal standards
- The Roman conquest of native civilizations was a crime against the *Schwerpunkt* of each destroyed culture
- The Church's forcible baptism of the Balts was similarly criminal — imposing an alien *Schwerpunkt* on peoples with their own
- British missionaries destroying Asian cultures: the same sin at industrial scale

## The Tension with Nationalism

Herder's *Schwerpunkt* is explicitly anti-nationalist: he supposed "that different cultures could and should flourish fruitfully side by side like so many peaceful flowers in the great human garden." But the seeds of nationalism are present. Once you accept that a culture's *Schwerpunkt* is its most precious possession, it is a short step to arguing that any cultural contamination is a threat, that cultural purity must be defended politically, and that each *Volk* deserves its own state.

Herder himself rejected coercive nationalism; his 19th-century disciples did not.

## Relation to *Einfühlung*

*Schwerpunkt* and [[einfuhlung|Einfühlung]] are complementary: *Einfühlung* is the method, *Schwerpunkt* is the object. You achieve *Einfühlung* in order to grasp the *Schwerpunkt*. Together they constitute Herder's hermeneutics.

## Contrast with the Enlightenment Position

For Voltaire, d'Alembert, and Condorcet, "there is only universal civilisation, of which now one nation, now another, represents the richest flowering." The French are currently at the apex, and other nations progress insofar as they approach the French standard.

For Herder, this is "a plurality of incommensurable cultures" — each with its own *Schwerpunkt*, none measurable by the others. The Enlightenment position is not just factually wrong; it is a form of cultural imperialism.

## The Cosmopolitanism Critique

Herder's most famous articulation: "To belong to a given community, to be connected with its members by indissoluble and impalpable ties of common language, historical memory, habit, tradition and feeling, is a basic human need no less natural than that for food or drink or security or procreation." Cosmopolitanism — the deliberate shedding of particular cultural identity — is "the shedding of all that makes one most human, most oneself."
