# Rigid and Plastic Machines

[[levi-bryant]] positions all machines on a spectrum of *plasticity* — the degree to which a machine is modified by the inputs that flow through it and the couplings it enters into. This is one of the key typological distinctions in [[onto-cartography]] Ch 2 (pp. 50–53).

## The Spectrum

### Absolutely Rigid Machines (Ideal Pole)

An absolutely rigid machine would be so impermeable to the operations of other machines that it performs the same transformations on any input without being modified by them. The limiting case is the **Pythagorean theorem**: it operates identically on right triangles of any material, color, shape, or size. It is impermeable to the powers of wood, steel, string, or pencil (p. 51). Mathematical equations and laws of logic are the closest empirical instances.

Other examples approaching rigidity: industrial factories, bureaucracies, tin cookie cutters, dogmatic theologies, compulsive obsessive neuroses, certain fetishes, stove tops (p. 51). These machines are "only marginally transformed by the inputs that flow through them or upon which they exercise their operations, while nonetheless ineluctably exercising their operations on these flows" (p. 51).

### Absolutely Plastic Machines (Ideal Pole)

An absolutely plastic machine would be completely transformed — become something entirely different — in every encounter. Bryant suggests there are probably no absolutely plastic machines because there is no structureless matter (p. 51). However, certain subatomic particles approach this pole insofar as they display "a tendency to become something else entirely when encountering other particles or to be destroyed altogether" (p. 51).

Machines approaching plasticity: organic bodies, great works of art, anarchic political movements, brains, subatomic particles (p. 51). These are "strongly modified in terms of their local manifestations and becomings as a result of the flows that pass through them" (p. 51).

## What Plasticity Means for Identity

"All machines are more or less characterized by *plasticity*. There is no machine so rigid that it is not haunted by a plurality of virtual manifestations and becomings that may or may not become actualized." (p. 46)

This means machine identity is not fixed but processual. Powers wax and wane. The mind of a blind person with a seeing-eye dog is literally different from that of the same person in isolation (p. 46). A caterpillar's metamorphosis into a butterfly involves both the acquisition of new powers and the loss of others. Cold steel loses its malleability; the powers of thinking and experiencing are diminished by sleep deprivation.

Each machine "has a plasticity proper to the sort of machine that it is" (p. 46) — a rock cannot become a butterfly, but it has a range of possible becomings appropriate to rock-being.

## Resonance and Pluripotency

A special form of high plasticity is **resonance**: the capacity of a machine to produce *novel* local manifestations as a result of the entities it encounters, rather than simply repeating a fixed set of operations. A great work of art resonates — Kafka's *The Trial* produces different operations in psychoanalytic, political, theological, and bureaucratic-critical contexts (p. 52). See [[pluripotency-and-exaptation]].

The insight about great art: it is plastic not in the sense that it becomes something different with every reading (it remains *The Trial*), but in the sense that it is *pluripotent* — capable of producing genuinely different operations across historical and cultural milieus. "A great work of art resonates. If we take the concept of resonance seriously, then we understand it as a power capable of producing *novel* local manifestations as a result of the other entities that it encounters." (p. 52)

## Political and Ethical Stakes

The rigid/plastic distinction has direct political implications:
- **Rigid machines** (bureaucracies, corporations, dogmatic movements) resist change through couplings with individual humans. To change them requires targeting the flows they are actually open to — their operational language — rather than appealing to ethical persuasion (see [[thermodynamic-politics]]).
- **Plastic machines** (organic bodies, brains, anarchic movements) are more susceptible to transformation through new couplings, new flows, new environments.
