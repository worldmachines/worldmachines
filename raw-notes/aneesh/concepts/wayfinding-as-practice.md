# Wayfinding as Practice

## The Paradigm

Polynesian wayfinding is the practice of navigating long ocean passages without instruments, using the rising and setting positions of stars, the shape of swells refracted off distant islands, the colour of the water, and the behaviour of shore-nesting birds. The paradigmatic modern case is the 1976 Hōkūleʻa voyage from Maui to Tahiti, navigated by [[mau-piailug]] after a gap of about six centuries in native Hawaiian practice.

The technique has two load-bearing features. First, continuous attention: the wayfinder does not sleep in the full sense, because a night of drift produces integrated error that must be actively corrected. Second, directional bias: the wayfinder ignores most signals in favour of a few committed ones (keep the ENE swell on the port quarter, aim ten degrees west of the rising star), because symmetric attention to every signal averages to drift. The [[scallop-theorem]] is the physical reason.

## Generalisation

The practice generalises beyond oceans. In any environment that does not carry the practitioner forward — which is to say, any [[low-reynolds-culture]] — the same two features apply:

- **Continuous attention** to the work, with short rests rather than long absences. The work cannot be paused without integrated loss.
- **Asymmetric commitment** to a few directions over many possible ones. Committing is metabolically expensive; uncommitting is zero; the asymmetry is what produces displacement.

## Macro-Wayfinding

At the timescale of a career, wayfinding looks like holding a direction the surrounding field does not value and compensating for every pull away from it by continuous corrective work. [[anne-carpenter]]'s 20-year arc on image-based profiling is the paradigmatic recent biotech case: CellProfiler (2006), Cell Painting (2016), JUMP-CP (2023). The field caught up only after the accumulated artifact reached the point where the competition had no alternative training corpus.

## Micro-Wayfinding

At the timescale of an afternoon, wayfinding looks like the bench scientist working against generative-model output. The model returns a thousand candidates; most are plausible; most are not useful. Ranking by score and taking the top five is a reciprocal move — nothing is deposited that a future self can use. Refusing the model's top pick when it looks wrong, and pulling a fifth-ranked candidate forward because something in its structure reads as the better move, is the non-reciprocal stroke. Taste is the flagellum.

## The Deposit

Wayfinding in the sense used here is always artifact-producing. A voyage completed is a durable fact. A compound synthesised is a durable artifact. A proof that compiles is a durable entry in the archive. The practice and the [[artifact-requirement]] are linked: wayfinding is the technique, the artifact is the trace, the trace is what makes the next voyage possible.
