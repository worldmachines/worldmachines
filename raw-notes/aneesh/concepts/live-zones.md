# Live Zones

## Definition

A *live zone* is a structure in which energy invested by participants accumulates as durable state, rather than dissipating as heat. The test is the artifact: after a participant leaves, does something remain that the next participant can build on? If yes, the zone accumulates; if no, it is a dissipative structure masquerading as a live one.

The term is contrasted with *dead zones* or *dissipative structures*: organisations and platforms that absorb cognitive labour and return nothing durable to the labourer (the attention economy is the paradigm case). In the language of the [[low-reynolds-culture]], dead zones are scallops — energetic but reciprocal, producing zero net displacement — and live zones are flagella — asymmetric, committed, and moving the swimmer forward.

## The Accumulation Test

The operational test for live-zone status is the [[artifact-requirement]]: does the structure's architecture make motion-without-deposit impossible? CTAN requires documentation at upload. Tiny Tapeout requires a working RTL design before fabrication. Mathlib requires proofs that compile. These are not cultural norms but architectural constraints.

## Three Structural Types

Research across ~23 candidate projects produced three recurring shapes:

1. **Individual obsession.** One person, one room, decades. Piotr Wozniak's 40 years of self-logged sleep and memory experiments. Fabien Sanglard's reverse-engineered game engines. Habring²'s 20-year iteration of the A11 watch movement. Accumulation substrate: a personal archive dense enough that others cite it as infrastructure.
2. **Physical infrastructure.** Hardware or measurements anchored in place. Tiny Tapeout's silicon chips. OpenFlexure's field-deployed malaria microscopes in Dar es Salaam. Raspberry Shake's 2,000 citizen seismic stations. Safecast's 180-million-datapoint open radiation archive, including the only independent public dataset from the Chornobyl Exclusion Zone after the Russian withdrawal.
3. **Small-group traces.** Format specifications, curricula, mentorship chains. CTAN (34 years of TeX packages). Mathlib (formal mathematics as executable proof). Hōkūleʻa and the Polynesian Voyaging Society (wayfinding curriculum after a six-century gap). The American Scientific Glassblowers Society (70+ years of apprenticed craft knowledge).

## Relation to the Divergence Machine

Live zones are how meaningful cultural motion occurs in a [[low-reynolds-culture]]. Without them, the individual is left with two options: dissipation (participate in the attention economy, produce nothing) or exile (withdraw, produce nothing). Live zones are the third option: participate in an architecturally non-reciprocal structure, produce artifacts, accumulate across time and contributors.

## Dissipative Analogues

Not every community is a live zone. Specific failure patterns:

- "Second-brain" publishing that does not force external compilation, citation, or deposit.
- Community Discords and Slacks that archive but do not structure contributions.
- "Open" projects whose openness is licensing-only, without architectural requirements for durable deposit.
- Residencies and retreats that function as networking theatre rather than producing committed outputs.

The skepticism check is structural: **if the participants left tomorrow, would anyone inherit anything they couldn't inherit from Twitter?**
