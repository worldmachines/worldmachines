# Humboldtian AI

Humboldtian AI is a proposed architectural response to LLM homogenization, named for [[Alexander von Humboldt]]'s method of [[Synthesis by Composition]] — composing domain-specific observations side by side rather than compressing them into a single framework.

## Properties

- **Compositional** — modular representations that maintain domain-specific structure, with a composition layer that reveals relationships without flattening them
- **Cosmopolitan** — draws on all traditions but believes in communicability across them (unlike [[Herderian AI]]'s incommensurability)
- **Preserves the situated observer** — acknowledges that outputs are produced from particular training distributions and optimization objectives
- **Art and science continuous** — treats aesthetic, narrative, and affective dimensions of knowledge as data, not noise

## Technical Analogues

Yann LeCun's JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) is structurally Humboldtian: it maintains domain-specific encoders while learning a predictor that maps between representation spaces, revealing connections without collapsing domains. Retrieval-augmented generation, mixture-of-experts architectures, and ensemble methods also embody compositional principles.

## Failure Mode

The synthesizer becomes the new hegemon. Whoever designs the composition layer decides what "connection" means, which domains get represented, and how conflicts are resolved. Humboldt could hold the tensions because he was Humboldt — genius, cosmopolitan, genuinely curious. An algorithm that claims to compose fairly is just a more sophisticated form of the averaging it claims to escape.

## Held in Tension With

[[Herderian AI]] — the structurally pluralist alternative. Neither resolves the problem alone. See [[the-cosmos-and-the-model]].
