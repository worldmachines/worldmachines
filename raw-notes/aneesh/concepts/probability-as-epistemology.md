# Probability as Epistemology

## The Claim

Numerical probability is not a fact about the world — it is a *construction* from judgment and assumption. [[David Spiegelhalter]], writing in *Nature*, argues that outside a narrow set of cases, there is no "true" probability lurking behind estimates.

Source: [[Jan. 3, 2025]]

## Where Objective Probability Exists (Maybe)

The narrow exceptions:
- **Statistical mechanics**: gas molecules obeying statistical laws
- **Mendelian genetics**: chromosomal inheritance probabilities

In weather forecasting, economics, risk modeling, and most of science — probability expresses personal or collective *uncertainty*, not a fact about reality.

Linguistic implication: saying "the probability" implies something that usually doesn't exist. "A probability" would be more epistemically honest.

## de Finetti's Escape Hatch

**Bruno de Finetti's 1931 exchangeability theorem** provides the practical resolution:

- **Premise**: If you judge a sequence of events as *exchangeable* — meaning the order of observations doesn't change your beliefs about future ones — this is mathematically equivalent to acting as if driven by an objective underlying chance.
- **Upshot**: You don't need to resolve whether objective probability exists. Starting from a purely subjective expression of belief, you *can and should* act as if events were governed by real chances.

> "Probability is a fiction, but the right fiction to operate with."

## Philosophical Context

This positions probability within a broader tradition of **useful fictions** — concepts that are epistemically constructed but practically indispensable. Compare:
- Hume on causation: we don't observe causal necessity, only constant conjunction, yet act as if causes are real
- Kant's regulative ideals: concepts used as if true without asserting their truth
- Vaihinger's *philosophy of as-if*

## Implications for Data Science and Knowledge Work

For the [[Knowledge Worker]] operating on probabilistic models:
- Acknowledging that "a probability" rather than "the probability" is at stake changes the ethical and epistemic status of claims
- Model outputs are not discoveries but constructions — calibrated bets, not revealed facts
- This matters enormously for communicating uncertainty to non-experts (see [[AI Democratization Gap]])
