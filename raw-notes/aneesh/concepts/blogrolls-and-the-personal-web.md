# Blogrolls and the Personal Web

## Definition

A **blogroll** is a list of links to other websites maintained on one's own site, typically in a sidebar. [[Aneesh Sathe]] in [[The Small God of the Internet]] calls them "how villages point at one another and remember they are villages."

The blogroll is one of the "small rites" of the [[RSS as Protocol Theology|RSS god]] — a liturgical act that sustains the open web by materializing the connections between independent sites.

## The Village Metaphor

Sathe's formulation is precise: blogrolls are about *villages pointing at one another*. This is:
- **Mutual**: not one-directional recommendation but a fabric of reciprocal acknowledgment
- **Territorial**: villages have boundaries, identities, shared concerns
- **Memory**: villages "remember" they are villages — the act of linking is an act of recognition and continuity

Contrast with platform follows/subscriptions: on social media, "following" is private infrastructure owned by the platform. Blogrolls are public, portable, owned by the publisher.

## OPML as Pilgrim's List

OPML (Outline Processor Markup Language) is a format for sharing subscription lists — a machine-readable version of the blogroll that people "swap like seed packets." The garden metaphor is apt: OPML exchanges are acts of cultivation and sharing.

## The Personal Web

The personal web is the constellation of independently hosted sites, home pages, and blogs maintained outside platform walled gardens. In [[The Small God of the Internet]], the surviving congregation of RSS includes:
- "Home pages with kettles on"
- "Personal sites that remember how to wave"
- "Gardeners who publish their lists of other gardeners"

The repeated gardening metaphor suggests the personal web as a maintained, living space — in contrast to the manufactured, managed platform environment.
