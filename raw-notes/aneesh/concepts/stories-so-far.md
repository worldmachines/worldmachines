# Stories-So-Far

[[doreen-massey]]'s preferred minimum unit for the things that populate space. Anything — a mountain, a city, a person, an institution, a coffee plant, a scientific tradition — is best treated as a *trajectory in progress* rather than a fixed object or a complete history.

> "By stories here, I mean simply the history, change, movement, of things themselves. By stories-so-far, I mean that things are always still in process." (Massey 2005: 12)

## Why "story"?

Not in the narrative-construction sense (the wiki is alert to the over-use of "narrative" — see [[narrative-as-frame]] when written). Massey strips the term down to: that-which-changes, that-which-has-history. A mountain has a story (its geological emergence, its erosion, its meeting with this glacier). The story is not invented; it is the entity's processual character.

## Why "so far"?

The crucial qualifier. Each story is **mid-sentence**. There is no final chapter. The "snapshot" we take of an entity is a snapshot of something in motion, caught passing.

This blocks two errors:

1. **The fixed-object error**: treating an entity as if it had no history, was simply *there*. (The container view of space encourages this — things are deposited *into* space.)
2. **The completed-narrative error**: treating an entity's history as if it had ended. (Most narrative impositions on history make this error.)

## Why it grounds Massey's spatial ontology

If everything-in-space is a story-so-far, then space is the dimension of their **coexistence-while-still-going**. The spatial "now" is a [[dynamic-simultaneity]] of in-progress trajectories. This is the explicit content of [[simultaneity-of-stories-so-far]].

## Cognates in the wiki

- The "world machines" framework (Rao) treats civilizational arrangements as machines still operating — never finished, always producing more.
- Bryant's "operations" of machines: every entity does things, and what it does is its ongoing story.
- The wiki's broader move from artifacts to processes parallels Massey's stories-so-far framing.
