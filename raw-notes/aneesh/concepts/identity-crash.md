# Identity Crash

## Core Idea

When identity is "smeared across too many anxieties," the system cannot maintain coherence. The person declares **anxiety bankruptcy** and crashes identity into some safe spot: a journal, a sabbatical, quitting a job.

This is not failure — it is a maintenance action. Per [[The Plato Plateau]], life examination occurs at identity crashes. Philosophy provides a toehold in the abstract sufficient to spur action in the actual.

## The Rock Soup Analogy

The [[Rock Soup Parable]] applies here: vaporized anxiety (diffuse, unlocalized identity) needs a condensation surface. The condensation point should be:
- **Disposable** — not precious or commitment-heavy
- **Sufficient** — stable enough to allow identity to reconstruct an "ordered world of meaning" around it

This might be a journal, a structured project, or even a temporary philosophical commitment.

## Triggers

- Identity spread across too many competing anxieties
- [[Anxiety as Generative Force]] motor overwhelmed by noise
- Environmental crises or severe internal disruption

## Recovery

Recovery involves selecting a philosophy appropriate to the degree of identity loss (see [[Levels of Philosophical Engagement]]) and allowing the anxiety-exploration-action motor to restart from a more focused base.
