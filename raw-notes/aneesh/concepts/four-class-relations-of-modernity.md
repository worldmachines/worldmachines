# Four Class Relations of Modernity

## Framework

[[Ivo Velitchkov]]'s elaboration of [[venkatesh-rao]]'s [[Modernity Machine]] thesis identifies four key class relationships whose restructuring constitutes the machine's operation:

1. **Monarch ↔ Oligarchy**
2. **Oligarchy ↔ Technocracy**
3. **Technocracy ↔ Workers**
4. **Monarch ↔ Technocracy**

The general trend: **previously weaker parties gain persistent new agency**. Setbacks occur but the trend persists. The composition of each class also changes over time.

## Historical Transformations

### Monarch ↔ Oligarchy: Fugger's Transformation
[[Jakob Fugger]] converted this relationship from **divine right** to **contractual creditor-debtor**. The monarch, once sovereign by God's grace, became Fugger's debtor. Oligarchs were no longer merely favored subjects — they were creditors with contractual leverage.

### Oligarchy ↔ Technocracy: Fugger and Wedgwood
- **Fugger**: To manage his bank network, he needed accountants, auditors, and lawyers — proliferating new technocratic roles. Shift from military/theological knowledge to portable, codified knowledge.
- **Wedgwood**: Royal patronage ("Potter to Her Majesty") made the technocracy-oligarchy relationship legible through cascading desire: monarch → nobles → merchants → masses.

### Technocracy ↔ Workers: Wedgwood's Division of Labor
[[Josiah Wedgwood]] introduced the division of labor (later refined by Henry Ford). Previously, one potter made a pot start to finish. Now: specialized, replaceable workers. The shift was *not* toward increased worker agency — it was toward de-skilling and interchangeability. (Contrast with Voltaire's approach.)

Women and children entered the workforce for the first time as part of this era's economic expansion.

### Monarch ↔ Technocracy: Voltaire's Transformation
[[Voltaire]] transformed the intellectual's relationship to power from **subservient court pet** to **negotiated celebrity**. He bought financial independence (lottery, lending) so he couldn't be silenced or starved, while remaining embedded in elite networks. The intellectual became a free agent who could operate outside patronage while exploiting it.

Voltaire also became a *creditor* to the ruling class (like Fugger transformed oligarchs into creditors), completing a structural parallel.

## The State's Own Legibility Projects

The post-Westphalia nation-state simultaneously restructured these relations through its own legibility projects: surnames, standardized weights/measures, cadastres. The state was making its subjects legible to itself — while Voltaire was beginning the inverse: making the state legible to its subjects.

## What's Missing

- The four-class model is somewhat static — it doesn't fully capture the emergence of new classes (the bourgeoisie, the industrial proletariat) as distinct from the four existing ones
- The "technocracy" category is large enough to include court philosophers, accountants, lawyers, scientists, engineers, and writers — the internal differentiation within this class is underexplored
- The model is Eurocentric in its examples; the same class relations may have different dynamics elsewhere
