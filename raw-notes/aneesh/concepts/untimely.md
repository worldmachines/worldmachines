# Untimely

In [[bayo-akomolafe-the-untimely]], the **untimely** is that which cannot be resolved in time. It is not a new temporality, an alternative clock, a plan, or a future one can intentionally build. It is the excess generated by temporal discipline itself: the interval between tick and tock, the upbeat created by the downbeat, the fugitive surplus that appears when the clock tries to count bodies.

Akomolafe's compressed definition: "something to do that we know nothing about." The phrase matters: the untimely is active, but not programmatic; it enlists bodies, but does not present itself as a blueprint.

## Structure

- **Not standard time** — it refuses punctual, productive, linear, progress-oriented temporality.
- **Not alternative time** — it is not merely indigenous time, deep time, long time, or spiritual time if these become substitute clocks.
- **Excess of the clock** — the clock is incomplete; its discipline secretes what it cannot govern.
- **Embodied as failure** — lateness, disability, grief, autism, monsterhood, and awkwardness may be misnamed forms of untimely perception.
- **Political as parapolitics** — it does not seek recognition by power but sanctuary in cracks.

## Why It Matters

The concept gives a non-progressive account of transformation. Most political theories ask: what future should we build, and by what program? Akomolafe asks instead: what is already working through us in the failures of programmatic time?
