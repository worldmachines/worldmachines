# Lore vs. Search vs. LLM

## The Typology

Sathe (see [[My Grandfather Had Lore]]) frames three historical modes of accessing reliable information:

| Era | Mode | Reliability Mechanism |
|-----|------|-----------------------|
| Pre-modern | **Lore** | Social reputation, oral tradition, lived experience of the source |
| Early internet | **Search** | Hyperlinks to primary sources; user can follow citations |
| Current | **LLMs** | Fluent generation from latent training data; no citation trail |

The Dubai camel proverb structures the narrative: prosperity (information quality) follows a cycle that can return to its starting point.

## The Regression Argument

The argument is not that LLMs are worse than lore — it's that:
1. Google's search quality has demonstrably declined (advertising incentives, SEO gaming)
2. LLMs replace declining search as the default information tool
3. LLMs lack the citation/source-visibility that even degraded search retained
4. Result: mass information access becomes *less* reliable than it was under peak search

If the LLM era fails to deliver epistemic reliability, the fallback is formal education and expert knowledge — lore's institutional successor. This is a regression in *democratized* information access even if experts individually retain quality knowledge.

## The Structural Insight

Each mode has a different failure mode:
- **Lore**: gatekept by social access, geographic proximity, and the limits of human memory
- **Search**: gameable by SEO, degraded by advertising incentives, does not synthesize
- **LLM**: [[Hallucination]], no confidence scoring, no citation trail, confidently wrong

The ideal mode — and the one the [[Composite AI Architecture (Experts + LLM + KG)]] attempts to recover — would combine LLM's accessibility with lore's authority and search's traceability.
