# Deep Time Without Decentering

**Deep time without decentering** names [[bayo-akomolafe]]'s critique, in [[bayo-akomolafe-the-untimely]], of temporal scale as an insufficient cure for modernity. Long time and deep time seem to answer short-termism by stretching the horizon to ten thousand years, geological epochs, or planetary timescales. But Akomolafe argues that this may leave the human subject in the same seat of mastery.

The problem is not that deep time is false. The problem is that scale expansion can become another human achievement: *look at us, capable of calculating millions of years into the past and future.*

## Claim

Long/deep time can be preservationist and humbling, but it does not necessarily dislodge:

- anthropocentric centrality;
- calculative mastery;
- modernity's confidence that the problem is merely horizon-length;
- the fantasy that better time-consciousness will save the same subject.

## Productive Tension

This complicates this wiki's existing positive uses of deep time in [[deep-time-and-place-identity]] and [[the-lake-district-as-meeting-place]]. Massey and McPhee use geological time to destabilize place identity; Akomolafe warns that deep time can also become another technology of mastery if the calculating subject remains intact.
