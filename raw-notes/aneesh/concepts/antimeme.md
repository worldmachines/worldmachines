# Antimeme

An **antimeme** is an idea, structure, or actor with high real-world impact but low *memetic* spreadability. Unlike memes (ideas that propagate easily), antimemes resist attribution, memory, and transmission — they are powerful precisely because they cannot easily be pointed at.

## Origin of the Term

The term is recent (likely from SCP Foundation fiction, though independently rediscovered). Aneesh Sathe uses it in [[royalty-administration-and-antimemetics|Royalty, Administration, and Antimemetics]] to describe administrative bureaucracies in pre-modern empires.

## Structural Properties of Antimemetic Structures

1. **Diffused responsibility** — power distributed across many roles so no single actor is attributable
2. **Deniable authority** — formal titles obscure actual power ("merely recorded imperial decisions")
3. **Interpretive rather than legislative action** — shaping outcomes through application and interpretation rather than visible decree
4. **Low visibility by design** — the [[Mughal Administration]] matrix structure made decision loci opaque even to other officials

## Why Antimemetics Are Politically Useful

In pre-modern states, popular anger needed a target. Keeping administrators invisible ensured that discontent would be directed at the memetically visible ruler — who could be replaced — rather than at the administrative machinery, which was harder to dislodge. The [[Defenestration of Prague]] illustrates the mechanism: royal representatives (visible) were defenestrated for mismanagement by their administrators (invisible).

## Modern Instances

- **Civil service**: Sir Humphrey Appleby in *[[Yes, Minister]]* — "self-effacing discretion" masks enormous power
- **Corporate boards**: responsibility diffused between board, CEO, and management layers
- **Platform algorithms**: vast social impact with no attributable decision-maker

## Tension With Democratic Accountability

The antimemetic design of bureaucracy is efficient (administrators can work without fear of mob violence) but it is structurally resistant to democratic accountability. This is a deep tension in modern governance. See [[Bureaucratic Diffusion of Responsibility]].

## Contrast: Memetic Structures

Royalty, architecture (Alhambra, Taj Mahal, Forbidden City), and ceremony are aggressively memetic — designed to be seen, remembered, and attributed. They absorb accountability so that the machinery behind them can operate freely.
