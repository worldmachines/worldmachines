# Pull vs. Push Machines

## The Distinction

A key mechanical metaphor within the [[world-machines-framework]], introduced by [[venkatesh-rao]]:

- **Pull Machine**: Driven by shared attractor forces — a telos that draws all participants toward the same destination. Convergent because the *same small set of pull forces act on everybody*.
- **Push Machine**: Driven by individual or small-scale forces pushing from behind. Divergent because each push acts independently, sending things in random directions.

## The Analogy

Rao's driving metaphor: think of **front-wheel drive** (pull) vs. **rear-wheel drive with no steering** (push). The latter will go off in random directions. The divergence is structural, not incidental.

## Modernity Machine as Pull

The [[Modernity Machine]] was a pull machine, "pulled along by a *telos* of Progress™." All participants — regardless of geography or class — were oriented toward the same endpoints: legibility, rationalization, material abundance, scientific knowledge, nation-state formation. This is why MM history is *convergent*: the same playbook applies across contexts.

## Divergence Machine as Push

The [[divergence-machine]] is a push machine, driven by "individual or small-scale push forces." This is structurally *why* it diverges. Divergent history is a much stronger function of **grounding conditions** — local context, initial state, particular contingencies — than convergent history.

Implication: You cannot write a universal account of the DM era the way you can write a universal account of the MM era. History under the DM is inherently more local, more path-dependent, more sensitive to initial conditions.

## Epistemological Consequences

This distinction matters for how WM histories are written:
- MM histories can be written with universal categories (legibility, class restructuring, Progress)
- DM histories must track local grounding conditions; universal categories will miss the point

The [[Liveness Machine]] raises the question: what is the motor character of the third machine? Push and pull are both exogenous metaphors. Liveness suggests something more like *emergence* — self-organization from feedback — which is neither pushed nor pulled but recursively self-generating.

## What's Missing

Rao flags this as a "random thought I'm trying to chase down." The push/pull distinction is evocative but:
- No mechanism specified for *what* does the pushing in the DM
- The transition from pull to push is not explained — why does the DM emerge with a push character?
- The cybernetics/autopoiesis tradition (which Rao is "mildly hostile to") would have a ready answer in feedback loop dynamics
