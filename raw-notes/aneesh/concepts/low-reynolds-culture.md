# Low-Reynolds Culture

## The Analogy

The Reynolds number is the ratio of inertial to viscous force in a fluid. At high Reynolds number, inertia dominates; fluids are turbulent; swimmers coast after propulsion ceases. At low Reynolds number, viscosity dominates; fluids are laminar and reversible; swimmers stop the moment they stop pushing.

The [[modernity-machine]] behaved like a high-Reynolds cultural fluid. It generated large-scale inertia in a single direction — the [[argument-of-progress]] — and individual motion rode that inertia. An education, a profession, a career arc, a pension: each stage was subsidised by the momentum of the surrounding culture. Convergent intent was effectively free; divergent intent was expensive.

The [[divergence-machine]] is a low-Reynolds cultural fluid. The canonical direction has dispersed into a fan of available directions, none pulling. The infrastructure (institutions, credentialling systems, career paths) persists but no longer generates inertia. Individual motion now requires continuous local work. The [[scallop-theorem]] applies: reciprocal cultural moves produce no net displacement.

## Observable Properties

1. **No coasting.** A career that would have carried a worker twenty years ago no longer carries them. Apparent activity (meetings, credentials, engagement) does not equal apparent progress.
2. **Reciprocal moves fail.** Quiet quitting and lying flat are reciprocal withdrawals; the [[scallop-theorem]] predicts they produce zero net displacement, and empirically they do. The bath does not notice the withdrawal.
3. **Brownian bath.** The attention economy is not a directional headwind; it is the thermal medium in which every swimmer is steeped. Ignoring it is not resisting it. It is being diffused by it.
4. **Linear drag.** Slowing down offers no efficiency reward; the metabolic cost per unit direction is fixed. This is why "do less, more carefully" feels no easier than "do more, sloppily" — the fluid taxes motion at a flat rate.

## The Cost Inversion

Previously, execution was expensive and choice was cheap. Now, [[the-cost-of-intent-across-machines|execution is cheap and choice is expensive]]. The viscosity is in the production layer, not the execution layer: producing a genuine intention (a direction worth committing to) is now the metabolically expensive step. The low-Reynolds analogy explains why: without a canonical pull, the swimmer must produce all directional force locally, continuously, and without inertial amortisation.

## The Exit

The [[artifact-requirement]] is the cultural flagellum. Structures that force participants to leave a non-retractable deposit (a fabricated chip, a proof that compiles, a calibrated spectrum, a versioned package) are non-reciprocal by design. These are the [[live-zones]] of the Divergence Machine; the practice of moving through them is [[wayfinding-as-practice]].
