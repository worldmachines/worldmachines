# Rewilding Technology

## The Problem

Civilized forms of [[Liveness]] deaden themselves. Civilization, by definition, is about *never quite* exposing itself to the unbridled forces of wilderness. The systematic separation of liveness from death creates what Rao calls an "ersatz heaven":
- Death is concentrated at the periphery (slaughterhouses, hospices, foreign wars, internment camps)
- An anemic form of liveness is enshrined at the center
- The result: growing deadness at one end and "almost-aliveness" at the other

## The Prescription

To reanimate civilization is to rewild it. This requires two moves:

**At the death end**: Surrender corpses to recycling forces — scavenging, cannibalization, decomposition. Don't maintain artificial life support for dying institutions, technologies, and paradigms.

**At the birth end**: Remove protections from the barely living. Expose newly born entities to the full force of uncertainty and risk. Incubator conditions cannot be permanent.

## What Rewilding Is Not

Popular imagination pictures rewilding as the solarpunk image: robots in meadows, harmonious human-machine-nature coexistence. This is wrong. Real rewilding looks like a mussel-covered pier piling:
- Dense, illegible, competitive
- Murderous competitiveness lurking beneath apparent routine
- No legible aesthetic of "fitness" — no Instagram ideals, no BattleBots heroics

## The Gramsci Gap Context

Rewilding is especially necessary in a [[Gramsci Gap]]. When the old world is dying and the new is struggling to be born, the normal instinct is to either mourn the old or protect the new. Rewilding says: let both processes happen without management, at the cost of greater discomfort and greater liveness.

## Werner Herzog's Witness

[[Werner Herzog]]'s jungle monologue (from *Burden of Dreams*) provides the aesthetic:

> "Taking a close look at what is around us, there is some sort of harmony. It is the harmony of overwhelming and collective murder."

The jungle is not peaceful; it is vicious. Civilization's comfort zones are achieved by making that viciousness invisible. Rewilding makes it visible again — and accepts it.

## Technological Application

What does rewilding look like in [[New Nature]]?
- Allowing technological "species" to compete without regulatory protection (death at the periphery)
- Removing artificial supports for incumbent platforms (surrender corpses)
- Accepting that new protocols and standards will fight viciously — in boring, legibility-free ways
- Not aestheticizing the competitive process (resisting the "Bloodsport Planet" aesthetics)
