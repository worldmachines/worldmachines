# Space and Place in Cognition

## Origin

The framework adapts humanistic geography — principally [[Yi-Fu Tuan]]'s *Space and Place: The Perspective of Experience* (1977) — into a theory of cognition and inquiry.

**Epigraph (Tuan)**:
> "A farmer has to cut down trees to create space for his farmstead and fields. Yet once the farm is established it becomes an ordered world of meaning—a place—and beyond it is the forest and space."

## The Core Distinction

| | **Space** | **Place** |
|---|---|---|
| Character | Undifferentiated, pure possibility | Ordered, meaningful, navigable |
| Experience | Disorienting without markers | Provides standpoint and identity |
| Risk | Paralysis; impossible to act | Constraint; forecloses alternatives |
| Cognitive analog | Unexamined question | Stabilized belief / knowledge |

## Place-Making as Cognitive Act

A place comes into being the moment we *interrupt* undifferentiated space. The act of interruption:
- Imposes a way of looking, being, and acting.
- Defines the identity inhabited during a quest.
- Creates a home base for further exploration.

**Thinking = place-making**: converting ungraspable into knowledge-anchored.

## Fractal Structure of Place

Places are nested. Identity is the outermost place; closer inspection reveals it dissolves into smaller places the way a city dissolves into neighbourhoods. See: [[The Mind as Semi-Solid Smoke]].

## Tools as Micro-Places

Every tool is a micro-place. Tools:
- Frame what can be thought.
- Foreclose alternative moves.
- Commit the user to a worldview.

The search bar is open space; Netflix's suggestions are a curated place. See: [[Tools and Worldview Commitment]], [[Algorithmic Curation as Place-Making]].

## Socratic Dialogue as Cartography

Socratic dialogue interrupts space and makes concrete what was nebulous — *interrogation as cartography*. Each exchange plants a map point in the question-space. See: [[Socratic Thinking]].

## Applied Inward: The Mind

Internal cognitive landmarks ("insta-places") are conjured from experience and treated as solid — even when they are not. This is [[Normative Self-Blindness]]. The mind is a collection of semi-solid smoke landmarks held together by the functional necessity of acting. See: [[The Mind as Semi-Solid Smoke]].

## Applied Outward: Social Media

Orientation is a biological need: organisms need accurate mental maps to survive. Social platforms create noisy signals that gesture toward places but never deliver genuine orientation — leaving users lost and hungry. See: [[Information Hunger and Orientation]], [[Signal-to-Orientation Ladder]].
