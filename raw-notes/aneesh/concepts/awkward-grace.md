# Awkward Grace

In the Q&A after [[bayo-akomolafe-the-untimely]], [[bayo-akomolafe]] reframes grace as awkward rather than amazing. Grace is not smooth rescue or beautiful arrival. It is monstrous interruption: the thing that appears at the door and disrupts the order that thought it wanted salvation.

His central example comes from Dostoevsky's Grand Inquisitor: Christ returns, but the church imprisons him because his arrival disrupts the church's business. Grace appears as the thing the institution cannot welcome without being undone.

## Monster as Reworker

Akomolafe uses "monster" not as Disney villainy but in a folkloric sense: the monster reworks the middle. It disturbs the binary and reconfigures bodies, times, and relations. Grace is monstrous because it changes the conditions under which grace could be recognized.

## Relation to the Untimely

Awkward grace is a theological-affective version of [[untimely]]. It arrives off-schedule, cannot be forced by attention, and breaks the terms of recognition. Like [[idowu-function]], it is a third that does not resolve the binary but alters its field.
