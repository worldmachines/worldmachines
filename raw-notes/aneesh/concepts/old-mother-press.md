# Old Mother Press

## The Figure

Coined in [[The Small God of the Internet]] as part of the mythological genealogy of RSS:

> "Long before he knocked on doors with a bundle of headlines, there was Old Mother Press, the iron-fingered goddess of moveable type, patron of ink that bites and paper that complains. Her creed was simple: get the word out. She marched letters into columns and columns into broadsides until villages woke up arguing the same argument."

## Genealogical Function

Old Mother Press establishes that RSS (ArrEsEs) is not a modern invention but an inheritor of a deep tradition of information dissemination:

- **Old Mother Press** (printing press, ~1450–present) → columns, broadsides, the synchronization of public argument
- **ArrEsEs** (RSS, ~1999–present) → the broadsheet delivered to each door at once; text in tidy rows, dates that mind their place

The family resemblance: both technologies are about *getting the word out* without an intermediary who filters or distorts. Both trust the reader to encounter the full document. Both are pull-based (you go to the printer's shop; you subscribe to the feed).

## The Key Inheritance

> "[ArrEsEs] still bears her family look: text in tidy rows, dates that mind their place, headlines that know how to stand up straight."

This is also an implicit contrast with platform feeds: algorithmic ranking violates the "family look" — dates don't mind their place, headlines are optimized for clicks rather than standing up straight.

## Implications for Media History

Old Mother Press's "creed was simple: get the word out" — she "marched letters into columns and columns into broadsides until villages woke up arguing the same argument." This is both a celebration and an implicit critique: the printing press created *synchronized public discourse* (villages arguing the same argument), which is both the precondition for democracy and for panic, propaganda, and moral panic.

RSS inherits the synchronization property (everyone's feed updates on the same cadence) but distributes it: you subscribe to what you choose, not what the broadside vendor distributes.
