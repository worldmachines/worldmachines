# Tools and Worldview Commitment

## The Claim

> "Picking a tool commits us to a world view."

Every tool is a **micro-place**: it interrupts undifferentiated possibility-space and imposes a particular way of looking, acting, and thinking. By choosing a tool, you:
- Frame what can be thought within that tool's affordances.
- Foreclose moves that the tool does not support.
- Adopt an implicit ontology about the problem-space.

See: [[Space and Place in Cognition]]

## Illustrative Examples

**Netflix**: The search bar is open space (pure possibility). Netflix's suggestion algorithm creates a *place* shaped by watch history — it narrows possibility while appearing to offer choice. The suggested space is not the full space; it is one curated path through it.

**Language**: Natural language itself is a tool that commits users to particular categories, distinctions, and affordances. Choosing English vs. German vs. a formalism (mathematics, code) is a worldview commitment.

**Scientific method**: Choosing the experimental method as a tool commits you to a universe that is lawful, observable, and repeatable. Other stances (mystical, interpretive) are foreclosed.

## Relation to the Printing Press

When the printing press created information overload, the tool of *organized knowledge comparison* (bibliography, citations, criticism) emerged as a corrective micro-place. Choosing that tool over mystical synthesis (cf. [[Giordano Bruno]]) was a worldview commitment that enabled the scientific revolution.

See: [[Printing Press and Information Overload]]

## Relation to AI

LLMs as a tool commit users to a particular mode of knowledge-engagement: conversational, probabilistic, associative. The tool forecloses certain forms of rigor (precise citation, formal proof) while opening others (exploratory dialogue, analogical reasoning).

The Socratic use of LLMs — treating the session as inquiry rather than answer-extraction — is itself a choice of how to wield the tool, and thus a worldview commitment.

See: [[LLMs as Epistemic Tools]]

## Broader Resonances

This claim resonates with:
- **McLuhan's "the medium is the message"**: the tool shapes the content of thought.
- **Heidegger on equipment** (*Zeug*): tools shape the world they disclose.
- **Kuhn on paradigms**: the tools of a scientific community determine what questions can be asked.
