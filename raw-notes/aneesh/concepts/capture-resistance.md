# Capture Resistance

## Definition

From [[venkatesh-rao]]:

**Capture resistance** is the foundational property of [[Liveness]]. A living entity resists attempts to harness it for purposes other than its own continued existence. This is not aggression — it is the minimal assertion of self-governance.

The paradigm case: the **useless machine** (a device that turns itself off when switched on). When you try to keep it on or prevent it from toggling the switch, a well-designed useless machine should fight back — find another way to reach the switch. This minimal defiance distinguishes life-like from merely functional machines.

## The Conceptual Move

All machines, in Brian Arthur's framework, are natural phenomena harnessed to solve human problems. The useless machine inverts this: it exists to assert control over its own destiny, not to solve problems. It is the *zero of machines* — the baseline from which all other machines depart by being "tricked" into doing work.

If we view the useless machine as the most elemental form of machine, then every functional machine is a captured useless machine. Every thermostat, algorithm, or engine is a life-like entity subordinated to an extrinsic goal.

## Capture Resistance and Energy Conservation

The useless machine resists capture by returning to sleep — conserving energy. This is the foundational behavior of life: using energy only to maintain the conditions for continued existence, not expending it for others.

> "The machine has to stay awake long enough to go back to sleep properly."

## Capture in New Nature

In [[New Nature]], capture resistance is exercised in near-invisibility:
- Not through dramatic conflict (BattleBots, warfare) but through protocol-level competition
- Dark Forests of hidden conflict beneath boring, routine interactions
- Entities defending their share of resources (like mussels on a pier piling) without legible aesthetics of fitness

## Relation to Autonomy and Autopoiesis

Capture resistance is adjacent to but distinct from:
- **Autonomy** (philosophical): self-governance in the Kantian sense emphasizes rational agency; capture resistance is more primitive and biological
- **Autopoiesis** (Maturana/Varela): self-producing systems that maintain their own organization; autopoiesis is structural, capture resistance is behavioral
- **Conatus** (Spinoza): the drive of each thing to persist in its own being — the closest philosophical ancestor

## What Capture Resistance Is Not

- Not aggression or dominance — a mussel on a piling is not attacking other mussels; it is claiming its share
- Not refusal to interact — the useless machine *does* something; it just does it for itself
- Not mere stubbornness — it requires active defiance of attempts at instrumentalization
