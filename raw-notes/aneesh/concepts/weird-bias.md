# WEIRD Bias

## Origin of the Term

WEIRD (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic) was coined by Henrich, Heine & Norenzayan (2010) in their influential paper "The Weirdest People in the World?" — which demonstrated that psychological research overwhelmingly samples from WEIRD populations while treating its findings as universal.

Their finding: WEIRD populations are outliers on many psychological dimensions (visual perception, moral reasoning, fairness intuitions, sense of self) rather than representatives of human cognition generally.

## Extension to AI

USC researchers (Dehghani, Sourati, 2026) apply the WEIRD bias critique to LLM training data. Because LLMs are trained primarily on internet text — which massively overrepresents English and other European languages, Western cultural contexts, and dominant ideological frameworks — their outputs:

- Mirror the "language, values and reasoning styles of Western, educated, industrialized, rich and democratic societies"
- Treat WEIRD reasoning patterns as the default standard for "credible speech, correct perspective, or even good reasoning"
- Actively suppress or marginalise non-WEIRD cognitive styles when users interact with them

## The Universalism Trap

WEIRD bias is a structural enactment of the [[Monism vs. Pluralism|monist]] assumption that there is one correct way to reason and express ideas. When this assumption is embedded in models used by billions, it becomes self-reinforcing: as more people use WEIRD-biased models, WEIRD-inflected expression becomes normatively dominant — even in populations the training data did not include.

## Connections to the Counter-Enlightenment

[[Johann Gottfried Herder]] argued that each culture embodies a distinct and valuable form of human excellence, and that the imposition of any single cultural standard — however well-intentioned — destroys irreplaceable human goods. WEIRD bias in AI is a technological enactment of exactly this kind of cultural imposition. See [[Counter-Enlightenment]].

## Proposed Remedies

- Deliberate diversification of LLM training data to reflect actual global human diversity
- Separate models or model components trained on non-WEIRD corpora
- User-controlled adjustments to model outputs based on cultural context
- Research programs that measure and document cognitive diversity before it is lost to homogenization
