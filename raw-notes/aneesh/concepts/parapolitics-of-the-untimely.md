# Parapolitics of the Untimely

The **parapolitics of the untimely** is [[bayo-akomolafe]]'s name for a political orientation that does not seek politics-as-usual: representation, recognition, visibility, justice within the existing accommodation. It works beside or in excess of politics, in the cracks where the world is already incomplete.

In [[bayo-akomolafe-the-untimely]], this includes gathering, cooking, reading, grieving, experimenting, and staying with the trouble of genocide, climate change, pandemics, fascism, and loss without turning the response into a blueprint.

## Core Claims

- **Justice is not enough** — not because justice is bad, but because modernity can absorb justice claims into legibility and recognition.
- **Sanctuary is not a project** — it is not something built on Tuesday and Thursday by plan; it is what the world conspires to create through cracks in bodies and institutions.
- **Decolonization is incompleteness** — not a new completeness replacing colonial order, but the discovery that the colonial order was never complete.
- **Failure can be connective** — people meet through failed punctuality, impossible grief, disability, and other ungovernable rhythms.

## Contrast

Parapolitics is not quietism. It is active, but its activity is not the activity of sovereign subjects implementing a plan. It is closer to being enlisted by [[untimely]] forces already moving through the situation.
