# Simultaneity of Multiple Trajectories

A signature Massey phrase used throughout *For Space* (2005) but consolidated in Chapter 5. Where [[dynamic-simultaneity]] names the **slice** (the spatial "now"), this concept names the **elements** of that slice: trajectories, each carrying its own temporality, each a "story so far."

> "[Space is] the sphere of coexisting multiplicity . . . space as a simultaneity of stories-so-far." (Massey 2005: 54; cf. *For Space* preface, p. 9: "the sphere of the possibility of the existence of multiplicity in the sense of contemporaneous plurality")

## The "story so far" framing

Every entity — a mountain, a city, a person, a coffee plant — is *in process*. Treating it as a fixed object misses what it actually is. Treating it as a snapshot misses that the snapshot is of a moving thing. The right minimum unit is the **trajectory**: where this entity has come from, where it is going, what it has met.

Place, on this account, is not the durable identity of a location but the **constellation of trajectories momentarily articulated** here. (See [[places-as-events]] in Part Four.)

## Why it defeats the closures of Part Two

1. **Against [[space-as-representation-critique]]:** a trajectory cannot be "captured" by representation; representation is itself a trajectory.
2. **Against [[prison-house-of-synchrony]]:** trajectories are open; a snapshot of their meeting is not a closed system.
3. **Against [[horizontalities-of-deconstruction]]:** trajectories are multi-temporal; the world is not a flat text awaiting deconstruction.

## Why it grounds politics

If the spatial "now" is a simultaneity of multiple trajectories, then it is also the site where the unchosen co-presence of differently-going stories must be **negotiated**. There is no rule given in advance; there is no universal that says how one trajectory should accommodate another. This is the bridge to Part Five — see [[throwntogetherness]] (when written) and the "politics of the event of place."
