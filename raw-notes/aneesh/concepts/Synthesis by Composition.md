# Synthesis by Composition

Synthesis by composition is the method of combining knowledge from multiple domains by placing their distinct representations *side by side* — preserving each domain's native structure and letting connections emerge through juxtaposition. It is contrasted with **synthesis by compression**, which passes all domains through a single framework (statistical, theoretical, or algorithmic) that reduces them to a common unit.

## The Paradigm Case: Humboldt's Naturgemälde

[[Alexander von Humboldt]]'s *Naturgemälde* of Mount Chimborazo composed botanical, geological, atmospheric, and climatic data on a single enormous sheet. Each domain retained its own measurement system. Nothing was averaged. The viewer's eye moved between domains, and connections became visible through spatial proximity rather than theoretical reduction.

## The Contrast: LLM Embedding Space

Large language models achieve synthesis by compression: all knowledge is encoded into a uniform vector space, and outputs are generated by predicting the most probable next token. Domain-specific structure is dissolved into parameters. The connections the model finds are statistical correlations, not composed juxtapositions.

| | Composition | Compression |
|---|---|---|
| Domains | Preserved in native form | Dissolved into common representation |
| Connections | Emerge from juxtaposition | Computed from statistical correlation |
| Observer | Active — brings their own eyes | Passive — receives averaged output |
| Information loss | Minimal — each domain speaks | Significant — variation compressed toward mean |
| Architectural analogy | [[Humboldtian AI]], JEPA | Autoregressive LLMs |

## In Spatial Epistemology

In the vocabulary of [[spatial-epistemology-the-master-thread]]: composition creates *places* (bounded, inhabitable, the observer can look around). Compression creates *space* (undifferentiated, navigable only by the system itself). See ["The Shelter as Epistemic Engine"](https://aneeshsathe.com/the-shelter-as-epistemic-engine/).
