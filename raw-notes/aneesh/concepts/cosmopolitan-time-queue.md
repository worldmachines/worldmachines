# Cosmopolitan Time Queue

The figure at the heart of Part Three of [[doreen-massey]]'s *For Space* (2005). When the operation of [[spatialising-modernity]] is run, the world's geography is converted into a single line — a queue — along which societies are ranked: Western Europe at the front; "developing" countries strung out behind; "backward" places at the back.

> "Co-existing heterogeneity is rendered as (reduced to) place in the historical queue." (Massey 2005: 68–69)

The queue's effects:

1. **Difference becomes lag.** Mozambique and the UK are not two contemporaries with their own trajectories; they are two points on one line. "Africa" is not different from the West — it is *behind* the West.
2. **The future is foreclosed.** Whatever is "ahead" is the future of those "behind." There is no other future on offer.
3. **Politics is futile.** Resistance is irrational — you can't push the line backward, you can only delay your own arrival.
4. **Other temporalities are repressed.** All temporalities must align to the one queue; none can have their own duration, their own direction.

## Who runs the queue

Massey's polemic in Chapters 6 and 8 is that the queue is **politically produced**, not naturally given. Specific institutions (the IMF, the World Bank, structural adjustment programmes, the discourse of "development," the West's universities) operate the queue and enforce its order. See [[aspatial-globalisation]], [[globalisation-as-inevitability-rhetoric]].

The queue is also a *Western* artefact. The conceptual move — single timeline, ranked positions — emerged from a specific intellectual and colonial history. See [[johannes-fabian]] on anthropology's role.

## How to undo it

[[coevalness]] — the recognition that the world's societies are radical contemporaries, each with their own trajectories, none assignable to a "stage." This is not relativism (Massey is explicit that the concept of "progress" and "development" can still be used) but it is anti-monism: no single line, no single direction, no single endpoint.

> "What are opposed . . . are not the same societies at different stages of development, but different societies facing each other at the same Time." (Fabian 1983: 155, quoted Massey 2005: 69)

## Cognates

- Rao's "Modernity Machine" / "History Machine" — the queue is the machine's signature output. See [[cosmopolitan-time-queue-and-world-machines]].
