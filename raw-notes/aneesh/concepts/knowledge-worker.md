# Knowledge Worker

## Definition

A knowledge worker is someone whose primary work is cognitive — producing analysis, synthesis, judgment, code, writing, or other intellectual outputs rather than physical goods. The term originates with Peter Drucker.

The concept surfaces across several sources as the figure most affected by the conditions of intellectual infrastructure.

## Sources in this Wiki

- [[Jan. 7, 2025: Building Dwelling Thinking]]: "In the life of today's knowledge worker thinking is everything." Knowledge workers must bring together building and thinking to dwell — their products are places where others dwell.
- [[Jan. 3, 2025]]: The [[AI Democratization Gap]] hits knowledge workers asymmetrically — sophisticated knowledge workers extract more from AI tools than their less-expert counterparts.

## The Dwelling Requirement

[[Martin Heidegger]]'s framework (see [[Dwelling as Philosophical Concept]]) applies with particular force to knowledge workers. Their work *is* dwelling — it requires cognitive presence, orientation in a problem space, and the capacity to think and build simultaneously.

Products and tools that fragment attention or demand conformity to their logic are particularly hostile to knowledge workers, whose core competency depends on the quality of their cognitive environment.

## Conditions of Access

[[Virginia Woolf]] and the [[Studiolo and Self-Cultivation]] discussion raise the structural dimension: intellectual private space — the condition for sustained knowledge work — is not equally distributed. The knowledge worker figure can mask the inequality of who has the conditions to do this work.
