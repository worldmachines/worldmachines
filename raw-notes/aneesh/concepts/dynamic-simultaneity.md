# Dynamic Simultaneity

Massey's replacement for the structuralist **synchrony**. A spatial "now" is not the instantaneous cross-section of a closed system (the Hegelian "essential section" or the structuralist langue-slice); it is the *coexistence* of many trajectories — each one a "story so far" with its own internal temporality — articulated at this moment in this particular constellation.

> "The argument started from the position that space is a discrete multiplicity, but one in which the elements of that multiplicity are themselves imbued with temporality. A static contemporaneity was rejected in favour of a dynamic simultaneity." (Massey 2005: 55)

## Why "dynamic" matters

Static simultaneity would be the snapshot — every element frozen in place at one tick. Dynamic simultaneity is the recognition that each element in the constellation is **itself ongoing**, mid-process, mid-trajectory. The "snapshot" abstraction is real (we can talk about a moment) but the elements it captures are not frozen objects — they are processes-caught-passing.

This is what defeats the [[prison-house-of-synchrony]]: a dynamic simultaneity cannot be causally closed, because its constituents are open processes still in motion.

## Why "simultaneity" matters

Not "sequence." The structuralist temptation is to treat any apparent coexistence as really a sequence of synchronic states, with diachrony as the bridge between snapshots. Dynamic simultaneity refuses this: the trajectories really *are* concurrent. They affect each other in the present, not by transmitting along a single time-line but by happening together.

This is also what defeats the [[aspatial-globalisation]] move (see Part Three): refusing to recognise simultaneity means converting coexistence into a developmental queue — different places become different "times" — see [[cosmopolitan-time-queue]] and [[spatialising-modernity]].

## Relation to Bergson

Bergson's *duration* is preserved, not rejected. Each trajectory has its own duration. What Massey adds is the **co-implication** of those durations — they are not parallel monads, they meet. The spatial dimension is what allows that meeting. Cf. [[the-co-implication-of-space-and-time]].
